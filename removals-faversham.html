Removal services in Faversham — everything you need to know

Faversham is one of Kent's most attractive and sought-after market towns — a historic settlement on the creek that bears its name, with a beautifully preserved medieval town centre, an exceptional independent food, drink and retail scene, and a strong community character that draws buyers and renters from London and from across the South East who value its authenticity and quality of life.

The town is positioned between Sittingbourne to the west and Canterbury to the east on the A2 corridor, with a direct rail service to London Victoria and Charing Cross making it accessible for commuters. Faversham has seen growing inward migration in recent years as more people discover the quality of life it offers, and this has created an increasingly active and competitive housing market.

We carry out regular removals throughout Faversham and the surrounding Swale district villages, covering the town itself including the historic town centre, the residential areas of Davington, Preston Street, Tanners Street and the newer developments on the town's outskirts, as well as the surrounding villages of Boughton-under-Blean, Selling, Throwley, Oare, Hernhill, Ospringe and the Swale marshland communities including Graveney, Seasalter and Goodnestone.

Our local knowledge of Faversham

Faversham's historic town centre presents some of the most distinctive removal challenges in Kent. The medieval street pattern, with its irregular layout, narrow lanes and historic buildings that crowd close to the carriageway on streets like Court Street, Preston Street and the Abbey Street area, requires careful assessment for each removal address. Some of Faversham's most sought-after properties are in streets that can only accommodate a transit-sized vehicle rather than a full Luton or 7.5-tonne lorry, which affects planning for larger moves.

We always carry out a route assessment for Faversham historic core addresses before arriving on moving day, confirming the maximum vehicle size that can access the specific street and planning the loading position to minimise obstruction to other road users. For moves from properties in the most restricted parts of the town centre, we may plan a shuttle operation using a smaller vehicle to transfer items to a larger lorry parked at a suitable distance.

The Faversham Creek and the surrounding Oare and Nagden areas have their own distinctive access challenges, with narrow approach roads and some properties accessed via unmade tracks. The creek-side properties are particularly sought after and we carry out a number of moves from this area each year, always assessing the access situation carefully in advance.

The rural villages around Faversham — Selling, Throwley, Sheldwich, Boughton Monchelsea, Painter's Forstal and Hernhill — are typical of the North Downs and Chart Hills landscape: scattered properties accessed via narrow lanes, limited turning space and the characteristic challenges of rural Kent removal work that our team handles with experience and care.

Did you know? Your home contents insurance almost certainly does not cover your belongings during a removal. Every move we carry out is protected by comprehensive goods-in-transit insurance covering full replacement value. We carry proof of cover on every job and provide it on request before you commit to a booking.
