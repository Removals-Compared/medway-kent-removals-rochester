Our local knowledge of Deal

Deal has a distinctive historic character: the Conservation Area along the seafront with Georgian and early-Victorian terraces, the Tudor castle and the harbour-side area, and the modern residential areas inland in Mongeham, Sholden and Upper Deal. The road network is shaped by the A258 from Dover, the A256 from Sandwich and a tight central network of historic streets. For removal lorries the main planning consideration is the Conservation Area, where listed properties, narrow streets and parking restrictions need careful planning.

Deal housing is heavily varied. The Conservation Area along Beach Street, Middle Street and the streets immediately behind the seafront has substantial Georgian terraces, often three or four storeys, with original features, sash windows, narrow stair turns and limited driveway access. Walmer to the south has a mix of Victorian, Edwardian and modern housing. Mongeham, Sholden and Upper Deal inland have post-war and modern family housing with easier access. Each area needs different planning.

Parking restrictions are heavy across the Conservation Area, the seafront and the central streets near the castle. Permit zones, time-limited restrictions and pay-and-display cover most of the central streets, with additional conservation-area considerations around listed buildings. We routinely apply for a parking suspension from Dover District Council 7 to 10 working days in advance for any central Deal move. The inland modern areas have far more straightforward parking.

Deal sees a particularly active retirement and lifestyle-relocation market, Londoners and people from inland Kent moving to or buying a Conservation Area property. We handle a steady volume of these alongside the standard local relocations and outbound jobs. Most are fixed-price quotes with extended journey insurance included.

On every Deal move our team confirms the vehicle size, parking plan and any council suspension requirement in advance. For modern family homes in Mongeham, Sholden, Upper Deal and the inland estates we routinely use a 7.5 tonne lorry; for Conservation Area Georgian terraces, Walmer Victorian properties and the central streets we plan with a Luton-bodied vehicle. Conservation-area listed properties get particular attention to protection and careful manual handling.

Deal moves regularly link with the wider East Kent coast, for the immediately adjacent town we coordinate directly with our Dover removals service, and for jobs north along the Thanet coast we link with our Ramsgate removals service coverage.

Did you know? Your home contents insurance almost certainly does not cover your belongings during a removal. Every move we carry out is protected by comprehensive goods-in-transit insurance covering full replacement value. We carry proof of cover on every job and provide it on request before you commit to a booking.

How we price your Deal removal

Every Deal quote we write is built from the actual specifics of your move, not a one-size-fits-all rate card. The factors that genuinely affect the price are the volume of your contents, the property type and access at both ends, the distance involved, the date you want to move, and any extras such as packing, furniture dismantling and reassembly, or storage. For 3-bedroom homes and larger we offer a free pre-move survey, in person or via a video walkthrough, so the quote is grounded in what is actually being moved rather than a guess from a postcode and a property size.

Once you accept the quote, the figure is fixed. We do not add charges on the day for stairs, parking waits, heavier items, additional time, fuel, or anything else. The price you sign off on is the price you pay, and any underestimate on our part is our cost to absorb, not yours. For Deal moves we factor in the M2-A2-A258 corridor travel time and the central Conservation Area approach, and the journey from our Rochester depot takes 60 to 70 minutes outside peak rush.

Because we operate our own crew and our own vehicles rather than subcontracting work out, our pricing stays consistently competitive against the larger national operators serving Kent, without compromising on care or on accountability for your belongings.
