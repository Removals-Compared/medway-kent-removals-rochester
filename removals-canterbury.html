Removal services in Canterbury — everything you need to know

Canterbury is one of the most significant cities in Kent and in the country as a whole — a UNESCO World Heritage Site, a major commercial and cultural centre, a university city with three institutions drawing students from across the UK and internationally, and a highly sought-after residential destination for families, professionals and retirees drawn by the city's exceptional quality of life, excellent schools and improving transport connections.

We carry out regular removals throughout Canterbury and across the full Canterbury district, serving all city neighbourhoods and suburbs including the area within the ancient city walls, Wincheap, Thanington, Rough Common, Chartham, Harbledown, Sturry, Fordwich, Littlebourne, Bridge, Bekesbourne, Blean, Whitstable, Herne Bay, Tankerton, Seasalter, Whitstable, Hernhill and all surrounding villages.

Canterbury has an unusually varied removal market driven by three distinct customer groups. The first is the permanent residential market — families and professionals buying and selling homes in and around the city. The second is the large student population of the three Canterbury universities — the University of Kent, Canterbury Christ Church University and the University of the Creative Arts — who generate a substantial volume of student removal demand at the start and end of each academic year. The third is the city's growing commercial sector, which generates significant office relocation demand.

Our local knowledge of Canterbury

Canterbury's historic core, contained within the ancient Roman and medieval city walls, presents some of the most challenging removal logistics of any location we serve across Kent. The medieval street pattern was not designed for motor vehicles, and the narrow lanes, low archways, restricted access zones and pedestrianisation schemes that characterise this part of Canterbury require considerable advance planning for removal vehicles.

Many of the streets within and immediately adjacent to the city walls are restricted to certain vehicle types and sizes, and the city's extensive pedestrian zones mean that some properties can only be accessed by removal vehicle during specific hours, typically early morning before the pedestrian restrictions come into force. We research every Canterbury city centre removal address individually and plan the access strategy before we arrive on moving day.

The student accommodation market in Canterbury generates its own specific removal challenges. The large student halls of residence operated by the three universities — including the extensive University of Kent campus on the hill above the city and the various Canterbury Christ Church campus buildings — have their own rules about moving vehicle access, lift usage and communal corridor protection. We have experience moving students in and out of all the main Canterbury university accommodation types and know the specific procedures for each.

The outer suburbs and villages of the Canterbury district offer very different and generally more straightforward removal logistics. Whitstable and Herne Bay, on the North Kent coast, are popular relocation destinations with a mix of Victorian seafront properties, inter-war bungalows and newer developments. Access is generally good throughout both towns, with the occasional challenge on the narrower seafront and old town streets.

Did you know? Your home contents insurance almost certainly does not cover your belongings during a removal. Every move we carry out is protected by comprehensive goods-in-transit insurance covering full replacement value. We carry proof of cover on every job and provide it on request before you commit to a booking.
