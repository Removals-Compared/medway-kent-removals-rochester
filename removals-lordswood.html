Our local knowledge of Lordswood

Lordswood is a planned post-war community, largely developed from the 1960s onwards on the high ground south of Chatham. The road layout reflects that planned origin: wide spine roads (North Dane Way, Lordswood Lane), generous estate roads and a logical hierarchy of cul-de-sacs and closes off the main routes. For a removal team this is an excellent layout to work in, turning circles are predictable, parking is rarely a problem, and visibility for larger vehicles is generally clear.

The Lordswood housing stock is overwhelmingly modern: semi-detached and detached family houses, a smaller proportion of bungalows on the older estates, and a good range of executive 4 and 5-bedroom properties on the more recent developments. Most homes have a driveway and a garage, which means our 7.5 tonne lorry can usually park directly outside the property without obstructing the street. The local Lordswood shopping centre, the schools and Lordswood Industrial Estate sit at the centre of the community and shape much of the local traffic flow.

Parking restrictions are minimal across Lordswood, there are no permit zones, no pay-and-display areas, and the only meaningful access constraints come on a small number of older closes with tight entrance widths. We confirm the access situation the day before every move and, where needed, plan with a Luton-bodied vehicle rather than a full lorry. We never charge customers extra for that decision.

Lordswood draws a steady flow of inbound relocations from the M2 corridor, from London commuters using the A229 down to Junction 3, and from local moves within Medway. We carry out a corresponding range of outbound long-distance jobs each year, all on fixed-price quotes with extended journey insurance included as standard.

For Lordswood moves we routinely use our 7.5 tonne lorry on the main estate roads where parking and turning is straightforward. On the older closes around the original 1960s development we plan with a Luton-bodied vehicle to keep access tight without needing to shuttle. Every quote includes confirmation of the vehicle size and parking plan before the day, and we never add charges on the day for vehicle size or access changes.

Lordswood moves frequently extend into adjacent estates and out to the wider Medway towns, for the immediately neighbouring streets we run direct routes via our Walderslade removals service, and for moves into central Medway we coordinate with our Chatham removals service coverage.

Did you know? Your home contents insurance almost certainly does not cover your belongings during a removal. Every move we carry out is protected by comprehensive goods-in-transit insurance covering full replacement value. We carry proof of cover on every job and provide it on request before you commit to a booking.

How we price your Lordswood removal

Every Lordswood quote we write is built from the actual specifics of your move, not a one-size-fits-all rate card. The factors that genuinely affect the price are the volume of your contents, the property type and access at both ends, the distance involved, the date you want to move, and any extras such as packing, furniture dismantling and reassembly, or storage. For 3-bedroom homes and larger we offer a free pre-move survey, in person or via a video walkthrough, so the quote is grounded in what is actually being moved rather than a guess from a postcode and a property size.

Once you accept the quote, the figure is fixed. We do not add charges on the day for stairs, parking waits, heavier items, additional time, fuel, or anything else. For Lordswood moves specifically we factor in the A229 corridor and the J3 M2 access, and short cross-Medway runs to Chatham, Gillingham and Strood are typically completed inside a single day with no overnight storage required.
