Removal services in Sittingbourne — everything you need to know

Sittingbourne is the largest town in the Swale district of Kent, located on the North Kent coast between Medway and Faversham. The town has experienced significant growth in recent decades and continues to expand with new residential development, making it one of the more active housing markets in Central North Kent. Sittingbourne's location on the A2 corridor between Medway and Canterbury, with a fast commuter rail service to London Victoria and Charing Cross via the Chatham Main Line, makes it an increasingly popular choice for people relocating from both London and from other parts of Kent.

We carry out regular removals throughout Sittingbourne and the wider Swale district, covering the town itself across all its residential areas including Murston, Chalkwell, Borden, Tunstall, Milton Regis and the town centre, as well as the Sheppey towns of Sheerness, Minster-on-Sea and Queenborough accessible via the Sheppey Crossing, the coastal town of Whitstable and all surrounding villages and rural areas of the Swale district.

Sittingbourne's housing stock is predominantly post-war in its outer residential areas, with a mix of council and housing association properties, inter-war semis and more recent private housing estates. The town centre and the Milton Regis area contain older period properties. The ongoing expansion of Sittingbourne on its southern and eastern edges is adding significant new-build housing that generates growing removal demand from completions at developments including those at Quinton Road and other strategic housing sites around the town.

Our local knowledge of Sittingbourne

Sittingbourne town centre has some access challenges for larger removal vehicles, particularly around the High Street and the areas immediately adjacent to the railway station. The town centre has undergone significant regeneration in recent years and the road layout has changed accordingly. We keep our knowledge of Sittingbourne's current road and access arrangements up to date.

The Isle of Sheppey, accessible via the Sheppey Crossing, presents distinctive logistics for removals. The island is connected to the mainland by a single fixed crossing — the Sheppey Crossing bridge — which can be subject to closure in extreme weather conditions. For island moves, we plan our timing carefully to avoid the peak congestion that forms on the bridge approach roads at commuter times, and we always have a contingency plan for the rare occasions when the crossing is affected.

The rural areas of Swale district — the villages along the A2 corridor including Teynham, Newington and Sittingbourne's surrounding villages, as well as the more remote communities of the Swale estuary and Faversham Creek areas — present the characteristic challenges of rural Kent removal work. Narrow lanes, limited turning space and properties accessed via private tracks are common, and we assess each rural address individually to ensure we bring the correctly-sized vehicle.

Sittingbourne's industrial heritage has left a distinctive pattern on the town's residential areas, with some neighbourhoods having unusual access arrangements that reflect the former industrial land uses. The Murston area in particular has some roads and access points that require specific routing knowledge. Our team knows Sittingbourne's street layout thoroughly and plans every move based on the specific address.

Did you know? Your home contents insurance almost certainly does not cover your belongings during a removal. Every move we carry out is protected by comprehensive goods-in-transit insurance covering full replacement value. We carry proof of cover on every job and provide it on request before you commit to a booking.
