Long distance moves from Rochester, Medway and Kent

A long distance move is any move where the driving becomes part of the plan: Kent to Manchester, Medway to Bristol, Rochester to Edinburgh, or the reverse, bringing a family home to Kent from elsewhere in the country. These moves carry more moving parts than a local job: completion timings at both ends, a route that can include the M25 at its worst, overnight logistics for the longest trips, and no possibility of nipping back for whatever got forgotten.

Our answer to all of that is planning and ownership. One crew, from our own staff, loads your home in Kent and unloads it at the destination. Your belongings never change vehicles, never sit in a freight depot, and are never handed to a partner firm you have not met. The crew that wrapped your furniture is the crew that unwraps it, three hundred miles later, and the price agreed before the day covers the whole journey: fuel, tolls, crew hours and, where the schedule needs it, overnight accommodation. Nothing is added afterwards.

The routes we run most

From our Rochester base, minutes from the M2 and the A2 with fast access to the M25, M20 and the Dartford Crossing, the whole country is reachable, and over the last few years clear patterns have emerged in where Kent families go:

  • Kent to London and back: our most travelled corridor. Families leaving London for Medway's space and schools, and Kent professionals moving closer to work. See our guide to moving from London to Kent.
  • Kent to the South West: Bristol, Bath, Devon and Cornwall, the classic lifestyle move, often chasing coastline without London prices.
  • Kent to the Midlands and the North: Birmingham, Nottingham, Leeds, Manchester and Liverpool, usually for work, family or universities.
  • Kent to East Anglia: Essex, Suffolk and Norfolk, close enough for a single long day, far enough to need proper planning around the Dartford Crossing.
  • Kent to Scotland and Wales: the longest runs we do, planned as two-day moves with secure overnight arrangements for the vehicle.

We run every one of these in both directions. If you are relocating to Kent, we are the local firm at your destination end, which is exactly what you want: the crew that delivers your furniture knows the estate roads of Wainscott and the parking realities of Rochester High Street.

How a long distance move is planned

Distance amplifies small errors, so the plan carries more detail than a local move. After your quote is accepted, we confirm access at both properties, not just yours: a narrow lane in Cornwall or a permit-parking street in Manchester changes the vehicle choice and the schedule. We map the route around known pinch points and your completion time, and we decide together whether your move is a long single day or a two-day move with loading the day before.

The two-day pattern is the one we recommend for most full-house moves beyond about three hours' drive, and it takes the stress out of the completion chain: we load calmly the afternoon before, your belongings stay secure and insured on our locked vehicle overnight, and the crew sets off early to be at your new home as the keys arrive, working the unload in the early afternoon rather than at dusk. For moves to Scotland or the far South West, the overnight happens en route, and your quote says exactly how.

What does a long distance removal cost?

Long distance moves are quoted individually, because the honest price depends on volume, distance, access and schedule. To give usable orientation: a full three-bedroom house from Medway to the Midlands typically lands in the low-to-mid four figures, the South West similar or a little more, and Scotland at the upper end, with smaller loads proportionally less. A one-bedroom flat moving a long way is often better served by a dedicated man and van run, and we will tell you when that is true.

Two things we promise about every long distance price. First, it is fixed and complete: fuel, tolls, crew time, and any overnight costs are inside the number, not appended to it. Second, it is built on a real survey of your contents, because underquoted volume is how other firms' customers end up watching a second van being ordered on moving day. We measure first, then commit, then honour it.

Comparing quotes for a long move? Ask each firm two questions: does the same crew stay with the goods door to door, and is the price genuinely fixed including overnight costs? Cheaper long distance quotes are usually cheaper because the answer to one of those is no.

Packing, storage and the long-move extras

Almost every long distance customer takes our full packing service, and for good reason: three hundred miles of motorway is a serious test of packing, and professionally packed goods carry stronger insurance cover. Packing happens the day before loading, so the schedule stays calm.

Storage matters more on long moves too. When you are buying at a distance, sale and purchase dates align even less often than usual. Our secure storage near Rochester holds complete households between the Kent end and the destination end, for a week or for months, and we deliver onwards when your new home is ready, wherever it is. For moves into Kent, the same works in reverse: we can receive your belongings into storage before you arrive and deliver once you have keys.

Moving day, far from home

The long-move day itself runs like our local moves, stretched over more miles: a confirmation call the evening before, punctual arrival, floor protection down, methodical wrapped loading with the inventory checked as the van fills. Then the road. You travel in your own time, and the crew lead keeps you updated so you are never wondering where your furniture is. At the destination, the crew places everything room by room, rebuilds the beds and wardrobes they dismantled in Kent, and walks the house with you before the long drive home.

One practical difference from a local move: keep more with you. Documents, medicines, chargers, a change of clothes, the kettle box, and anything you would not want to be without for twenty-four hours belongs in your car, not the van. We will remind you of this in the pre-move call, along with a short checklist tuned to your specific route and schedule.

Planning your own journey on moving day

On a local move you follow the van; on a long move you run your own parallel journey, and it deserves a plan of its own. Decide early whether you are travelling on moving day or the evening before: for two-day moves, most families travel on day one after the van is loaded, stay overnight near the new home and meet the crew fresh in the morning. Book that accommodation when you book the move, not the week before, especially in summer.

Pack the car deliberately: documents, medicines, chargers, valuables, a kettle kit, one change of clothes each, and whatever keeps children and pets sane across four hours of motorway. If you have two cars, agree who carries what and who arrives first. Plan your fuel and food stops rather than improvising them, and share your live location with our crew lead so timing coordination at the destination is effortless. Finally, hold the first evening lightly: beds rebuilt, takeaway ordered, boxes ignored. The unpacking belongs to the days after, not the night you arrive.

The first week after a long move

A long-distance move does not finish when our van leaves; it finishes when the new place works. From hundreds of these moves, the first-week list that matters: register with a GP and, if you have children, confirm school start dates immediately, because both queues move slower than you expect. Update your address for driving licence, vehicle log book, insurance and electoral roll in one sitting. Find the supermarket, the pharmacy and the takeaway before you need them. Unpack by room with the kitchen first and boxes gone from bedrooms by day three, because sleeping among cardboard wears thin fast. And if a box turns up damaged or something is missed, tell us straight away; distance does not change our responsibility, and our insurance process works the same three hundred miles from Rochester as it does three streets away.

Budgeting the whole relocation, not just the removal

The removal is usually the single largest line on a long-distance moving budget, but it is rarely the only one, and the families who arrive calm are the ones who budgeted the whole journey. Alongside your fixed removal quote, allow for: a night's accommodation near the new home if yours is a two-day move; fuel and food for your own drive; professional cleaning of the home you are leaving, which many sale contracts and almost all tenancies expect; and early utility set-up costs at the destination, where a new broadband installation can carry both a fee and a three-week lead time worth starting before you travel.

Two budget lines deserve special respect. First, overlap costs: if you can carry both properties for even a few days, the move relaxes enormously, and our storage is the fallback when you cannot. Second, the contingency: chains wobble, vans cannot unload in a closed street festival, and weather happens; a few hundred pounds of unallocated budget turns each of these from a crisis into an anecdote. What you should not need to budget for is the removal price moving, because ours does not. The number on your quote is the number on your invoice, whether the journey is to Maidstone or to Aberdeen.

When you are ready to put real numbers against your plans, the process takes minutes to start: call 01634 971005 or send the quote form with your destination, your rough dates and your property size. We will survey properly, quote a fixed door-to-door price, and hold your provisional date while your chain firms up. Long moves reward early conversations, so even if completion is months away and the destination is still a shortlist of towns, talk to us; we have priced enough of Britain to tell you quickly what each option really costs to reach.

Wherever the road ends, the measure of the move is the same one we apply in Medway: did the crew arrive when promised, was every box and bed exactly where it should be by evening, and did the invoice match the quote to the penny. Three hundred miles does not change the standard; it just makes it matter more. That is the service, and it starts with one phone call to Rochester.
