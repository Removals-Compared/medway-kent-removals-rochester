Moving House in Sittingbourne: A Complete Guide

Published 22 Jul 2025  |  8 min read  |  Medway & Kent Removals

Sittingbourne is a market town in the ME10 postcode, sitting between the Medway towns and Canterbury along the A2 corridor. Long overlooked in favour of its neighbours, Sittingbourne has undergone significant regeneration in recent years — particularly around Milton Creek and the town centre — and now offers an increasingly attractive proposition for buyers seeking good value, reasonable London commute times and easy access to both the Kent coast and the wider county.

Sittingbourne neighbourhoods

Sittingbourne Town Centre

The ME10 postcode covers central Sittingbourne including the High Street and the areas immediately around the railway station. The town centre has seen significant investment with the Sittingbourne town centre regeneration project bringing new retail, residential and public space to the heart of the town. Properties here include Victorian and Edwardian terraces and modern apartments in the newer developments.

Murston and Kemsley

To the east of the town centre, Murston and Kemsley are residential areas with a mix of post-war housing and more modern estates. Kemsley Fields is a significant new residential development that has attracted families and first-time buyers seeking new-build properties at accessible prices within easy reach of Sittingbourne station.

Milton Regis

North of the town centre toward Milton Creek, Milton Regis is a historic village that has been absorbed into greater Sittingbourne. The area has a distinct character from the main town and some attractive older properties. The Milton Creek waterfront area is undergoing regeneration with new residential and leisure development.

Borden and Tunstall

To the south of Sittingbourne, the villages of Borden and Tunstall offer a rural alternative with larger properties, good schools and easy access to the A249 and M2. Borden has an outstanding primary school and a strong local community. Property here commands a modest premium over the town itself but remains excellent value for the setting.

Property in Sittingbourne

Sittingbourne is one of the most affordable towns in Kent for the level of access it offers. The average property price in ME10 is approximately £240,000 to £290,000. A three-bedroom terraced house costs between £200,000 and £260,000. A four-bedroom detached house in Borden or Tunstall costs between £310,000 and £430,000. For buyers relocating from London or the Medway towns seeking more space for the budget, Sittingbourne offers compelling value.

Commuting from Sittingbourne

Sittingbourne station on the Southeastern mainline offers direct services to London Victoria taking approximately 65 to 75 minutes. The A2 and M2 provide good road connections to Rochester, Chatham, Maidstone and the M25. Annual season tickets from Sittingbourne to London are approximately £4,500 to £5,000. The town is also well placed for employment in the wider Medway towns, Maidstone and Canterbury.

Schools in Sittingbourne

Sittingbourne has several good primary schools across the ME10 postcode. Secondary school options include Westlands School and Fulston Manor School as the main comprehensive options. Grammar school access from Sittingbourne — to schools in Maidstone, Chatham and Faversham — requires travel but is accessible via the A2 and M2.

Moving day in Sittingbourne

Sittingbourne has a straightforward road network for removal logistics. The A2 through the town provides good access from the motorway network. The main access challenges are in the older terraced streets close to the town centre where on-street parking can be congested. New-build developments at Kemsley Fields and Milton Creek have modern access roads. Medway and Kent Removals covers Sittingbourne and all ME10 postcodes regularly from our Rochester depot.

Sittingbourne to Medway towns — a popular move route

Many families move between Sittingbourne and the Medway towns as they progress through the property market in either direction. Medway and Kent Removals carries out this route regularly. A typical Sittingbourne to Rochester or Chatham move takes between three and six hours depending on volume.
