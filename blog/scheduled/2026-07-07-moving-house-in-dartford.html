Moving House in Dartford: A Complete Guide

Published 8 Jul 2025  |  8 min read  |  Medway & Kent Removals

Dartford sits at the western gateway to Kent — directly on the M25 at the Dartford Crossing and with fast rail services to London Bridge and London Charing Cross in under 30 minutes. It is one of the most practically connected towns in Kent for London commuters and has seen significant regeneration investment, making it an increasingly attractive option for buyers and renters seeking value and connectivity in North West Kent.

Dartford neighbourhoods

Dartford Town Centre

The DA1 postcode covers central Dartford including the High Street, the railway station and the streets closest to the town centre amenities. Properties here are predominantly Victorian and Edwardian terraces, post-war housing and modern apartments. The town centre has been significantly improved by recent investment and has a good range of retail and food options.

Stone and Bean

To the north of Dartford toward the Thames, Stone and Bean are suburban areas with a range of semi-detached and detached housing popular with families. Good primary schools and easy access to the A2 and M25.

Swanscombe and Greenhithe

To the west of Dartford, Swanscombe and Greenhithe sit on the Thames estuary and have seen significant regeneration. Greenhithe in particular has modern riverside apartment developments with outstanding Thames views and Ebbsfleet International station within easy reach. Popular with young professionals seeking a contemporary riverside lifestyle at accessible prices.

Ebbsfleet Garden City

One of the most significant new town developments in the South East, Ebbsfleet Garden City is being built around Ebbsfleet International station — which offers high-speed services to London St Pancras in 18 minutes. The development will eventually accommodate 15,000 new homes and is already a significant destination for buyers seeking fast London access in a modern, well-planned environment.

Property in Dartford

Dartford offers excellent value for the journey time to London. The average property price in DA1 is approximately £320,000 to £370,000. A three-bedroom semi-detached house costs between £290,000 and £380,000. For London buyers relocating to Dartford, the value for money compared to equivalent Zone 4 or Zone 5 properties is very strong.

Commuting from Dartford to London

Dartford station has fast services to London Bridge and London Charing Cross taking approximately 25 to 35 minutes — one of the fastest Kent commutes to central London. Ebbsfleet International station offers high-speed Javelin services to London St Pancras in just 18 minutes. Annual season tickets from Dartford to London are approximately £3,400 to £3,800. The M25 at Dartford Crossing also provides road access to East London, Essex and the M11.

Schools in Dartford

Dartford Grammar School for Boys and Dartford Grammar School for Girls are both selective state schools in the Kent grammar system with strong academic records. Several comprehensive schools serve the Dartford area. Primary school provision includes a number of Good and Outstanding rated schools across the DA1 and DA2 postcodes.

Moving day in Dartford

Dartford's proximity to the M25 and M2 makes it one of the more straightforward North Kent towns for removal logistics. The town centre has standard parking restrictions but nothing as complex as Canterbury or Rochester's historic cores. Ebbsfleet and Swanscombe developments have modern access roads designed for larger vehicles. Medway and Kent Removals covers Dartford and all surrounding DA postcodes regularly.

Ebbsfleet developments have specific move-in procedures

If you are moving into one of Ebbsfleet Garden City's newer developments, check with the property management team about any specific vehicle access, time restrictions or lift booking procedures that apply to your building. We always confirm these details in advance for all new-build and managed development moves.
