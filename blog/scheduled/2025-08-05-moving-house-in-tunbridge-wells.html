Tunbridge Wells Guide

Moving House in Tunbridge Wells: A Complete Guide

Published 5 Aug 2025  |  9 min read  |  Medway & Kent Removals

Royal Tunbridge Wells is one of Kent's most complete and sought-after towns — a Georgian spa town with one of the finest independent retail and restaurant scenes in the South East, outstanding schools both state and independent, beautiful Wealden countryside on the doorstep and fast trains to London Charing Cross in under 55 minutes. For families and professionals relocating to West Kent, Tunbridge Wells is consistently at the top of the list.

Tunbridge Wells neighbourhoods

The Pantiles and Town Centre

The Pantiles — a colonnaded Georgian promenade unique in England — is Tunbridge Wells at its most distinctive. Independent shops, galleries, restaurants and the famous chalybeate spring sit under the colonnade. Properties around the Pantiles and in the streets of the TN1 postcode are predominantly Georgian and Victorian townhouses and period apartments. This is the most characterful and most sought-after address in the town. Properties command a significant premium but represent outstanding value against equivalent London properties.

Broadwater Down and Nevill Park

To the west of the town centre, Broadwater Down and Nevill Park are two of the most prestigious residential addresses in Kent — large Victorian and Edwardian detached houses with substantial gardens in wooded private roads. Properties here command the highest prices in Tunbridge Wells and attract high-net-worth buyers seeking maximum privacy and space within easy reach of the town and the station.

Hawkenbury and St James

To the east of the town centre, Hawkenbury and St James are more affordable residential areas with good primary schools and easy access to the town centre and railway station. Popular with young families and first-time buyers seeking their first Tunbridge Wells property.

Southborough and Pembury

The surrounding villages of Southborough and Pembury — both within a few minutes of the town — offer slightly more affordable alternatives to Tunbridge Wells itself while remaining within easy reach of the town's amenities and schools. Southborough has its own shops and community identity. Pembury has an outstanding primary school.

Property in Tunbridge Wells

Tunbridge Wells commands premium prices for a Kent town — consistently among the most expensive in the county. The average property price in TN1 and TN4 is approximately £420,000 to £500,000. A three-bedroom semi-detached house in a typical Tunbridge Wells street costs between £400,000 and £550,000. Properties in Broadwater Down and Nevill Park start at £900,000 and can exceed £2 million for the largest houses. For buyers relocating from Zone 2 London, the space and lifestyle improvement at these prices remains compelling.

Commuting from Tunbridge Wells to London

Tunbridge Wells station has direct services to London Charing Cross and London Bridge taking approximately 50 to 60 minutes. Trains run frequently throughout the day. Annual season tickets cost approximately £4,500 to £5,000 — good value for the quality of life that Tunbridge Wells offers. Many Tunbridge Wells residents commute three or four days per week and work remotely for the remainder.

Schools in Tunbridge Wells

Tunbridge Wells Grammar School for Boys and Tunbridge Wells Girls Grammar School are both outstanding selective state schools with strong academic track records. Tonbridge School and several other independent schools are accessible within 10 to 15 minutes. The independent sector in Tunbridge Wells itself includes Rose Hill School and Beechwood Sacred Heart School. Primary school provision is strong across the TN postcodes with several Outstanding rated schools.

Moving day in Tunbridge Wells

Tunbridge Wells presents a range of access challenges depending on the specific property. The Pantiles and historic town centre streets can be narrow and congested. The private roads of Broadwater Down and Nevill Park require careful vehicle selection — many have restricted access for large vehicles. The surrounding villages have typical country lane access requirements. Medway and Kent Removals surveys all Tunbridge Wells properties before moving day and selects the appropriate vehicle for every location.

Tunbridge Wells is one of our busier destinations and Friday moves particularly fill up fast. For moves in or around Tunbridge Wells, book at least six to eight weeks in advance to secure your preferred date.
