Moving House in Gravesend: A Complete Guide

Published 10 Jun 2025  |  9 min read  |  Medway & Kent Removals

Gravesend sits on the southern bank of the Thames in the DA11 and DA12 postcodes — technically in Kent but with a character and identity strongly shaped by its proximity to London and its river heritage. For families and professionals seeking Thames-side living with fast London access and good value property, Gravesend is one of the most practical and underrated options in North Kent.

Gravesend's neighbourhoods

Gravesend Town Centre and the Riverside

The DA11 postcode covers central Gravesend and the riverside areas closest to the Thames. Properties here include Victorian and Edwardian terraces, post-war housing and an increasing number of modern riverside apartments that take advantage of the remarkable Thames views. The town centre has a good range of retail and independent restaurants. The riverside setting — with direct views across the Thames to Tilbury in Essex — is one of Gravesend's most distinctive features.

Northfleet

West of Gravesend, Northfleet is a residential area with a strong community identity and more affordable property than the town centre. The Northfleet area has seen significant regeneration investment and has good access to Gravesend station and the A2.

Chalk and Shorne

To the east of Gravesend, the villages of Chalk and Shorne offer a semi-rural alternative with larger detached properties, excellent countryside access and Shorne Country Park immediately accessible. Popular with families seeking more space while remaining within easy reach of Gravesend's amenities and rail links.

Istead Rise and Meopham

South of Gravesend, the villages of Istead Rise and Meopham sit in the North Downs and offer a more rural character with larger properties and outstanding countryside. The Meopham area has its own railway station on the Medway Valley Line. Property here is more affordable than equivalent rural Kent locations with better London access.

Property in Gravesend

Gravesend offers excellent value — the best in North Kent for London access. The average property price in DA11 is approximately £290,000 to £340,000. A three-bedroom terraced house typically costs between £250,000 and £310,000. Larger detached properties in Chalk and Shorne cost between £400,000 and £600,000. For buyers relocating from London, the value compared to equivalent Zone 4 or Zone 5 London properties is extremely compelling.

Commuting from Gravesend to London

Gravesend station is on the Southeastern mainline with fast trains to London Victoria and London Charing Cross taking approximately 35 to 45 minutes. This makes Gravesend one of the fastest London commutes of any Kent town and a significant draw for London professionals seeking better housing value. The A2 and M2 provide good road access to the M25, Dartford Crossing and East London. Annual season tickets from Gravesend to London are approximately £3,800 to £4,200 — excellent value for the journey time.

Schools in Gravesend

Gravesend Grammar School is a selective boys' school in the Medway grammar system with a strong academic reputation. Mayfield Grammar School provides the selective option for girls. The non-selective sector includes Northfleet School for Girls, Northfleet Technology College and several good primary schools across the DA11 and DA12 postcodes.

Moving day in Gravesend — practical considerations

Gravesend town centre has controlled parking zones and some streets with restricted access that require advance planning for removal vehicles. The A2 through Gravesend can be congested during peak hours. For moves from Gravesend to Medway or vice versa, the A2 and then M2 is the standard route. Medway & Kent Removals carries out regular moves between Gravesend and all parts of the Medway towns and wider Kent.

Gravesend to Rochester — a common move route

Many families move between Gravesend and the Medway towns as they progress through the property market. Medway & Kent Removals covers both areas and carries out this route regularly. A typical move takes between four and seven hours.
