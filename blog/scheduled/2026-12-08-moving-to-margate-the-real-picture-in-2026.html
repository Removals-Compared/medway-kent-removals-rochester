Moving to Margate: The Real Picture in 2026

Published by Medway Kent Removals | December 2026

Margate has been Kent's most talked-about destination for a decade. The Hackney-on-Sea narrative has brought artists, creatives and London buyers in waves. Here is the honest picture after years of moving people in and out of the town.

What has genuinely changed

The Old Town is excellent. The independent food and drink scene is real and has depth. Dreamland is a genuine cultural asset. Turner Contemporary has put Margate on the international map in a way that is permanent. These things are not hype - they are real improvements.

What the articles do not always mention

Margate has significant areas of deprivation alongside the gentrified parts. The town is not uniformly transformed - it is patchy. The Cliftonville area east of the town centre has improved markedly. Other parts are unchanged. Research the specific street before you buy.

The train

Margate to London St Pancras on a fast train is around 90 minutes. Slower trains take longer. For a daily commute this is at the outer limit of what most people sustain. For flexible workers it is entirely viable.

Property values

Property prices in Margate have risen significantly since 2016. The discount versus London is narrower than it was. The value case is less dramatic than a decade ago but still exists for buyers from inner London boroughs.
