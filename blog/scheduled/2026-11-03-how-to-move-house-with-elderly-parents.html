How to Move House with Elderly Parents

Published by Medway Kent Removals | November 2026

Moving an elderly parent is one of the more emotionally complex moves a family faces. The logistics are manageable. The emotional dimension requires more care. Here is how to approach it well.

Involve them in every decision

Even if you are the one organising the move, your parent should be involved in decisions about what comes with them, what goes to family, what is donated and what is disposed of. Ownership of the process matters enormously for how they settle into the new home.

Do not rush the declutter

A lifetime of belongings cannot be sorted in a weekend. Plan for several sessions over several weeks. Some items will carry decades of memory. Rushing this process creates distress that can persist long after the move is complete.

Recreate the familiar

At the new property, prioritise unpacking the items that make the space feel like home - favourite chair, familiar pictures, the television in the right position. The physical environment significantly affects how quickly an elderly person settles.

Book a full packing service

For an elderly parent's move, professional packing removes a significant burden from both them and you. Our packers work carefully and respectfully with belongings that carry personal significance. Nothing is rushed.

Allow extra time on moving day

Elderly moves take longer. That is not a problem - it is just reality. We plan accordingly and never make an elderly client feel rushed on moving day.
