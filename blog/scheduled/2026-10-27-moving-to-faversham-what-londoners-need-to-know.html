Moving to Faversham: What Londoners Need to Know

Published by Medway Kent Removals | October 2026

Faversham has become one of the most talked-about destinations for London buyers looking to relocate to Kent. We move families into Faversham regularly. Here is what they consistently tell us after they have settled in.

The train connection is genuinely good

Faversham to London Victoria takes around 70 minutes. London St Pancras via Ashford is faster for some destinations. For a buyer coming from South or East London, this is genuinely competitive with many commutes within the M25.

The town itself is the surprise

Most London buyers expect a pleasant but quiet market town. They find a genuinely vibrant community with good independent restaurants, a strong arts scene, a historic market and the kind of social life that does not require a car to access. The Creek area particularly catches people off guard.

The housing stock is varied

Georgian townhouses in the centre, Victorian terraces in the residential streets, modern developments on the edges. Property prices are significantly below equivalent stock in Sevenoaks or Tonbridge. The value equation is currently very strong.

What surprises people

The parking. Faversham town centre has limited parking and it fills up quickly. New arrivals from London often find this less of an issue than they expected - they have adapted to not relying on a car. Those moving from suburban Essex or Surrey sometimes find it more of an adjustment.
