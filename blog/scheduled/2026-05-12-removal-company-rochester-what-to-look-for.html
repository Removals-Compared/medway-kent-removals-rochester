How to Find the Right Removal Company in Rochester, Kent

Published 13 May 2025  |  9 min read  |  Medway & Kent Removals

Finding the right removal company in Rochester is one of the most important decisions you will make during a house move. Get it right and moving day runs smoothly, your belongings arrive safely and the stress of the move is minimised. Get it wrong and you face hidden charges, uninsured damage and a team unfamiliar with Rochester's specific access challenges that turn an already demanding day into a nightmare.

This guide covers exactly what to look for when choosing a removal company in Rochester and Medway — the essential checks, the right questions to ask and the red flags that should make you look elsewhere.

Why local knowledge matters more in Rochester than almost anywhere

Rochester is not a straightforward town to move in. The historic city centre has narrow medieval streets, pedestrianised zones and controlled parking areas that require advance planning. Victorian terraces throughout ME1 have narrow hallways and steep stairs that require the right crew size and equipment. New-build developments near the Medway waterfront have their own access restrictions. A removal company without genuine local knowledge of Rochester and Medway will encounter problems on moving day that a locally-based team would never face.

When you choose a Rochester-based removal company — particularly one with a depot in the city as Medway & Kent Removals has at Knight Templar Way ME2 — you get a team that knows every street, every parking restriction and every access challenge before they arrive. That local knowledge is not a small thing. On moving day it is the difference between a move that finishes on time and one that runs three hours over schedule.

The 8 essential checks before booking any removal company

1. Are they fully insured?

This is the most important check of all and it must be verified before booking. Any professional removal company must carry comprehensive goods-in-transit insurance covering the full replacement value of your belongings from the moment loading begins until the last item is placed in your new home. They must also carry public liability insurance. Ask to see the insurance certificate — not just a verbal confirmation. If a company hesitates when asked, assume they do not have adequate cover.

2. Do they offer a fixed price?

Hourly rate removals can spiral significantly on moving day. Traffic delays, access difficulties, more belongings than expected — all of these add time and therefore cost to an hourly rate move. A fixed price agreed in advance based on a proper survey of your home means you know exactly what you are paying regardless of how the day unfolds. Always insist on a fixed price based on a survey, not an estimate over the phone.

3. Do they carry out a pre-move survey?

A pre-move survey — either in person at your property or via a detailed video call — is how a professional removal company accurately assesses the volume of your belongings, identifies any access challenges and provides an accurate fixed price. Any company that provides a quote without surveying your home is guessing. That guess will either be inflated to cover unknowns or will result in additional charges on moving day when the volume turns out to be larger than assumed.

4. Are their reviews genuine and local?

Check Google reviews specifically. Look for reviews from Rochester and Medway customers — not just generic positive reviews. Read the negative reviews carefully. How a company responds to complaints tells you as much about their professionalism as the complaints themselves. A consistent pattern of 5-star reviews from local customers over an extended period is a strong quality indicator. Be cautious of companies with a sudden cluster of reviews or reviews that feel generic and unspecific.

5. Do they use their own vehicles and employ their own staff?

Some removal companies subcontract moves to third parties, particularly during busy periods. This means the people handling your belongings may be unfamiliar with the company's methods, may not be covered by the company's insurance in the same way and may have no previous experience of Rochester's specific access challenges. Always ask directly — will the crew on moving day be your own employed staff in your own vehicles?

6. What is their cancellation and postponement policy?

House purchases fall through. Completion dates change. Your removal company should have a clear, fair and written policy for postponements and cancellations. Ask specifically — what notice is required to change your moving date without penalty? What happens if your completion is delayed on the day? A professional company will have clear answers to both questions.

7. Can they handle specialist items?

If you have a piano, a large safe, American-style appliances or antique furniture, confirm before booking that the removal company has the equipment and experience to handle these items safely. Always declare specialist items when requesting your quote — a piano that is not planned for on moving day causes significant delays and potential damage.

8. What is their claims process if something is damaged?

Ask this question directly before booking. A professional company will explain their claims process clearly and confirm the insurance excess. Vague answers — "we'll sort it out if it happens" — are a red flag. You want to know in advance that any claim would be handled promptly and fairly.

Get at least three quotes before deciding

For a move of any significant size, get at least three fixed-price quotes from different removal companies. The cheapest quote is not always the best value — compare what is included in each quote, the company's insurance cover and their local reputation alongside the price.

Red flags — when to look elsewhere

  • Demanding full payment upfront — a deposit is standard, full payment before the move is not
  • No verifiable business address — a professional removal company has a physical depot, not just a mobile number
  • Very low quotes without a survey — expect hidden charges on moving day
  • No written confirmation — everything must be confirmed in writing before you pay a deposit
  • Reluctance to provide insurance documentation — if they hesitate, assume the cover is inadequate
  • No online presence or reviews — a professional removal company operating in 2025 has a website and Google reviews

Questions specific to Rochester moves

Beyond the general checks, there are questions specific to Rochester and Medway moves that are worth asking any removal company you are considering:

  • Do you know the parking suspension process for Medway Council?
  • Have you moved properties in the Rochester historic core before?
  • What size vehicles do you operate and which would you use for my specific move?
  • Do you have experience with properties on the Medway waterfront or in older ME1 streets?

A Rochester-based removal company should be able to answer all of these questions immediately and with confidence. A company based elsewhere that is unfamiliar with Rochester specifically may struggle — and that unfamiliarity will show on moving day.
