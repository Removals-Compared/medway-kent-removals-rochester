What Removal Companies Wish You Knew Before Moving Day

Published by Medway Kent Removals | November 2026

After hundreds of moves across Kent and Medway, we have seen the same avoidable situations come up repeatedly. Here is what our team genuinely wishes customers knew before the van arrives.

Boxes should be sealed and labelled on the sides, not the top

When boxes are stacked in a van or a room, you cannot see the top. Labels on the side mean our crew can read them at a glance and place them in the right room immediately. This one change makes unloading significantly faster.

Tell us about parking before moving day, not on it

A removal van needs to park close to your property. If there is a Controlled Parking Zone, a narrow lane, a height barrier or a shared driveway that needs prior notice, we need to know in advance. Discovering this on moving day causes delays and stress that are entirely avoidable.

Do not pack your essentials box last

The essentials box - kettle, mugs, phone charger, important documents, medication, children's comfort items - should be packed the day before and travel in your car, not on the van. You will need it on arrival before the van is unloaded.

The piano cannot go through the window without planning

We move pianos regularly. An upright piano through a first-floor window requires planning, specialist equipment and sometimes scaffolding. None of this is insurmountable but it needs to be arranged in advance, not discovered on the day.

Keep your phone on all day

On completion day particularly, we need to be able to reach you quickly if there is a delay or a question about where something goes. An unreachable customer on moving day is the most common cause of avoidable problems.
