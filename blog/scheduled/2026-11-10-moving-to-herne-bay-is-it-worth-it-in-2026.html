Moving to Herne Bay: Is It Worth It in 2026?

Published by Medway Kent Removals | November 2026

Herne Bay has changed significantly over the past five years. Rising property prices, a growing food and arts scene and increasing London buyer interest have transformed the conversation around this North Kent coastal town. Here is our honest assessment.

The seafront

Herne Bay's seafront is genuinely excellent. The pier, the beach, the King's Hall - there is real quality here for a coastal town of its size. Summers are busy but manageable. The seafront in winter has a particular atmosphere that long-term residents value.

The commute

Herne Bay to London St Pancras via Faversham is around 80 minutes. For many London buyers this is the outer limit of what is practical for a daily commute. Those working flexibly find it entirely workable.

The value

Property prices in Herne Bay remain below Whitstable and significantly below Faversham. That gap has been narrowing but there is still meaningful value for buyers coming from London or the Medway towns.

What the estate agents do not mention

Some streets in Herne Bay are significantly more desirable than others. The seafront and the conservation area around the town centre are excellent. Some of the outlying residential streets are more ordinary. Research individual streets before making an offer.
