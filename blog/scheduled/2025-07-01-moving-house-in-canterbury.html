Moving House in Canterbury: A Complete Guide

Published 1 Jul 2025  |  9 min read  |  Medway & Kent Removals

Canterbury is one of England's most historic and culturally significant cities and an increasingly popular destination for people moving within Kent and from further afield. The UNESCO World Heritage Cathedral, the Marlowe Theatre, an outstanding independent food and drink scene and the high-speed rail service to London St Pancras in 56 minutes combine to make Canterbury one of the most complete cities in the South East.

Canterbury neighbourhoods

Canterbury City Centre — within and near the walls

The CT1 postcode covers the historic city centre including the area within the Roman walls. Properties here include medieval and Tudor buildings, Georgian townhouses, Victorian terraces and modern apartments. This is Canterbury at its most atmospheric and most sought-after. Properties within the walls command a significant premium and are in consistent demand from professionals, academics and those who value character and location above space.

Wincheap and South Canterbury

South of the city walls, Wincheap and the broader south Canterbury area offer a more affordable entry point to the Canterbury market. Victorian and Edwardian terraces line the streets radiating south. Good access to the city centre on foot and by bus, and closer to the A2 for road commuters.

Bridge, Chartham and the Stour Valley villages

For those seeking a village setting within easy reach of Canterbury, the villages of Bridge, Chartham and Chilham in the Stour Valley offer outstanding character properties, beautiful countryside and a quieter pace of life. Canterbury city centre is 10 to 15 minutes by car. Property prices command a premium but represent outstanding value by South East standards.

Property in Canterbury

The average property price in CT1 is approximately £330,000 to £390,000. A three-bedroom terraced house in a typical Canterbury street costs between £280,000 and £360,000. A four-bedroom detached house in the suburbs or surrounding villages costs between £450,000 and £700,000. Period properties within the walls and in the most desirable streets command significantly more.

Commuting from Canterbury

The high-speed Southeastern Javelin service from Canterbury West to London St Pancras takes 56 minutes. Standard services from Canterbury East to London Victoria take approximately 90 minutes. Annual season tickets on the high-speed service cost approximately £5,000 to £5,500. Many Canterbury residents commute two or three days per week and work remotely for the remainder.

Schools in Canterbury

The Langton Grammar School for Boys and Barton Court Grammar School for Girls are both highly regarded selective state schools. The King's School Canterbury — founded in 597 AD — is nationally recognised as one of the finest independent schools in the country. Canterbury Christ Church University and the University of Kent ensure a significant and permanent academic community in the city.

Moving day in Canterbury

Canterbury's medieval street layout creates genuine access challenges for removal vehicles. Many streets within and near the city walls are narrow, have weight restrictions or are subject to pedestrian priority at certain times. Medway and Kent Removals surveys access for all Canterbury moves as part of our standard process and uses appropriately sized vehicles for every Canterbury property.

Book Canterbury moves well in advance

Canterbury is one of our busier destinations and end-of-term periods for the universities create very high demand in June and September. Book at least six weeks in advance for moves during these periods.
