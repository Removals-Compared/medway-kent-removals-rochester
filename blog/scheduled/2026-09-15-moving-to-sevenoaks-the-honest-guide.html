Moving to Sevenoaks: The Honest Guide

Published by Medway Kent Removals | September 2026

Sevenoaks consistently appears in lists of the best places to live in the South East. As removal specialists who move families into and out of Sevenoaks regularly, here is our honest assessment of what you are moving to.

The commute

Sevenoaks to London Bridge takes approximately 28 minutes on a fast train. Cannon Street is 32 minutes. It is one of the best commuter connections in Kent. The station is walkable from the town centre. This is the primary reason most people move here, and it is genuinely excellent.

The schools

Sevenoaks School is one of the top independent schools in the country. The state secondary provision through Knole Academy and Trinity School is solid. Primary schools in the area are consistently good to outstanding. School places are competitive - apply early if this is a priority.

The housing

Sevenoaks is expensive by Kent standards. Expect to pay a significant premium over comparable property in Tonbridge or Maidstone. Period properties in the town centre and detached houses in surrounding villages command high prices. The trade-off is genuine quality of housing stock.

The practical stuff

Traffic through the town centre at peak hours is genuinely bad. Parking is limited and the one-way system catches newcomers out regularly. If you are moving furniture to a property in the town centre, early morning is essential. We always plan Sevenoaks moves around access windows.
