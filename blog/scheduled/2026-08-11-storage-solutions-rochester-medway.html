Storage Solutions in Rochester and Medway: A Complete Guide

Published 12 Aug 2025  |  7 min read  |  Medway & Kent Removals

Storage is one of the most useful tools available during a house move in Medway and Kent — and one of the most frequently needed, given the active property market and common occurrence of completion gaps, renovation projects and downsizing in the area. This guide explains when storage genuinely helps, what it involves and how Medway and Kent Removals can provide a seamless storage solution as part of your move.

When storage is needed during a Medway or Kent house move

Completion gap — when your dates do not align

The most common reason for storage in the Medway property market is a gap between the date you must vacate your current property and the date you can access your new one. This happens when a property chain has not completed synchronously — you need to be out of your old home before the purchase of the new one has completed.

A completion gap of one to four weeks is common in the Medway towns property market, particularly for chains involving first-time buyers, remortgages and properties at different price levels. In this situation, storage is not just useful — it is essential. Medway and Kent Removals can collect from your current property, store your belongings in our secure facility and deliver to your new property as soon as it is ready.

Renovation or building work in progress

Many buyers in Rochester, Chatham and Maidstone purchase older properties that require significant renovation before they are ready to live in — new kitchens, bathrooms, rewiring, replastering or structural work. Moving household goods into a building site risks irreparable damage from dust, moisture and construction debris. Storing belongings until the work is complete and the property is clean and ready is the right approach and saves significant cost in cleaning and potential damage.

Downsizing — more belongings than the new home can accommodate

Medway's property market sees a significant volume of downsizing moves — families whose children have left home moving from larger family houses to smaller properties, retirees moving from houses to bungalows or apartments. When the new property is significantly smaller than the current one, storage provides breathing space to decide what to keep and what to sell, donate or dispose of — rather than making rushed decisions under moving day pressure.

Between properties — renting temporarily

Some buyers choose to rent temporarily between selling their current home and completing on their new purchase — to strengthen their negotiating position as a chain-free buyer. In this situation, furniture and household goods are typically too large for the temporary rental property. Storage solves this efficiently, with belongings kept safely until the permanent new home is ready.

What to look for in a Medway or Kent storage provider

  • Comprehensive insurance — all items in storage must be insured against loss, damage and theft from the moment of collection until delivery. Confirm the coverage level and the claims process before committing.
  • Dry and monitored facility — storage facilities must be dry, well-maintained and monitored. Moisture is the single biggest risk to stored household goods over any significant period.
  • Itemised inventory — every item collected should be inventoried on collection so you have a complete record of what is in storage at all times.
  • Flexible terms — storage needs during a house move are often unpredictable. Your provider should offer flexible terms that allow you to access or retrieve your belongings without excessive notice periods or penalty charges.

How long will you need storage?

For a completion gap situation, storage is typically needed for one to four weeks. For renovation projects, storage may be needed for four to twelve weeks depending on the scope of the work. For downsizing situations, three to six months of storage while decisions are made is common. Medway and Kent Removals offers storage from one week upward and prices are quoted based on the volume of items and duration.

Pack items for storage more carefully than for a move

Items that will be in storage for more than a few weeks need more careful packing than items moving directly to a new property. All items should be wrapped, boxed and labelled. Soft furnishings should be covered with dust sheets or stored in protective bags. Electronics should be in sealed boxes. A little extra care on packing for storage pays dividends when the items come out weeks or months later in perfect condition.
