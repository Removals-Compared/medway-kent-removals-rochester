How to Move House in December Without the Stress

Published by Medway Kent Removals | December 2026

December moves have a bad reputation they do not entirely deserve. Yes, it is cold, dark and the solicitors slow down before Christmas. But a December move in Kent also has genuine advantages - and with the right planning it is very manageable.

Book before the solicitors slow down

The biggest risk in a December completion is solicitor availability in the final two weeks of the month. If you are completing before 20 December, you are in reasonable shape. After 20 December, ask your solicitor explicitly whether they are working between Christmas and New Year and who covers if they are not.

The advantages of December

Removal companies are quieter. You will have more date flexibility and potentially better availability than in peak summer months. The property market slows too, which occasionally means motivated sellers and less competition on the buy side.

Protect your belongings from the cold

Electronics and some furniture do not respond well to being moved in freezing temperatures. We take extra care with items sensitive to cold and always wrap belongings thoroughly in winter moves. If snow or ice is forecast, we discuss contingency options with clients in advance.

The Christmas deadline pressure

Many buyers targeting a December completion are motivated by wanting to be in the new home for Christmas. This creates a real deadline. Build in buffer time wherever you can - do not assume everything will complete on the last Friday before Christmas Day.
