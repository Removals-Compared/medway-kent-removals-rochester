How Long Does a House Removal Take in Medway and Kent?

Published 24 Jun 2025  |  7 min read  |  Medway & Kent Removals

One of the most common questions we receive from customers planning a house move in Medway and Kent is how long the removal will take. The answer depends on several factors specific to your property and move — but this guide gives you realistic estimates based on actual removals across the Medway towns and wider Kent area, along with everything that can affect the timing on the day.

Typical removal times for Medway and Kent properties

One-bedroom flat or studio

A one-bedroom flat or studio in Medway — including the many conversion apartments in Rochester's historic core and Chatham's waterfront developments — typically takes between two and four hours for a professional two-person team to load, drive to the new property and unload. This assumes all items are packed and ready to load. Well-organised moves with minimal furniture and boxes at the lower end, moves involving a higher floor with no lift at the upper end.

Two-bedroom property

A typical two-bedroom house or flat in Medway — the most common property type across ME1 to ME8 — takes between three and six hours for a professional team. A well-organised, well-packed two-bedroom property with good access at both ends can be completed in three to four hours. Add access challenges, higher floors, larger volumes or packing on the day and the time moves toward six hours.

Three-bedroom house

The three-bedroom family home is the most common move we carry out in Medway and Kent. A typical three-bedroom house takes between five and eight hours. The variation is significant — a minimalist three-bedroom with organised boxes and good access moves faster than a family home with a full loft, a large shed, a garage and fifteen years of belongings. Our pre-move survey allows us to give you an accurate time estimate for your specific property.

Four or five-bedroom house

Larger family homes in Medway and Kent — particularly in the more spacious properties of Maidstone's Bearsted and Loose, Tonbridge and Tunbridge Wells — take between seven and twelve hours, often requiring two vehicles and a larger crew. For very large homes we may recommend a two-day move, with the first day dedicated to loading and the second to unloading and placement at the new property.

What adds time to a Medway or Kent removal

  • Unpacked items on moving day — the single biggest cause of delays. Our professional packing service the day before eliminates this completely.
  • Access challenges — narrow streets in Rochester's historic core and Chatham's Victorian terraces, high floors with no lift in conversion properties, restricted vehicle access in rural Kent villages.
  • Specialist items — pianos, large safes, American-style fridge freezers, heavy gym equipment all add significant time and require additional crew members.
  • Distance between properties — a move from Rochester to Folkestone or Ashford involves a longer drive than a local Medway move. The drive time is additional to the loading and unloading time.
  • Completion day delays — if keys are not released until 2pm or later, the move cannot begin at the delivery property until access is available. This is outside our control but worth planning around.
  • Traffic — the A2 through Gravesend and the M20 around Maidstone can be congested at peak times. We always plan routes and timing to minimise traffic impact.

How we plan your move timing

Every Medway & Kent Removals booking begins with a pre-move survey of your property. This survey — in person or by video call — allows us to assess the volume of your belongings, identify any access challenges at both properties and give you an accurate time estimate for your specific move. We then build your moving day plan around this estimate, including the recommended start time to ensure completion before the end of the day.

Confirm your key release time with your solicitor before planning the day

On completion day, keys are only released once funds have been received by your solicitor — typically between 11am and 2pm. Confirm the expected time in advance and plan your moving day around it. A move that cannot start at the delivery end until 2pm needs a larger crew or an earlier start at the collection end.
