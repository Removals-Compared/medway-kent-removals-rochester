How to Move House When You Have a Lot of Stuff

Published by Medway Kent Removals | September 2026

Moving a three-bedroom house is manageable. Moving a five-bedroom house that has been lived in for twenty years is a different challenge entirely. If you are facing a large move in Kent or Medway, here is how to approach it without being overwhelmed.

Start the declutter 8 weeks out

The single most effective thing you can do before a large move is reduce the volume. Go room by room, starting with the areas you use least - loft, garage, spare bedroom. Four categories: keep, donate, sell, dispose. Do not move things you do not need into a new house.

Book a full packing service

For a large property, attempting to pack yourself in the weeks before moving day while still living in the house is exhausting and usually results in a chaotic final 48 hours. A professional packing team can pack a five-bedroom house in a full day. The cost is worth it for the reduction in stress alone.

Book storage for overflow items

Large moves often involve a transition period where not everything can go straight to the new property. Storage during the move gives you breathing space to make decisions about furniture and belongings without the pressure of moving day.

Two-vehicle moves

Large properties often require more than one removal vehicle. At Medway Kent Removals we assess the volume of your belongings during the pre-move survey and allocate the right fleet size. A properly resourced large move is actually less stressful than an undersized one scrambling to fit everything in.
