Moving House in Maidstone: A Complete Guide

Published 27 May 2025  |  9 min read  |  Medway & Kent Removals

Maidstone is Kent's county town — a busy, well-connected market town set on the River Medway with a strong retail and restaurant offer, good schools, reasonable London commute times and a wide range of property types that attract everyone from first-time buyers to growing families and downsizers. This guide covers everything you need to know about moving house in Maidstone.

Maidstone neighbourhoods

Maidstone Town Centre and the Riverside

The ME14 and ME15 postcodes cover central Maidstone and the riverside area. The town centre has seen significant regeneration with the £50m Maidstone East development bringing new retail, leisure and residential properties to the heart of the town. The riverside offers a pleasant setting for walks and cycling along the Medway. Properties in the central area range from modern apartments to Victorian and Edwardian terraces on the streets leading up from the river.

Bearsted and Thurnham

To the east of Maidstone, the villages of Bearsted and Thurnham offer a semi-rural setting with excellent access to the North Downs. Bearsted has its own railway station on the Southeastern line to London Victoria. Properties here are predominantly larger family homes — detached and semi-detached — in well-maintained residential streets. One of the most popular areas for families relocating to Maidstone from London.

Loose and Boughton Monchelsea

To the south of Maidstone, the villages of Loose and Boughton Monchelsea offer an attractive alternative for those who want countryside access without leaving the Maidstone area. Loose in particular has a beautiful village setting with a stream running through the centre, period properties and outstanding primary school. Property prices are at a premium for the Maidstone area but still excellent value by South East standards.

Shepway and Senacre

On the southern outskirts of Maidstone, Shepway and Senacre are more affordable residential areas popular with first-time buyers and families. Good primary schools and easy access to the town centre and the M20 motorway at junction 8.

Property in Maidstone

Maidstone offers a wide range of property at prices that represent good value for a Kent county town. The average property price in ME14 and ME15 is approximately £290,000 to £340,000. A two-bedroom terraced house in a typical Maidstone street costs between £210,000 and £270,000. A four-bedroom detached house in Bearsted or Loose costs between £450,000 and £650,000. The most affordable properties are in the Shepway and Senacre areas.

Commuting from Maidstone

Maidstone has two main railway stations — Maidstone East and Maidstone West — with Southeastern services to London Victoria taking approximately 65 to 75 minutes. The M20 motorway runs south of the town providing fast road access to the M25, Dartford Crossing and London. Junction 7 of the M20 is the main Maidstone junction. The M2 at junction 3 is accessible via the A229, connecting to Rochester, Chatham and the Dartford Crossing.

Schools in Maidstone

Maidstone has outstanding grammar school provision. Maidstone Grammar School for Boys and Invicta Grammar School for Girls are both highly regarded selective state schools that consistently achieve strong results. Valley Park School and Cornwallis Academy are the main comprehensive options. The Maidstone area has numerous good primary schools, several of which are rated Outstanding by Ofsted.

Maidstone's town centre and leisure offer

Maidstone has one of the strongest town centre retail offers in Kent with the Fremlin Walk and Maidstone Shopping Centre providing major brands alongside a good independent sector. The Museum of Kent Life on the edge of the town is one of the most visited attractions in the county. The Hazlitt Theatre provides a regional arts and entertainment programme. The River Medway offers a pleasant waterfront setting and Mote Park — a large country park within easy walking distance of the town centre — provides outstanding open space for families.

Moving day in Maidstone — what to expect

Maidstone's town centre can be congested during peak hours and the roads around the M20 junction can be slow at morning and evening rush hours. For moves within Maidstone or between Maidstone and Medway, we recommend starting early to avoid peak traffic on the A229 and M20 approaches. Parking in central Maidstone is managed and some streets have controlled parking zones requiring advance planning for removal vehicles.

The villages to the east and south of Maidstone — Bearsted, Loose, Boughton Monchelsea — have narrow lanes in some areas that require careful vehicle selection. Medway & Kent Removals surveys access at both properties before every move to ensure the right vehicle is selected for the specific locations involved.

Maidstone to Rochester — a popular local move

Many families move between Maidstone and Rochester and Chatham as their circumstances change. Medway & Kent Removals carries out this route regularly and knows it thoroughly. A typical Maidstone to Rochester or Chatham move takes between four and seven hours depending on volume.
