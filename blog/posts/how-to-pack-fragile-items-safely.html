Packing

How to pack fragile items safely for a house move in Kent

📅 1 May 2026 🕐 6 min read 👥 Medway & Kent Removals Team

Moving house can be one of life's most stressful experiences, particularly when you're faced with packing your most treasured and fragile possessions. Whether you're relocating from Rochester to Canterbury, moving within the Medway towns, or heading to any corner of Kent, protecting your delicate items during transit requires careful planning, the right materials, and proven techniques. At Medway & Kent Removals, we've helped countless families across Kent safely transport their precious belongings, from antique china collections in Chatham to crystal glassware in Maidstone. This comprehensive guide will walk you through everything you need to know about packing fragile items safely, ensuring your valuables arrive at your new home in perfect condition.

Understanding What Makes Items Fragile During a Move

Before diving into packing techniques, it's essential to understand what makes certain items vulnerable during transportation. Fragile items are susceptible to damage from vibration, impact, temperature changes, and pressure. During a typical house move in Kent, your belongings will experience various stresses as they travel along motorways like the M2 and M20, navigate local roads through towns like Sittingbourne and Faversham, and are loaded and unloaded from removal vehicles.

Common fragile items that require special attention include glassware, china and ceramics, mirrors, artwork, electronics, musical instruments, ornaments, and antiques. Each category presents unique challenges and requires specific packing approaches to ensure safe transportation.

Essential Packing Materials for Fragile Items

Successful fragile item packing starts with having the right materials at hand. Quality packing supplies are an investment in protecting your valuables, and skimping on materials often leads to costly damage.

Protective Wrapping Materials

Bubble wrap is perhaps the most versatile protective material for fragile items. Choose medium-sized bubbles for most items, as they provide excellent cushioning without taking up excessive space. For extremely delicate pieces like fine crystal or porcelain figurines, small bubble wrap offers more precise protection around intricate details.

Packing paper, also known as newsprint, is invaluable for wrapping items before applying bubble wrap. Unlike newspaper, packing paper won't leave ink stains on your belongings. It's particularly useful for filling hollow items like vases and providing the first layer of protection for glassware.

Foam sheets and foam corner protectors offer excellent protection for flat items like mirrors and picture frames. The density of foam provides superior shock absorption, making it ideal for items that might be subject to impact during the loading process.

Appropriate Box Selection

Choosing the right boxes is crucial for fragile item protection. Small to medium-sized boxes are generally best for fragile items, as they're easier to handle and less likely to become too heavy. Dish packs, which are specially designed boxes with double-thick walls, are excellent for china and glassware collections.

For particularly valuable or oddly-shaped items, custom crating might be necessary. Many removal companies serving the Kent area, including areas around Gravesend and Dartford, can arrange professional crating services for items like large mirrors or valuable artwork.

Step-by-Step Guide to Packing Different Types of Fragile Items

Packing China and Ceramics

China and ceramic items require careful individual wrapping and strategic box placement. Start by lining your box with several layers of packing paper or bubble wrap to create a cushioned base. For plates, wrap each one individually in packing paper, then bubble wrap, ensuring complete coverage. Pack plates vertically rather than flat, as this position distributes weight more evenly and reduces the risk of breakage.

When packing cups and mugs, stuff the hollow areas with packing paper to prevent crushing, then wrap the entire item. For items with handles, like teacups or serving pieces, pay special attention to these vulnerable protrusions by adding extra padding around them.

Bowls should be wrapped individually and can be nested carefully if they're similar sizes, but always place padding between nested pieces. Fill any remaining space in the box with packing material to prevent items from shifting during transport.

Glassware and Crystal Protection

Glassware requires even more meticulous care than china due to its increased fragility. Begin by gathering all your glassware and assessing each piece for existing chips or cracks, as these weak points are likely failure spots during a move.

For drinking glasses, stuff the interior with packing paper, then wrap the entire glass in paper followed by bubble wrap. Pay particular attention to the rim and base, as these are the most vulnerable areas. Wine glasses and champagne flutes need extra care around their stems - consider using specialty glass dividers or individual glass boxes for particularly valuable pieces.

Crystal items, being more valuable and often more delicate than regular glassware, may warrant individual boxes for each piece. When packing crystal serving pieces or decorative items, use generous amounts of bubble wrap and ensure the packed item cannot move within its container.

Electronics and Appliances

Modern homes contain numerous electronic devices that require careful packing. If you still have original packaging for items like televisions, computers, or stereo equipment, use it - manufacturer packaging is designed specifically for safe transport.

For electronics without original packaging, anti-static bubble wrap is essential to prevent damage from static electricity. Remove all cables and accessories, packing them separately in clearly labeled bags. Screen protectors or cardboard can shield television and computer screens from scratches.

Small appliances like coffee makers or blenders should have their removable parts packed separately. Fill hollow spaces with packing material and ensure the main unit is well-cushioned within its box.

Artwork and Mirrors

Artwork and mirrors present unique challenges due to their size, weight, and the vulnerability of glass or canvas surfaces. For framed artwork, start by creating an 'X' pattern across the glass with masking tape to prevent shattering if breakage occurs. Wrap the entire piece in packing paper, then bubble wrap, paying special attention to corners and edges.

Mirror packing requires similar techniques but often benefits from additional cardboard protection. Specialty mirror boxes, available from packing supply stores throughout Kent, provide excellent protection for standard-sized mirrors.

Large artwork or mirrors may require professional packing services. Companies experienced in Kent removals understand the specific challenges of transporting such items through areas like Canterbury's narrow historic streets or Rochester's older residential areas.

Advanced Packing Techniques for Maximum Protection

The Double-Box Method

For extremely valuable or fragile items, the double-box method provides superior protection. Pack your item normally in an appropriately sized box, then place this box inside a larger box with padding material filling all empty space. This technique provides double protection and additional shock absorption.

Creating Compartments

For collections of similar items, creating compartments within boxes prevents pieces from knocking against each other. Use cardboard dividers or construct your own using spare cardboard and tape. This technique is particularly effective for glassware collections or sets of china.

Weight Distribution and Box Balancing

Proper weight distribution within boxes and across your move is crucial for safety and protection. Heavy items should go in small boxes to keep them manageable, while ensuring that fragile items aren't crushed under excessive weight. When loading removal vehicles, fragile boxes should be placed in secure positions where they won't shift during transport through Kent's varied terrain.

Common Packing Mistakes to Avoid

Many well-intentioned homeowners make costly mistakes when packing fragile items. Using newspaper directly against items can result in ink transfer, particularly problematic with light-colored china or fabric items. Overpacking boxes makes them difficult to handle safely and increases the risk of dropping, while underpacking allows items to shift and collide during transport.

Another common error is neglecting to fill empty spaces within boxes. Any void space allows packed items to move during transport, potentially causing damage. Similarly, using old or damaged boxes compromises protection - boxes should be sturdy enough to protect their contents throughout the moving process.

Failing to label boxes clearly as fragile is another oversight that can lead to rough handling. Clear, visible labeling ensures that everyone involved in your move understands which boxes require extra care.

Special Considerations for Kent Moves

Moving within Kent presents specific considerations that can impact how you pack fragile items. The county's mix of historic towns, modern developments, and rural areas means your belongings might travel on everything from motorways to narrow country lanes. Properties in areas like Rochester's historic core or Canterbury's medieval streets might have challenging access, requiring extra care in packing to accommodate potential additional handling.

Kent's weather can also be a factor, particularly during winter months or wet periods. While professional removal companies use covered vehicles, additional moisture protection might be wise for items sensitive to humidity, such as musical instruments or certain antiques.

The distance of your move within Kent affects packing decisions too. A local move within the Medway towns might allow for multiple trips with particularly fragile items transported separately, while a move from one end of Kent to the other requires everything to be packed for a longer journey.

When to Consider Professional Packing Services

While many homeowners successfully pack their own fragile items, certain situations warrant professional packing services. If you have extensive collections of valuable china, crystal, or artwork, professional packers bring specialized materials and techniques that ensure maximum protection.

Time constraints often make professional packing worthwhile. Properly packing fragile items is time-consuming, and if you're managing a move while working or caring for family, professional services can relieve significant stress.

Insurance considerations also play a role. Many removal companies offer enhanced coverage for items they pack themselves, providing additional peace of mind for valuable belongings.

Final Preparations and Moving Day

As moving day approaches, final preparations ensure your carefully packed fragile items receive proper treatment. Create a detailed inventory of packed fragile items, noting their condition before packing. This documentation proves valuable for insurance purposes and helps track items during the move.

On moving day, conduct a brief orientation with your moving team, pointing out which boxes contain fragile items and any special handling requirements. Professional removal companies serving Kent are experienced in handling delicate items, but clear communication ensures everyone understands your priorities.

Consider transporting the most valuable or irreplaceable fragile items yourself if practical. Items with significant sentimental value or extreme fragility might travel more safely in your personal vehicle, where you have complete control over their handling.

Unpacking and Settling In

The packing process doesn't end when you arrive at your new home. Careful unpacking is equally important to ensure your fragile items emerge undamaged. Unpack fragile items promptly to prevent boxes from becoming damaged by moisture or accidents, and inspect each item as you unpack it, documenting any damage immediately.

Set up display areas or storage locations before unpacking fragile items completely. Having designated spaces ready prevents items from sitting exposed while you organize your new home.

Moving house across Kent doesn't have to mean anxiety about your precious belongings. With proper packing techniques, quality materials, and careful planning, your fragile items can arrive safely at your new home, ready to help you settle into the next chapter of your life. Whether you're moving locally within the Medway area or relocating across the county, taking time to pack fragile items correctly is an investment in preserving both their monetary and sentimental value.
