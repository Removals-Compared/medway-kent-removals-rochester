Moving to Gravesend — an honest guide to living in North Kent

📅 8 May 2026 🕐 6 min read 👥 Medway & Kent Removals Team

Moving to Gravesend offers an exciting opportunity to settle in one of North Kent's most characterful towns. With its rich maritime heritage, excellent transport links to London, and growing reputation as a desirable place to live, Gravesend has become increasingly popular with families, young professionals, and retirees alike. This comprehensive guide explores everything you need to know about living in Gravesend, from its bustling town centre to the emerging Ebbsfleet Garden City development, helping you make an informed decision about your potential relocation to this historic Kent town.

Why Choose Gravesend for Your New Home?

Gravesend's appeal lies in its perfect balance of historical charm and modern convenience. Located on the south bank of the River Thames, this North Kent town offers residents the best of both worlds: a strong sense of community rooted in centuries of history, combined with excellent connectivity to London and the wider Kent region. The town's strategic position makes it particularly attractive for commuters, while its affordable housing market compared to London suburbs makes it an excellent choice for first-time buyers and growing families.

The town has undergone significant regeneration in recent years, with new developments, improved infrastructure, and enhanced shopping facilities breathing new life into the area. Living in Gravesend means enjoying riverside walks along the Thames Path, exploring the historic town centre, and having easy access to the beautiful Kent countryside, including the nearby Shorne Woods Country Park and the North Downs.

Transport Links and Commuter Benefits

One of Gravesend's strongest selling points is its exceptional transport connectivity. The town is served by two railway stations: Gravesend station offers direct services to London Victoria, Charing Cross, and Cannon Street, with journey times of approximately 30-40 minutes during peak hours. This makes it an ideal location for London commuters seeking more affordable housing without sacrificing convenience.

For those working in the Medway area, including Rochester, Chatham, and Gillingham, the road connections via the A2 provide straightforward access across North Kent. The M25 is easily accessible, opening up routes to Gatwick Airport, Surrey, and Essex. The developing Ebbsfleet International station, just a short distance away, offers high-speed rail services to London St Pancras in just 17 minutes, as well as international Eurostar connections to Paris and Brussels.

Understanding Gravesend's Neighbourhoods

Moving to Gravesend means choosing from a variety of distinct neighbourhoods, each with its own character and advantages. Understanding these different areas is crucial when planning your relocation, as each offers different lifestyle benefits and property types.

Town Centre Living

The heart of Gravesend offers urban living with a small-town feel. The town centre has been revitalised in recent years, with the Thamesgate Shopping Centre providing modern retail facilities alongside traditional high street shops. Living in the town centre means walking distance to amenities, restaurants, and the historic Gravesend Pier. Properties here range from converted Victorian buildings to modern apartment developments, making it popular with young professionals and those seeking low-maintenance living.

Riverside and Waterfront Areas

The areas closest to the Thames offer some of Gravesend's most desirable properties, with many homes enjoying river views. The Promenade area has seen significant development, with new apartment blocks and renovated period properties attracting residents who want to live close to the water. These areas are perfect for those who enjoy riverside walks and water-based activities, with the Thames Path providing scenic routes both east and west along the river.

Ebbsfleet Garden City

For those considering Ebbsfleet Garden City relocation, this developing area represents one of the UK's most ambitious new town projects. Located between Gravesend and Dartford, Ebbsfleet offers brand-new homes, modern amenities, and excellent transport links. The development is designed with sustainability and community in mind, featuring green spaces, cycling paths, and energy-efficient housing. This area is particularly attractive to young families and professionals who want modern living with excellent connectivity to both London and the Kent countryside.

Suburban Gravesend

The residential areas surrounding the town centre offer excellent value family housing, with tree-lined streets, good schools, and plenty of green space. Areas like Pelham, Singlewell, and Istead Rise provide a more suburban feel while maintaining good access to the town centre and transport links. These neighbourhoods are particularly popular with families moving from London or other parts of Kent who want more space and better value for money.

Housing Market and Property Types

The Gravesend property market offers excellent value compared to London and many other parts of Kent. Moving to Gravesend typically means getting more space for your money, whether you're looking for a first home, family house, or investment property. The area offers a good mix of property types, from Victorian terraces and Edwardian semis to modern apartments and new-build developments.

Property Values and Trends

House prices in Gravesend remain significantly more affordable than London, while offering excellent growth potential due to the ongoing regeneration and improved transport links. The proximity to Ebbsfleet International and the continued development of the Garden City have positively influenced property values across the area. First-time buyers particularly benefit from the range of affordable starter homes and the government's Help to Buy schemes available on new developments.

The rental market is also strong, supported by commuters and workers in the growing logistics and business sectors around Ebbsfleet and the Thames Gateway. This makes Gravesend attractive to property investors as well as owner-occupiers.

Schools and Education

Families moving to Gravesend will find a good selection of educational options. The area is served by several well-regarded primary schools, including Thong Church of England Primary School and Westcourt Primary School, both of which receive positive Ofsted ratings. For secondary education, Gravesend Grammar School is a selective school with an excellent academic reputation, while The Hundred of Hoo Academy provides comprehensive education with strong community links.

The proximity to Rochester and the wider Medway area also opens up additional educational opportunities, including the prestigious Rochester Grammar Schools and Medway's various college and university options. For families considering Ebbsfleet Garden City relocation, new schools are planned as part of the development, ensuring educational provision grows with the community.

Shopping, Dining, and Entertainment

Living in Gravesend means enjoying a revitalised town centre with improving shopping and dining options. The Thamesgate Shopping Centre anchors the retail offering, while the historic high street maintains its character with independent shops, cafes, and restaurants. The town's multicultural population has contributed to an excellent variety of international cuisine, from traditional British pubs to authentic Indian, Turkish, and Eastern European restaurants.

Cultural Attractions and Activities

Gravesend's rich history is reflected in its cultural attractions. The town is famous for its connection to Pocahontas, who is buried in St George's Church, and this heritage is celebrated at the Pocahontas statue near the riverfront. The Woodville Theatre provides regular entertainment, from amateur dramatics to professional touring shows, while the nearby Cyclopark offers world-class cycling facilities for sports enthusiasts.

The riverside location provides numerous recreational opportunities, with river cruises, fishing, and water sports all available. The close proximity to the Kent countryside means excellent walking and cycling opportunities, while being just a short drive from Rochester's historic castle and cathedral adds cultural richness to the area.

Healthcare and Essential Services

Moving to Gravesend provides access to good healthcare facilities through several GP practices and the Darent Valley Hospital in nearby Dartford. The hospital provides comprehensive healthcare services including emergency care, maternity services, and specialist treatments. Several dental practices serve the area, and pharmacy services are readily available throughout the town centre and residential areas.

Essential services are well-established in Gravesend, with reliable waste collection, good postal services, and comprehensive utility coverage. The town's infrastructure continues to improve as part of ongoing regeneration efforts, with new developments in the Ebbsfleet area setting high standards for modern amenities and services.

Community and Lifestyle

Living in Gravesend means becoming part of a diverse and welcoming community. The town has a strong multicultural heritage, reflected in its festivals, food, and community events. The annual Gravesend Borough Market and various seasonal celebrations bring residents together and create a strong sense of community spirit.

For families, there are numerous parks and recreational facilities, including Windmill Hill, which offers panoramic views across the Thames and excellent walking opportunities. The town's compact size means most amenities are within walking or cycling distance, promoting a more sustainable and community-focused lifestyle.

The Future of Gravesend

The ongoing development of Ebbsfleet Garden City and continued investment in transport infrastructure suggest a bright future for the area. The government's commitment to the Thames Gateway regeneration means continued improvements to housing, employment opportunities, and community facilities. For those considering Ebbsfleet Garden City relocation, the long-term vision includes substantial job creation, improved shopping and leisure facilities, and enhanced green spaces.

The proximity to London while maintaining Kent's characteristic charm makes Gravesend increasingly attractive to a diverse range of residents. Whether you're a first-time buyer, growing family, or looking to downsize, the area offers options that are likely to appreciate in value while providing an excellent quality of life.

Planning Your Move to Gravesend

When planning your relocation to this attractive North Kent town, it's important to work with local professionals who understand the area thoroughly. From finding the right neighbourhood to suit your lifestyle to managing the logistics of your move, local expertise makes all the difference in ensuring a smooth transition to your new home.

Consider visiting different areas of Gravesend at various times of day to get a true feel for the community and transport links. Explore the growing Ebbsfleet development if you're interested in modern living, or investigate the characterful older areas if you prefer period properties with historical charm. The excellent road connections to Rochester, Medway, and the rest of Kent mean you can easily explore the wider area before making your final decision.
