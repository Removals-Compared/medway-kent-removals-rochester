How to Choose a Removal Company in Kent: The Complete Guide

Published 3 Jun 2025  |  8 min read  |  Medway & Kent Removals

Choosing the right removal company in Kent is one of the most important decisions you make during a house move. The removal company you choose will have complete physical control of everything you own for several hours — your furniture, your electronics, your irreplaceable personal items. Getting this decision right matters enormously.

This guide covers exactly what to look for when choosing a removal company in Kent — from insurance to local knowledge, fixed pricing to reviews — and gives you the specific questions to ask before committing to any booking.

Why local knowledge matters in Kent

Kent's diversity as a county creates genuine logistical complexity for removal companies. The narrow medieval streets of Canterbury and Rochester are completely different from the rural lanes of the Weald. The coastal properties of Whitstable, Herne Bay and Folkestone present different access challenges to the suburban estates of Maidstone and Tonbridge. A removal company with genuine local knowledge of the specific towns and postcodes involved in your move will anticipate and avoid problems that a company unfamiliar with the area would encounter on moving day.

The 10 essential checks

1. Goods-in-transit insurance

Non-negotiable. Ask to see the insurance certificate — not just a verbal confirmation. The policy must cover the full replacement value of your belongings and must be current. Public liability insurance is also essential. Without both, you have no meaningful protection if something goes wrong.

2. Fixed price vs hourly rate

Always insist on a fixed price. Hourly rates can increase significantly if the move takes longer than anticipated due to access issues, traffic delays or higher volumes than expected. A fixed price based on a proper survey means you know your total cost before moving day.

3. Pre-move survey

Any professional removal company should survey your home before providing a quote — in person or via video call. A quote provided without a survey is a guess. That guess may be intentionally low to win your booking with additional charges added on the day, or it may be inflated to cover the risk of unknowns.

4. Their own staff and vehicles

Ask directly — will the crew on moving day be your employed staff in your own vehicles? Some removal companies subcontract during busy periods to agencies or self-employed drivers. This affects consistency of quality, insurance coverage and accountability.

5. Google reviews — local and specific

Check Google reviews for reviews from Kent customers specifically — not generic positive reviews that could have been written anywhere. Look at the dates — a spread of reviews over an extended period indicates a genuine ongoing business. Read negative reviews carefully and check how the company responded.

6. BAR membership

The British Association of Removers (BAR) is the primary trade body for the UK removal industry. BAR members must meet minimum standards for insurance, training and professional conduct. While BAR membership is not the only indicator of quality, its presence is a positive signal and its absence is worth noting.

7. Cancellation and postponement policy

Completion dates in Kent's active property market can and do change at short notice. Your removal company must have a clear and fair written policy for postponements and cancellations. Ask specifically what notice is required to reschedule without penalty.

8. Packing service availability

A full-service removal company should offer professional packing in addition to the move itself. Even if you plan to pack your own boxes, check that the company can supply quality packing materials and can assist with specialist items such as fragile antiques, mirrors, artwork or pianos.

9. Claims process for damage

Ask directly what happens if something is damaged during your move. A professional company explains the claims process clearly, confirms the insurance excess and gives you confidence that any claim would be handled promptly. Vague or evasive answers are a significant red flag.

10. Written confirmation of everything

Your quotation, booking confirmation, moving date, price, services included and payment terms must all be confirmed in writing before you pay a deposit. A verbal booking or a confirmation by text message alone is not sufficient.

Get three quotes before deciding

Always get at least three fixed-price quotes from different removal companies for any significant move. Compare the total price, what is included, the insurance coverage and the company's local reputation — not just the headline number.

Specific questions for Kent moves

Beyond the general checks, these questions are specifically relevant for moves within Kent:

  • Do you have experience moving properties in [your specific town]?
  • What size vehicle would you use for my move and why?
  • Do you know the parking restriction situation for my street?
  • Have you moved properties with similar access challenges before?
  • Are you familiar with Medway Council's parking suspension process?

A genuinely local Kent removal company should answer all of these questions immediately and confidently. Hesitation or vague answers suggest the company is not as locally experienced as they might claim.
