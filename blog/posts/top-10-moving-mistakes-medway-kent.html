Moving Tips

Top 10 mistakes people make when moving house in Medway and Kent

📅 21 May 2026 🕐 6 min read 👥 Medway & Kent Removals Team

Moving house is one of life's most stressful experiences, and when you're relocating within Medway and Kent, there are specific challenges that can catch even the most organised people off guard. After helping countless families move across Rochester, Chatham, Gillingham, and surrounding areas, we've seen the same mistakes repeated time and again. The good news is that most moving disasters are entirely preventable with proper planning and awareness. Here are the ten most common moving house mistakes we encounter in Kent and Medway, along with practical advice on how to avoid them.

1. Starting the Moving Process Too Late

The biggest mistake we see in Rochester and surrounding Medway towns is people underestimating how long the moving process actually takes. Many residents think they can organise everything in a week or two, but this approach leads to rushed decisions, higher costs, and unnecessary stress.

Why This Happens in Kent

Kent's property market moves quickly, especially in desirable areas like Rochester's historic quarter or the newer developments around Chatham Maritime. When a house sale completes faster than expected, families often find themselves scrambling to arrange everything at the last minute.

The Real Timeline You Need

Start planning your move at least 8-10 weeks before your moving date. This gives you time to research removal companies, declutter properly, and handle all the administrative tasks without panic. In Medway, where parking restrictions and narrow Victorian streets are common, early planning becomes even more critical.

Begin by creating a moving checklist that includes booking your removal company, arranging time off work, sorting school transfers if you have children, and notifying utility companies. The earlier you start, the more choice you'll have with removal dates and the better rates you're likely to secure.

2. Failing to Declutter Before Packing

One of the costliest mistakes Medway residents make is moving everything they own without considering what they actually need. This not only increases your removal costs but also makes unpacking in your new home overwhelming.

The True Cost of Moving Everything

Removal companies charge based on volume and weight. If you're moving from a typical three-bedroom Victorian terrace in Rochester to a modern home in Rainham, you might discover that not everything will fit comfortably. Moving items you don't need wastes money and creates clutter in your new space.

A Practical Decluttering Strategy

Start decluttering room by room at least six weeks before your move. Create three piles: keep, donate, and discard. For items you're unsure about, apply the one-year rule. If you haven't used something in the past year, you probably won't miss it.

Consider donating good-quality items to local Kent charities or selling valuable pieces online. Many Medway residents successfully use local Facebook groups or car boot sales to clear unwanted items while making some extra money toward moving costs.

3. Not Researching Removal Companies Thoroughly

Choosing a removal company based solely on price is a mistake that can cost you dearly. In Kent and Medway, there are many removal services available, but quality and reliability vary significantly.

What to Look for in a Medway Removal Company

Always verify that your chosen removal company has proper insurance and is a member of professional trade associations. Check their Google reviews, but more importantly, ask for recent local references from moves in your area. A company familiar with Medway's streets will know about potential access issues, parking restrictions, and the best routes to avoid traffic delays.

Ask detailed questions about their packing materials, team size, and what happens if something goes wrong on moving day. A reputable company will provide clear, written estimates and be transparent about their terms and conditions.

Red Flags to Avoid

Be wary of quotes that seem too good to be true, companies that demand large upfront payments, or those unwilling to provide insurance details. Door-to-door sales approaches are another warning sign. Legitimate removal companies don't typically cold-call or knock on doors looking for business.

4. Inadequate Packing and Labelling

Poor packing is responsible for most damage during moves, and inadequate labelling creates chaos when you're trying to settle into your new Kent home.

Common Packing Mistakes

Many people pack boxes too heavy, particularly when moving books or kitchen items. A box should never be so heavy that one person can't lift it safely. Overpacking leads to boxes breaking and potential injuries during the move.

Another frequent error is using inappropriate materials. Old cardboard boxes, newspaper for wrapping delicate items, and inadequate protection for furniture all increase the risk of damage. Invest in proper packing materials or ask your removal company about supplying them.

Effective Labelling Systems

Create a labelling system that works for your family. At minimum, each box should show which room it belongs in and a brief description of contents. Consider using different coloured labels for each room to make identification even easier.

Pack a clearly marked "first day" box for each family member containing essential items like toiletries, medications, phone chargers, and a change of clothes. This prevents frantic searching through boxes on your first night in your new Medway home.

5. Ignoring Insurance and Valuation Options

Many Kent residents don't properly understand their insurance coverage during a move, leaving themselves financially vulnerable if something goes wrong.

Understanding Basic Coverage

Standard removal company insurance often provides minimal coverage, typically around £40 per item regardless of actual value. This might cover a dining chair but won't come close to replacing a damaged antique or electronic equipment.

Your home insurance policy might provide some coverage during moves, but this varies significantly between insurers and policies. Don't assume you're covered without checking your specific terms.

Making Informed Insurance Decisions

Consider additional valuation coverage for valuable items. While this increases your moving costs slightly, it provides much better protection for expensive furniture, artwork, or electronics. Make a detailed inventory of valuable items with photographs taken before packing.

For particularly valuable or sentimental items, consider transporting them yourself rather than putting them in the removal van. Important documents, jewellery, and family heirlooms are often better kept with you during the move.

6. Forgetting to Notify Important Parties

One of the most frustrating post-move discoveries is realising you've forgotten to update your address with crucial organisations, leading to missed mail, service interruptions, or worse.

Essential Notifications

Start with the most critical updates: your bank, employer, HMRC, and any benefit providers. Register with a new GP practice if you're moving to a different area of Kent, as waiting lists can be longer than expected, particularly in popular towns like Rochester or Gravesend.

Don't forget about subscription services, online shopping accounts, and loyalty programs. These might seem minor, but they can cause confusion later when deliveries go to your old address or important notifications don't reach you.

Setting Up a Mail Redirection

Royal Mail's redirection service provides a safety net for missed notifications, but it's not foolproof. Set it up for at least six months to catch any organisations you might have forgotten. Use this time to update your address with anyone who contacts you through redirected mail.

7. Poor Planning for Moving Day Logistics

Moving day logistics in Medway and Kent require careful planning due to parking restrictions, narrow streets, and busy traffic patterns, especially around Rochester and Chatham town centres.

Parking and Access Issues

Many Medway streets require parking permits or have restrictions during certain hours. Contact your local council about suspended parking bays if you're moving from or to a street with limited access. This small fee can prevent significant delays and frustration on moving day.

Check whether your removal van can access your property directly. Some Kent roads have weight restrictions, height barriers, or are simply too narrow for large vehicles. Your removal company should assess this beforehand, but it's worth double-checking.

Preparing Your Home

Clear pathways through your home the night before your move. Remove rugs that could cause trips, secure pets in a safe room, and ensure all items are ready for collection. Protect floors and walls, particularly in period properties where doorways might be narrow or floors delicate.

Pack a cleaning kit separately so you can do any necessary cleaning before handing over keys. Many moving contracts require properties to be left in a clean condition.

8. Underestimating Costs and Hidden Expenses

Moving costs always seem to exceed expectations, and many Medway residents find themselves financially stretched by expenses they hadn't anticipated.

Hidden Costs to Budget For

Beyond the obvious removal company fees, consider costs like packing materials, storage if there's a gap between moves, cleaning supplies, takeaway meals on moving day, and potential hotel costs if your move overruns.

If you're moving during the school term, you might need childcare on moving day. Factor in the cost of connecting utilities in your new home, potential deposit requirements, and any immediate repairs or improvements needed.

Creating a Realistic Budget

Add at least 20% to your estimated moving costs to cover unexpected expenses. This might seem excessive, but it provides peace of mind and prevents financial stress during an already challenging time.

Get written quotes from multiple removal companies and compare what's included. The cheapest quote might not include packing materials, insurance, or weekend/evening work, making it more expensive overall.

9. Not Preparing for the First Week

Many people focus entirely on moving day and forget to plan for their first week in their new Kent home. This oversight can turn what should be an exciting time into a stressful struggle.

Essential First Week Planning

Pack a survival kit with everything you'll need for the first few days. This should include basic tools, cleaning supplies, toiletries, medications, phone chargers, and enough clothes for several days. Include some basic food items and coffee or tea, because finding the kettle on day one can be challenging.

Research your new area before moving. Locate the nearest supermarket, pharmacy, petrol station, and takeaway restaurants. If you have children, find the nearest park or playground to help them settle in.

Utility Connections

Arrange utility connections for your moving day or the day before if possible. There's nothing worse than arriving at your new Medway home to find no electricity, gas, or internet connection. This is particularly important during winter months when heating is essential.

Test everything once you move in. Check taps, lights, heating, and any appliances. Report any issues immediately to avoid disputes later about whether damage occurred during your move or was pre-existing.

10. Trying to Do Everything Yourself

The final major mistake we see in Kent and Medway is people attempting to handle too much of their move independently, either to save money or because they want complete control over the process.

When DIY Goes Wrong

While hiring friends with vans might seem cost-effective, amateur moves often result in damaged furniture, injured helpers, or moves that take much longer than anticipated. Professional removal companies have the right equipment, experience, and insurance to handle unexpected situations.

Renting a van and doing everything yourself might save money upfront, but consider the true costs: van rental, fuel, packing materials, potential time off work, and the risk of damage or injury. For most families, professional help represents better value.

Finding the Right Balance

You don't need to hire professionals for everything, but identify tasks where expertise matters most. Professional packers excel at fragile items and efficient space usage. Professional movers have equipment to handle heavy furniture safely.

Consider a hybrid approach where you pack some items yourself but leave fragile or valuable pieces to the professionals. This can provide cost savings while maintaining peace of mind.

Making Your Medway Move Stress-Free

Moving house in Kent and Medway doesn't have to be the stressful ordeal many people experience. By avoiding these common mistakes and planning thoroughly, you can ensure a smooth transition to your new home. Remember that investing in proper planning, quality packing materials, and professional help often saves money in the long run by preventing damage, delays, and costly mistakes.

Every move is unique, particularly in areas like Medway where property types vary from modern developments to Victorian terraces, each with their own challenges. The key is starting early, staying organised, and recognising when professional help will make your life easier.
