Top 10 Moving Mistakes to Avoid in Medway and Kent

Published 19 Aug 2025  |  7 min read  |  Medway & Kent Removals

After completing thousands of house moves across Medway and Kent, our team has seen the same mistakes made repeatedly — mistakes that cause stress, add cost and turn what should be a manageable day into a genuinely difficult one. Here are the 10 most common moving mistakes and exactly how to avoid them.

Mistake 1: Booking too late

Friday moves, end-of-month completions and summer moves book up weeks in advance in Medway and Kent. Calling a removal company the week before your moving date and finding nothing available is a preventable disaster. Book as soon as you have a confirmed completion date — or even before if your exchange date is confirmed.

Mistake 2: Not getting a pre-move survey

Accepting a removal quote provided over the phone without a survey is risky. The company is guessing your volume. If the actual volume is larger, you face additional charges on moving day. Always insist on a pre-move survey before a price is agreed.

Mistake 3: Underestimating how long packing takes

A three-bedroom family home in Medway takes most people significantly longer to pack than expected. Starting packing the week before moving day inevitably results in panic, poor decisions and incomplete packing on moving day morning. Start six to eight weeks before the move.

Mistake 4: Not labelling boxes properly

Boxes labelled only with the room name — "kitchen", "bedroom" — slow down unloading significantly. Label with the contents too: "kitchen — crockery — FRAGILE". And label on the side, not the top.

Mistake 5: Forgetting the parking suspension

In controlled parking areas of Rochester, Chatham, Gillingham and other Medway town centres, a removal vehicle cannot park legally without a formal suspension from Medway Council. Apply with five working days minimum notice. Medway and Kent Removals manages this for you.

Mistake 6: Not decluttering before moving

Moving items you do not want or need adds time and cost to the removal and clutters the new property from day one. A thorough declutter before the move reduces volume, reduces cost and makes the new property feel fresh and organised from the moment you arrive.

Mistake 7: Packing essentials in the van

Phone chargers, medications, important documents, toilet paper and the kettle ending up buried in the removal van means a frantic search at the end of an exhausting day. Pack an essentials box and keep it in your car, not on the van.

Mistake 8: Not taking meter readings

Failing to photograph meter readings at both the old and new properties on moving day leads to billing disputes with energy suppliers that can take months to resolve. Take readings and photographs at both addresses — at the old property before leaving and at the new property before the crew starts unloading.

Mistake 9: Trying to move specialist items without declaring them

A piano, a heavy safe or gym equipment discovered on moving day that was not declared in the survey requires additional crew and equipment that may not be available. Always declare every specialist item when requesting your quote.

Mistake 10: Not checking insurance

Assuming your removal company is insured without verifying it is a genuine risk. Ask to see the insurance certificate before booking. Check the coverage level is sufficient for the value of your belongings. Do not assume.

Share this list with anyone helping you move

The most common mistakes are made by the people organising the move, not the removal company. Share this list with your partner, family members or anyone helping with planning so everyone avoids the same pitfalls.
