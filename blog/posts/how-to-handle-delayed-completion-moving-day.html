How to handle a delayed completion on moving day in Kent

📅 18 May 2026 🕐 6 min read 👥 Medway & Kent Removals Team

Moving day should be straightforward. You've packed your belongings, booked your removal company, and everything is set for your new start in Kent. But then your solicitor calls with news no homeowner wants to hear: completion has been delayed. As a removal company serving Rochester, Medway and the wider Kent area for over a decade, we've helped countless families handle this stressful situation. Here's everything you need to know about managing a delayed completion and protecting yourself from unnecessary costs and complications.

Understanding Completion Delays in Kent Property Transactions

A completion delay occurs when the legal transfer of property ownership cannot happen on the agreed date. This might sound like a minor administrative hiccup, but when you have removal vans loaded with your entire household and nowhere to unload them, it becomes a significant problem very quickly.

Completion delays affect thousands of property transactions across Kent each year. Whether you're moving from a terraced house in Gillingham to a family home in Maidstone, or relocating from London to a cottage in the Kent countryside, these delays can derail your carefully planned moving day.

Common Causes of Completion Delays

Property chains are often the culprit behind completion delays in Kent. When someone in your chain faces last minute issues, it creates a domino effect that can delay multiple transactions simultaneously. We've seen this happen frequently with moves between popular Kent towns like Chatham, Strood, and Rochester, where active property markets mean longer chains.

Mortgage issues represent another frequent cause of delays. Last minute lending problems, missing documentation, or sudden changes in a buyer's financial circumstances can halt proceedings just hours before completion. This is particularly common during busy periods when mortgage lenders are processing high volumes of applications.

Legal complications can also emerge unexpectedly. Problems with property searches, boundary disputes, or issues with leasehold properties can require additional time to resolve. These problems often surface in areas with older housing stock, which is common throughout historic Kent towns.

Immediate Steps When You Learn About a Delay

Contact Your Removal Company First

The moment you discover your completion has been delayed, contact your removal company immediately. At Medway & Kent Removals, we understand these situations happen, and we're always ready to help find solutions. Many removal companies in Kent have experience with completion delays and can offer flexible arrangements to minimize your stress and costs.

Be honest about the likely delay timeframe. If your solicitor says completion might happen later the same day, this requires different handling than a delay of several days or weeks. The earlier you communicate with your removal team, the more options they can offer.

Assess Your Current Situation

Take stock of where you stand when the delay is announced. Are you already out of your old property? Is the removal van loaded? Have you handed over keys? Your current circumstances will determine which options are available to you.

If you're fortunate enough to learn about the delay before your removal team arrives, you have more flexibility. However, if you discover the delay while your belongings are loaded onto the van, you'll need immediate solutions for storage and accommodation.

Storage Solutions During Completion Delays

Professional Storage Services

Most established removal companies in Kent offer storage solutions for exactly this situation. Your belongings can be safely stored in secure facilities while you wait for completion to proceed. This is often the most practical solution when facing delays of more than 24 hours.

When choosing storage, ensure the facility offers proper security, climate control for sensitive items, and easy access when you need to retrieve your belongings. Many storage facilities in the Medway area and wider Kent provide flexible short term arrangements specifically for property transaction delays.

Temporary Storage Options

For shorter delays, you might be able to arrange temporary storage with family or friends in the local area. If you're moving within Kent, having local connections can be invaluable during these situations. However, remember that furniture and large items require suitable space and may not be practical for temporary arrangements.

Some removal companies can keep your belongings on their vehicles overnight if the delay is expected to be very brief. This option typically costs less than formal storage but is only suitable for delays of 24 hours or less.

Accommodation When You Cannot Access Your New Home

Hotel and B&B Options in Kent

Kent offers numerous accommodation options for displaced homeowners facing completion delays. Rochester, Chatham, and Maidstone have various hotels that can accommodate families on short notice. When booking emergency accommodation, explain your situation as many local establishments understand the challenges of property transactions and may offer special rates.

For families with pets, finding suitable accommodation becomes more challenging. Research pet friendly hotels in Kent before your moving day, so you have options ready if delays occur. Many Premier Inns and independent hotels across Kent welcome pets with advance notice.

Staying with Family and Friends

Local family and friends can provide invaluable support during completion delays. If you have connections in Kent, reach out to them as soon as you learn about the delay. Even if they cannot accommodate your entire family, they might be able to help with children or pets while you arrange alternative solutions.

Managing Costs During Delayed Completion

Understanding Removal Company Charges

Reputable removal companies understand that completion delays are not your fault and will work with you to minimize additional costs. However, there will likely be some extra charges for storage, additional travel, or keeping your booking open beyond the original timeframe.

At Medway & Kent Removals, we always discuss potential delay scenarios during the booking process, so our customers understand what costs might arise. This transparency helps avoid unpleasant surprises during an already stressful situation.

Insurance Considerations

Check what insurance coverage applies to your belongings during storage periods. Most professional removal companies maintain insurance coverage for items in their care, but confirm this covers extended storage periods due to completion delays.

Your home contents insurance may also provide some coverage for temporary storage situations. Contact your insurer to understand what protection you have during the delay period.

Legal and Financial Implications of Delayed Completion

Understanding Your Rights

When completion delays occur, you may have legal recourse depending on the cause of the delay. If the delay results from the seller's actions or negligence, you might be entitled to compensation for additional costs incurred. Discuss this with your solicitor, particularly if you're facing substantial storage and accommodation costs.

Keep detailed records of all additional expenses caused by the delay. This includes removal company charges, storage fees, accommodation costs, and any other expenses directly related to the delayed completion. These records will be essential if you need to seek compensation later.

Protecting Your Financial Position

Completion delays can create unexpected financial pressure, particularly when you're managing mortgage payments on two properties or paying for emergency accommodation. Stay in close contact with your solicitor about when completion is likely to occur and budget for the additional costs accordingly.

If the delay extends beyond a few days, consider discussing options with your mortgage lender. Many lenders understand that completion delays occur and may offer flexibility with payment dates or other arrangements.

Communication Strategies During Delays

Staying Informed

Maintain regular contact with your solicitor throughout the delay period. Request daily updates on progress and realistic timescales for resolution. The more information you have, the better decisions you can make about storage, accommodation, and other arrangements.

Keep your removal company updated on any changes to the expected completion date. Professional companies will work with you to accommodate changes, but they need advance notice to manage their schedules effectively.

Managing Family Stress

Completion delays are particularly stressful for families with children or elderly relatives. Maintain open communication with family members about what's happening and what arrangements are being made. Children especially need reassurance during uncertain periods.

If you have children starting new schools in Kent, contact the schools to explain the situation. Most educational institutions understand that property transactions can face delays and will work with you to manage the transition smoothly.

Preventing Future Completion Delays

Early Planning and Communication

While you cannot prevent all completion delays, good planning can reduce the risk and minimize impact. Choose experienced solicitors with strong track records in Kent property transactions. Local solicitors often have better knowledge of common issues affecting properties in specific areas.

Maintain regular communication with all parties in your property chain throughout the transaction process. Early identification of potential issues gives everyone more time to find solutions before moving day arrives.

Building Flexibility Into Your Moving Plans

When planning your move in Kent, build some flexibility into your timeline if possible. Avoid scheduling completion on Fridays or before bank holidays, when delays can extend into long weekends. Similarly, try not to plan moves immediately before important events or deadlines.

Discuss contingency plans with your removal company during the booking process. Understanding your options before problems arise reduces stress and enables quicker decision making when delays occur.

Working with Professional Removal Companies During Delays

Choosing the Right Removal Partner

Experienced removal companies understand that completion delays happen regularly in the property market. When choosing a removal company for your Kent move, ask about their experience with completion delays and what options they offer when problems arise.

Professional companies will have established relationships with storage facilities, flexible scheduling systems, and clear policies about delay charges. This experience proves invaluable when you need quick solutions during stressful situations.

Clear Communication is Essential

The key to managing completion delays successfully is clear, honest communication between all parties. Your removal company can only help effectively if they understand your situation completely. Share all relevant information about the delay, expected timescales, and any constraints you face.

Professional removal teams have usually handled similar situations many times before and can offer practical solutions you might not have considered. Their experience managing logistics during uncertain situations can be invaluable during completion delays.

Regional Considerations for Kent Moves

Understanding Local Property Markets

Different areas of Kent have varying characteristics that can affect completion delays. Popular commuter towns like Sevenoaks and Tonbridge often have complex property chains involving London properties, which can increase delay risks. Rural areas might face delays related to property searches or access issues.

Understanding these local factors helps set realistic expectations for your move. Experienced local removal companies can provide insights into common issues affecting different areas of Kent.

Seasonal Considerations

Completion delays can be more problematic during certain times of year. Summer moves often involve families trying to relocate before new school terms, creating additional pressure when delays occur. Winter delays might involve weather related complications alongside the completion issues.

Consider seasonal factors when planning your Kent move and discuss these with your removal company. Experienced teams can advise on optimal timing and seasonal considerations for your specific situation.

Recovery and Moving Forward

When Completion Finally Occurs

When your delayed completion finally proceeds, you'll need to coordinate quickly with your removal company to complete the move. Professional companies will have your belongings ready to deliver as soon as you confirm access to your new property.

Take time to inspect your belongings when they're delivered, particularly if they've been in storage for several days. Report any concerns immediately to ensure they're addressed promptly.

Learning from the Experience

While completion delays are stressful experiences, they often provide valuable lessons for future moves. Consider what worked well during the delay management and what you might handle differently next time.

Share feedback with your solicitor and removal company about how the delay was managed. This helps improve services for future customers facing similar situations.

Dealing with a delayed completion on moving day never feels easy, but with the right preparation and professional support, you can manage the situation successfully.
