What to Do If Your Completion Is Delayed on Moving Day

Published 29 Jul 2025  |  6 min read  |  Medway & Kent Removals

A delayed completion on moving day is one of the most stressful situations in the house buying process. You have a removal crew booked and waiting, you need to vacate your old property and the keys to your new one have not been released because funds have not yet transferred between solicitors. It happens more often than many people realise — and knowing what to do in advance makes an enormous difference to how you handle it on the day.

Why completions get delayed

Completion delays on moving day are almost always caused by delays in the transfer of funds between solicitors in the chain. A chain of three or more transactions means several sets of solicitors must transfer funds in sequence — if any link in the chain is delayed, all subsequent completions are delayed. The most common causes include:

  • A solicitor in the chain not releasing funds on time
  • A lender releasing mortgage funds late
  • An administrative error in the chain causing a hold
  • A last-minute query or condition that must be resolved before funds are released

What to do immediately

Call your solicitor as soon as you suspect a delay — do not wait. Ask directly: have funds been received? If not, what is the current position and when do they expect funds to be released? Keep in regular contact throughout the day.

Notify your removal company immediately. Medway and Kent Removals will advise on options depending on the time and the severity of the delay. In most cases, the crew can wait for a reasonable period or we can arrange to return later in the day.

Your options if completion is significantly delayed

  • Wait it out — if the delay is one to three hours, the crew can often wait at the collection address or park nearby and return to complete the delivery when keys are released.
  • Overnight storage — if completion is delayed until the following day, your belongings can be kept securely in our vehicle or storage facility overnight and delivered the following morning.
  • Hotel for the night — if you have vacated your old property and the new one is not accessible, a hotel for the night is sometimes the most practical solution. Keep receipts — you may be able to recover costs from the party responsible for the delay.

Prevention — what you can do in advance

  • Ask your solicitor to confirm funds transfer timing in writing before moving day
  • Request an early morning completion target — funds transferred by 11am rather than 2pm gives far more time to resolve any issues
  • Have a contingency plan agreed with your removal company in advance
  • Ensure your essentials — documents, medications, chargers — travel with you rather than on the van
