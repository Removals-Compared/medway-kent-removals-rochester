Moving to Tonbridge or Tunbridge Wells: A Complete Guide

Published 24 Jun 2025  |  8 min read  |  Medway & Kent Removals

Tonbridge and Royal Tunbridge Wells are two of West Kent's most desirable towns — neighbouring but distinct in character, both offering fast London connections, outstanding schools and access to the beautiful High Weald and North Downs countryside. This guide covers both towns to help you decide which is the right fit for your move.

Tonbridge vs Tunbridge Wells — the key differences

Tonbridge is a busy, practical market town on the River Medway with a strong sporting and community identity. Tunbridge Wells is a more affluent spa town with a more refined independent retail and restaurant scene centred on the famous Pantiles. Tonbridge property is typically 15 to 25% less expensive than equivalent Tunbridge Wells property. Both offer outstanding schools and similar London commute times.

Property in Tonbridge

A three-bedroom semi-detached house in a typical Tonbridge street costs between £380,000 and £480,000. A four-bedroom detached house in the better residential areas costs between £500,000 and £750,000. The most desirable addresses — around the Pembury Road and to the south — command higher premiums.

Property in Tunbridge Wells

Property in Tunbridge Wells commands a premium for the county. A three-bedroom semi-detached costs between £400,000 and £550,000. A four-bedroom detached in Broadwater Down or Nevill Park starts at £700,000 and can exceed £1.5 million. The Pantiles area and historic centre are particularly sought-after.

Commuting from both towns

Both towns have direct Southeastern services to London Bridge and Charing Cross. Tonbridge takes approximately 40 to 50 minutes. Tunbridge Wells takes approximately 50 to 60 minutes. Annual season tickets are approximately £4,000 to £5,000 from both towns.

Schools in Tonbridge and Tunbridge Wells

Both towns have outstanding school provision. Tonbridge School is one of England's finest independent schools. Tonbridge Grammar School for Girls and The Judd School for Boys are excellent selective state options. Tunbridge Wells Grammar School for Boys and Tunbridge Wells Girls Grammar School are both strong. Several outstanding independent prep and senior schools serve the area.

Moving day in West Kent

Both towns present access challenges in some areas — Tonbridge's town centre streets and Tunbridge Wells' historic Pantiles area require careful vehicle selection. The High Weald countryside around both towns has narrow lanes requiring smaller vehicles for rural properties. Medway and Kent Removals surveys all access before every West Kent move.

Choose Tonbridge for value, Tunbridge Wells for lifestyle

If schools are the priority, both towns have equivalent provision. If budget is the primary concern, Tonbridge typically offers more for the money. If the independent restaurant, retail and cultural scene matters most, Tunbridge Wells is the stronger choice.
