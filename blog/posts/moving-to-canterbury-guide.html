Moving to Canterbury — what to expect in Kent's cathedral city

📅 9 May 2026 🕐 6 min read 👥 Medway & Kent Removals Team

Moving to Canterbury represents one of the most exciting opportunities Kent has to offer. As England's spiritual heart and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, this historic cathedral city combines centuries of fascinating history with modern amenities and excellent transport links. Whether you're relocating from nearby Rochester, the wider Medway area, or further afield, Canterbury offers a unique blend of cultural richness, educational excellence, and quality living that makes it one of Kent's most desirable destinations.

Why Choose Canterbury for Your New Home?

Canterbury's appeal extends far beyond its famous cathedral. This thriving city of approximately 55,000 residents offers an exceptional quality of life that attracts families, professionals, students, and retirees alike. The city successfully balances its historical significance with contemporary living, creating an environment where medieval streets house boutique shops, traditional pubs sit alongside trendy cafes, and ancient architecture provides the backdrop for a vibrant modern community.

The presence of the University of Kent and Canterbury Christ Church University brings energy and cultural diversity to the city. This academic influence creates a dynamic atmosphere with regular festivals, exhibitions, and events that enrich the local community. For those moving from Rochester or other Medway towns, Canterbury offers a different pace of life while maintaining easy access to the wider Kent region.

Transport Links and Accessibility

One of Canterbury's greatest advantages is its excellent connectivity. Canterbury West station provides high-speed rail services to London St Pancras International, with journey times of just 56 minutes. Canterbury East offers services to London Victoria and Charing Cross, typically taking around 1 hour and 20 minutes. This makes Canterbury an attractive option for commuters working in London who want to enjoy a better quality of life in Kent.

For those traveling within Kent, regular bus services connect Canterbury to Rochester, Chatham, Maidstone, and other major towns. The A2 and M2 provide excellent road connections, making it easy to reach the Medway towns, with Rochester just 30 minutes away by car.

Neighborhoods and Areas in Canterbury

The City Center

Living in Canterbury's historic center puts you at the heart of everything. The area around the cathedral offers stunning period properties, many of which are listed buildings with unique character features. While city center living comes at a premium, residents enjoy walking access to shops, restaurants, cultural venues, and the cathedral itself. The narrow medieval streets create a intimate community feel despite being in the city center.

Westgate

The Westgate area, stretching west from the city center, offers a mix of Victorian and Edwardian properties alongside newer developments. This area is popular with families due to its proximity to good schools and parks. St Dunstan's, just beyond Westgate, provides a quieter residential feel while remaining within walking distance of the city center.

Northgate

North of the city center, this area combines residential streets with easy access to the university campuses. The mix of students and long-term residents creates a vibrant community atmosphere. Properties range from period terraces to modern apartments, offering options for various budgets and lifestyle preferences.

Harbledown and Rough Common

These areas on Canterbury's outskirts offer more space and often better value for money. Popular with families seeking larger properties and gardens, these neighborhoods maintain good bus links to the city center while providing a more suburban living experience. The area offers excellent primary schools and plenty of green space.

Housing Market and Property Types

Canterbury's property market reflects its desirability and historical significance. The city offers an impressive range of housing options, from medieval cottages within the city walls to modern family homes in the suburbs. Period properties are particularly sought after, with many Tudor, Georgian, and Victorian buildings offering unique character features such as exposed beams, inglenook fireplaces, and original sash windows.

For those moving from Rochester or other Medway areas, you'll find Canterbury's property prices generally higher, reflecting the city's tourist appeal and university presence. However, the investment often proves worthwhile given the city's strong rental market and historical property value growth. First-time buyers might find better value in the outer areas like Rough Common or Harbledown, while still benefiting from Canterbury's amenities and transport links.

New Developments

Several new housing developments around Canterbury's outskirts offer modern amenities and energy-efficient homes. These developments often include parking, gardens, and contemporary features that appeal to families and professionals. Areas like Hersden and Sturry, just outside Canterbury, provide new-build options with good transport connections to the city center.

Education and Schools

Canterbury's reputation for educational excellence makes it particularly attractive to families. The city hosts several outstanding primary and secondary schools, both in the state and independent sectors. The Simon Langton Grammar Schools (for both boys and girls) are among Kent's most prestigious institutions, consistently achieving excellent results and university placements.

For primary education, schools like St Stephen's CE Junior School and Blean Primary School receive outstanding Ofsted ratings. The city's Church of England schools benefit from their connection to the cathedral, often incorporating the city's religious heritage into their educational approach.

Higher Education

The University of Kent and Canterbury Christ Church University make Canterbury a significant educational center. These institutions not only provide employment opportunities but also contribute to the city's cultural life through public lectures, performances, and community engagement programs. The universities' presence ensures excellent library facilities, sports centers, and cultural venues that benefit the wider community.

Employment Opportunities

Canterbury's economy combines traditional sectors with modern industries. The cathedral and tourism industry provide significant employment in hospitality, retail, and heritage management. The universities are major employers, offering opportunities in education, research, administration, and support services.

The city's proximity to London makes it attractive to professionals who can work remotely or commute occasionally. Many residents successfully combine Canterbury living with London careers, using the excellent rail connections for flexible working arrangements. For those seeking local employment, the healthcare sector, public services, and growing technology sector provide diverse opportunities.

Business Environment

Canterbury supports a thriving small business community, with numerous independent shops, restaurants, and service providers. The city's tourist appeal creates opportunities in hospitality, retail, and creative industries. The Kent Innovation Centre, located on the University of Kent campus, supports technology startups and growing businesses.

Leisure and Cultural Activities

Canterbury's cultural offering is exceptional for a city of its size. The Canterbury Cathedral Lodge hosts regular concerts, exhibitions, and cultural events. The Marlowe Theatre presents everything from West End productions to local amateur dramatics, while the Sidney Cooper Gallery showcases contemporary art alongside historical exhibitions.

The city's festival calendar includes the Canterbury Festival, featuring classical music, literature, and arts events. Food lovers can enjoy the Canterbury Food and Drink Festival, celebrating local producers and international cuisines. These events create a vibrant community atmosphere that distinguishes Canterbury from larger cities.

Outdoor Activities

Despite its urban nature, Canterbury offers excellent access to outdoor activities. The River Stour provides opportunities for punting and riverside walks, while Westgate Parks offer green space for families and dog walkers. The nearby Kent Downs Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty provides hiking, cycling, and nature watching opportunities just minutes from the city center.

Canterbury Golf Club and other nearby courses cater to golf enthusiasts, while the city's swimming pools, gyms, and sports centers ensure plenty of fitness options. The historic city walls create an interesting walking route that combines exercise with heritage exploration.

Shopping and Dining

Canterbury successfully combines high street shopping with independent boutiques. The Whitefriars Shopping Centre houses major retail chains, while the historic streets offer unique independent shops, antique stores, and specialist retailers. The weekly farmers' market showcases local Kent produce, including items from the Medway area and wider county.

The dining scene reflects Canterbury's diverse population and tourist appeal. Traditional pubs like The Falstaff serve classic British fare alongside local ales, while restaurants offer cuisines from around the world. The presence of university students ensures plenty of budget-friendly options, while fine dining establishments cater to special occasions and business meals.

Local Produce and Markets

Kent's reputation as the "Garden of England" is well represented in Canterbury's food scene. Local farmers' markets feature seasonal produce, artisan breads, and speciality items from across the county. Many restaurants pride themselves on using local ingredients, creating menus that celebrate Kent's agricultural heritage.

Transport and Getting Around

Canterbury's compact size makes it highly walkable, with most amenities within easy walking distance of residential areas. The city center's pedestrianized zones create a pleasant environment for shopping and socializing. Cycling is increasingly popular, with dedicated cycle paths and bike-friendly policies encouraging sustainable transport.

Public transport within Canterbury is efficient, with regular bus services connecting residential areas to the city center and universities. For travel to other Kent towns, including Rochester and the Medway areas, both rail and bus connections are reliable and frequent.

Parking Considerations

Like many historic cities, Canterbury faces parking challenges in the center. Residents should consider parking availability when choosing properties, particularly in the city center where many period buildings lack off-street parking. Park-and-ride services help manage city center congestion while providing convenient access for residents and visitors.

Healthcare and Services

Canterbury benefits from excellent healthcare facilities, including the Kent and Canterbury Hospital, which provides comprehensive medical services. The city hosts numerous GP practices, dental surgeries, and specialist clinics, ensuring residents have good access to healthcare services.

Local government services are well-established, with Canterbury City Council providing efficient refuse collection, planning services, and community support. The city's libraries, including the modern Canterbury Library, offer excellent facilities for residents of all ages.

Planning Your Move to Canterbury

Moving to Canterbury requires careful planning, particularly given the city's popularity and competitive property market. Start your search early, especially if you're looking for period properties or homes in the most desirable areas. Consider visiting during different times of year to experience how the tourist season affects daily life.

When planning your move from Rochester, Medway, or other areas of Kent, factor in the logistics of relocating to a historic city with narrow streets and limited parking. Professional removal services familiar with Canterbury's unique challenges can make the process much smoother and less stressful.

Timing Your Move

Consider Canterbury's academic calendar when planning your move, as the student population significantly affects the city's rhythm. September and January see increased activity as students arrive, while summer months coincide with peak tourist season. Spring and autumn often provide the best balance of good weather and manageable crowds.
