Rochester Guide

Moving to Rochester: A Complete Guide for New Residents

Published 26 Aug 2025  |  8 min read  |  Medway & Kent Removals

Rochester is one of Kent's most historically significant and characterful cities — and the home of Medway and Kent Removals. Whether you are relocating from London, from elsewhere in Kent or from further afield, this guide covers everything you need to know about moving to Rochester and making the most of what this extraordinary city has to offer.

Why people choose Rochester

Rochester offers a genuinely compelling combination of factors that few cities in the South East can match. Historic character — the Norman cathedral, the castle, the cobbled High Street — combined with practical value: property prices that are among the most accessible in Kent for the London commute time, a growing independent food and culture scene, and proximity to the Kent countryside and coast. For families, the grammar school system is a major draw. For professionals, the London commute of 45 to 55 minutes and the M2 motorway access make Rochester one of the most practical choices in North Kent.

Neighbourhoods in Rochester

The Historic Core — ME1

Rochester's medieval and Victorian heart — the streets around the Cathedral, the Castle and the High Street. Properties here are the most characterful and most sought-after in the city. Georgian townhouses, Victorian terraces and period conversion apartments. Premium prices but extraordinary location. Access challenges with narrow streets require local removal expertise.

Borstal and Wouldham

South of Rochester, the villages of Borstal and Wouldham sit in the Medway Valley with outstanding countryside access. Popular with families seeking a semi-rural setting within easy reach of Rochester's amenities. Predominantly detached and semi-detached housing.

Strood — ME2

Across the River Medway from Rochester, Strood offers slightly more affordable property with good A2 and M2 access. Fast access to Rochester station and the town centre via the Medway bridge. Popular with first-time buyers and commuters.

Property in Rochester

Rochester offers strong value for the London commute time. The average property price in ME1 is approximately £280,000 to £320,000. A three-bedroom terraced house costs between £230,000 and £290,000. A four-bedroom detached on the outskirts costs between £320,000 and £450,000. Period properties in the historic core command premiums but remain excellent value against London equivalents.

Commuting from Rochester

Southeastern services from Rochester station to London Victoria and London Charing Cross take approximately 45 to 55 minutes on faster services. The M2 motorway at junction 2 provides fast road access to the M25 and Dartford Crossing. The Ebbsfleet International high-speed service is accessible via the A2 for connections to London St Pancras in 18 minutes.

Schools in Rochester

Rochester Grammar School is a selective state school with a strong academic record. Several good comprehensive schools serve the ME1, ME2 and ME3 postcodes. Primary school provision includes a range of community and Church of England schools. The independent sector has several prep school options accessible within the Medway towns.

Rochester's cultural and lifestyle offer

The annual Dickens Festival, Rochester Cathedral, the castle, the independent High Street, and the River Medway waterfront provide a rich cultural backdrop. The Medway Valley Walk and the North Downs Way are accessible for walkers and cyclists. The Kent coast at Whitstable and Herne Bay is under 30 minutes by car.
