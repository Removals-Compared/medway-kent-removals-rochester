Area Guide

Moving to Rochester — the complete newcomer's guide to living in the city

📅 5 June 2026 🕐 6 min read 👥 Medway & Kent Removals Team

Rochester has become one of Kent's most sought-after places to call home, combining rich historical heritage with modern amenities and excellent transport links to London. Whether you're drawn by the cathedral city's medieval charm, affordable housing compared to surrounding areas, or the strong sense of community, moving to Rochester Kent offers newcomers an exceptional quality of life. This living in Rochester guide covers everything you need to know before making the move, from the best neighbourhoods to schools, transport, and local amenities that make this Medway town such an attractive destination for families and professionals alike.

Why Choose Rochester for Your Next Home

Rochester sits at the heart of the Medway Towns, offering the perfect balance between historic character and modern convenience. The city boasts England's second-oldest cathedral, a Norman castle, and connections to Charles Dickens, yet remains refreshingly affordable compared to nearby areas like Canterbury or Tunbridge Wells.

The high street has undergone significant regeneration in recent years, bringing new independent shops, cafes, and restaurants alongside established businesses. Property prices remain competitive, making it an attractive option for first-time buyers and families looking to relocate to Rochester Medway without breaking the bank.

Transport connections are excellent, with Rochester station providing direct services to London Victoria, St Pancras, and Cannon Street. The M2 and M20 motorways offer easy access to Dover, Canterbury, and the Channel Tunnel, while London is just 30 miles away.

Best Neighbourhoods and Areas in Rochester

Rochester City Centre

The historic city centre appeals to those wanting to live within walking distance of shops, restaurants, and cultural attractions. Victorian terraces and converted period properties dominate, with many featuring original characteristics like high ceilings and period fireplaces. Prices tend to be higher here, but you'll have Rochester Castle and Cathedral literally on your doorstep.

Borstal and Temple Marsh

These residential areas offer excellent value for money, particularly for families. Borstal features a mix of 1930s semi-detached houses and newer developments, while Temple Marsh has seen considerable new-build activity in recent years. Both areas provide good access to local schools and green spaces.

Strood

Technically separate but closely connected to Rochester, Strood offers some of the most affordable housing in the Medway area. The Temple Waterfront development has brought modern apartments and houses with river views, while established residential streets offer period properties at attractive prices.

Higham and Cuxton

For those seeking a more rural feel while staying within commuting distance, these villages on Rochester's outskirts provide larger properties with gardens, often at similar prices to smaller homes in the city centre. Both have good rail connections and access to countryside walks.

Transport Links and Commuting Options

Rail Services

Rochester station serves as a major transport hub for the Medway Towns. High-speed services reach London St Pancras in just 44 minutes, while conventional services to London Victoria take around an hour. Southeastern operates frequent services throughout the day, making Rochester an excellent choice for London commuters.

The Medway Valley line connects Rochester with Maidstone and Tonbridge, providing access to other parts of Kent without travelling via London. This route particularly benefits those working in Maidstone or needing connections to the south coast.

Road Access

The A2 runs directly through Rochester, providing excellent road links to Canterbury, Dover, and London. Junction 1 of the M2 sits just outside the city, offering quick access to the M25 and beyond. However, traffic can be heavy during peak hours, particularly around the Medway Tunnel and bridges.

Parking in the city centre can be challenging and expensive, but most residential areas offer on-street parking or driveways. Several park-and-ride schemes operate during busy periods and events.

Local Transport

Arriva Southern Counties operates local bus services connecting Rochester with Chatham, Gillingham, Gravesend, and surrounding villages. The 151 and 156 routes provide regular services to Bluewater Shopping Centre, while route 101 connects with Maidstone.

Schools and Education

Primary Schools

Rochester offers several well-regarded primary schools. St Nicholas CE Primary School consistently achieves good Ofsted ratings and benefits from small class sizes. Warren Wood Primary Academy serves the Borstal area and has excellent facilities including a forest school programme.

For families in Strood, Temple Mill Primary School has undergone significant improvements and now offers outstanding early years provision. Many parents also consider schools in neighbouring Higham and Cuxton, which often have smaller cohorts and strong community links.

Secondary Schools

The Rochester Math School, part of the prestigious King's College London Mathematics School network, attracts students from across Kent and beyond. This specialist sixth form college focuses on mathematics, further mathematics, and physics.

Warren Wood Academy serves ages 4-16 and has invested heavily in new facilities and teaching resources. The school achieved a 'Good' Ofsted rating and offers strong support for students with additional needs.

Grammar school options include nearby Fort Pitt Grammar School for Girls in Chatham and Sir Joseph Williamson's Mathematical School, though competition for places is intense.

Further Education

MidKent College operates a campus in Gillingham, just a short bus ride from Rochester, offering vocational courses and apprenticeships. The University of Greenwich has a campus at the Historic Dockyard Chatham, providing degree courses in engineering, business, and maritime studies.

Shopping and Retail

Rochester High Street

The historic high street combines chain stores with independent retailers. Marks & Spencer, Boots, and WHSmith anchor the retail offering, while independent bookshops, antique stores, and gift shops add character. The weekly farmers' market showcases local Kent produce and artisan goods.

Shopping Centres

The Pentagon Shopping Centre in nearby Chatham provides major retail brands under one roof, including Next, H&M, and Primark. Free parking and regular bus services from Rochester make it easily accessible.

Bluewater, one of Europe's largest shopping centres, sits just 20 minutes away by car or bus. With over 300 stores and restaurants, it handles most major shopping needs, from fashion to electronics and home goods.

Local Markets and Speciality Shops

Rochester Castle Market operates every Friday and Saturday, offering fresh produce, plants, and household goods. The monthly Dickens Market celebrates the city's literary connections with period costumes and Victorian-themed stalls.

Several farm shops in surrounding villages provide fresh local produce, while Chatham Historic Dockyard hosts craft fairs and speciality markets throughout the year.

Healthcare and Medical Services

GP Surgeries

Several medical practices serve Rochester residents. The Bridge Medical Practice on Star Hill offers comprehensive family healthcare with online appointment booking. Rochester Medical Centre provides additional services including minor surgery and health screening.

New residents should register with a local GP as soon as possible after moving. Most practices accept registrations online or by visiting the surgery with proof of address and photo ID.

Hospital Services

Medway Maritime Hospital in Gillingham serves as the area's main hospital, providing A&E services, maternity care, and specialist departments. The hospital underwent major redevelopment and now offers modern facilities across all departments.

For specialist services not available at Medway Maritime, Maidstone Hospital and several London hospitals are easily accessible by rail or road.

Dental and Specialist Care

NHS dental practices in Rochester often have waiting lists, so registering early is advisable. Several private dental practices offer immediate appointments and cosmetic services.

Opticians, physiotherapy clinics, and other healthcare specialists operate throughout the city centre and residential areas.

Recreation and Leisure Activities

Historic Attractions

Rochester Castle and Cathedral form the city's historic heart. The castle keep, one of the finest in England, offers stunning views across the Medway Valley. Rochester Cathedral, founded in 604 AD, hosts regular concerts and cultural events alongside religious services.

The Guildhall Museum showcases local history, while the Charles Dickens Centre celebrates the author's connections with the city. Many of Dickens' novels feature Rochester locations, and the annual Dickens Festival attracts visitors from around the world.

Parks and Green Spaces

Eastgate House Gardens provide a peaceful retreat in the city centre, while Vines Park offers playing fields, tennis courts, and children's play areas. The park hosts outdoor concerts and events during summer months.

For longer walks, the Saxon Shore Way follows the Medway Estuary, providing excellent views and wildlife watching opportunities. Ranscombe Farm Reserve, managed by Plantlife, protects rare chalk downland plants and offers several circular walking routes.

Sports and Fitness

Medway Park in Gillingham houses the area's main leisure centre, featuring swimming pools, sports halls, and a climbing wall. Several private gyms operate in Rochester, including budget chains and boutique fitness studios.

Rochester Rugby Club welcomes new members of all abilities, while Holcombe Hockey Club, based in nearby Chatham, competes at national level. Several golf courses within easy reach include Rochester & Cobham Park Golf Club and Deangate Ridge Golf Club.

Dining and Nightlife

Restaurants and Cafes

Rochester's dining scene has expanded significantly in recent years. The Corn Exchange offers modern British cuisine in a beautifully converted Victorian building, while Zizzi occupies a prime cathedral-view location.

Independent restaurants include Saffron, serving contemporary Indian cuisine, and The Crown Inn, a traditional pub with excellent Sunday roasts. Several coffee shops and cafes line the high street, perfect for meeting friends or working remotely.

Pubs and Bars

The city boasts several historic pubs, many with Dickens connections. The Eagle Tavern features in 'The Pickwick Papers', while The Bull Inn claims to be Rochester's oldest pub. The Good Intent offers craft beers and regular live music.

For evening entertainment, several bars stay open late, particularly on weekends. The nearby Chatham area provides additional nightlife options, including cocktail bars and late-night venues.

Cost of Living in Rochester

Housing Costs

Property prices in Rochester remain significantly below London levels while offering excellent transport links. A typical two-bedroom terraced house costs between £200,000-£280,000, while three-bedroom semi-detached properties range from £280,000-£400,000.

Rental costs average £800-£1,200 per month for a two-bedroom property, depending on location and condition. City centre properties command premium prices, while areas like Borstal and Strood offer better value.

Everyday Expenses

Council tax varies by property band and area, with Medway Council generally offering competitive rates compared to other Kent authorities. Utility costs align with national averages, though newer properties often feature better insulation and energy efficiency.

Local shopping costs remain reasonable, with several supermarkets competing for business. Tesco, ASDA, and Sainsbury's all operate large stores within easy reach, while Aldi and Lidl provide budget options.

Community and Local Culture

Community Groups and Societies

Rochester supports an active community with groups covering most interests and hobbies. The Rochester Choral Society welcomes new members, while Rochester Art Society organises exhibitions and workshops throughout the year.

Several residents' associations represent different neighbourhoods, organising social events and lobbying for local improvements. The Rochester Bridge Trust, established in 1399, continues to support local community projects and charities.

Annual Events and Festivals

The Rochester Dickens Festival, held each December, transforms the city centre with costumed characters, street performances, and Victorian market stalls. The Sweeps Festival in May celebrates traditional Morris dancing and folk music.

Rochester Castle Concerts feature major artists performing against the dramatic backdrop of the Norman keep, while the Cathedral hosts regular classical music concerts and choral performances.

Practical Moving Tips for Rochester

Parking and Loading

Many Rochester streets are narrow and parking can be limited, particularly in the historic city centre. When planning your move, consider that some areas require parking permits and loading restrictions apply during certain hours.

Professional removal companies familiar with Rochester know which routes work best for large vehicles and how to navigate the one-way systems around the cathedral and castle areas.

Timing Your Move

Summer months can be busy due to tourist traffic around the historic attractions. School holidays also bring additional congestion. Professional movers can advise on optimal timing to avoid delays and extra costs.

Local council services may have different collection days in different areas of Rochester and the wider Medway Towns, so check your new address arrangements in advance.

Moving to Rochester offers the chance to join a community steeped in history while enjoying modern amenities and excellent connections. From the moment you arrive, you'll find a welcoming city that balances urban convenience with small-town charm.
