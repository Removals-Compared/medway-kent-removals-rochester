Area Guide

Moving to Rochester — the complete newcomer's guide to living in the city

📅 29 May 2026 🕐 6 min read 👥 Medway & Kent Removals Team

Moving to Rochester Kent offers the perfect blend of rich history, modern amenities, and excellent transport links to London and beyond. As a removal company based right here in Rochester, we've helped hundreds of families make this beautiful Medway city their home. Whether you're relocating for work, seeking better value for money compared to London prices, or simply wanting to enjoy the historic charm of Kent's Cathedral City, this guide will help you understand what makes Rochester such an appealing place to live.

Why Choose Rochester for Your Next Move

Rochester sits at the heart of Medway, perfectly positioned between the countryside of Kent and the capital city. The historic cathedral city offers something genuinely special that many newcomers don't expect. You'll find a place where medieval architecture sits comfortably alongside modern developments, where you can walk to work in the morning and explore Roman ruins in the afternoon.

The cost of living here remains significantly lower than London, yet you're still only 30 minutes from the capital by train. Many of our clients are London commuters who've discovered they can afford a proper family home with a garden, rather than a cramped flat, without sacrificing their career prospects.

Transport Links and Commuting

Rochester station provides direct services to London Victoria, St Pancras International, and Cannon Street. The journey to central London typically takes 35-45 minutes, making it entirely feasible for daily commuting. You'll also find good connections to Canterbury, Dover, and other Kent towns.

The M2 and A2 provide excellent road connections, whether you're heading to London, Canterbury, or the Kent coast. The area around Medway Services makes accessing the motorway network straightforward for business travel or weekend trips.

Finding the Right Neighbourhood in Rochester

Each area of Rochester and the surrounding Medway towns has its own character. Understanding these differences will help you choose the perfect location for your family's needs.

Rochester City Centre

Living in the historic heart means walking to shops, restaurants, and the cathedral. The High Street area offers period properties, many beautifully converted, alongside modern apartments. You'll pay a premium for the convenience and character, but many residents love the ability to walk everywhere and feel part of the city's daily life.

Borstal and Nashenden Valley

These areas offer excellent family homes with more space and often better value than the city centre. Borstal provides easy access to the countryside while maintaining good links to Rochester station. Many properties here feature gardens and parking, which can be harder to find in the city centre.

Strood and Temple Marsh

Strood offers some of the best value housing in the Medway area. The Temple Marsh development provides modern family homes, while older parts of Strood feature Victorian terraces and 1930s semi-detached houses. The area has improved significantly in recent years and offers excellent transport links.

Walderslade and Hempstead

These areas provide a more suburban feel while remaining within easy reach of Rochester. You'll find larger family homes, often with driveways and gardens, plus good local schools. The trade-off is usually a slightly longer commute to the station.

Education and Schools

Rochester and Medway offer strong educational opportunities across all age groups. The area features several outstanding primary schools, good secondary options, and access to higher education.

Primary Schools

Many of our clients specifically relocate to Rochester for the school options. St Margaret's CE Primary, Warren Wood Primary Academy, and Fairview Community Primary all receive strong Ofsted ratings. The cathedral school, King's Rochester, provides independent education option for families seeking private schooling.

Secondary Education

The Rochester area includes several well-regarded secondary schools. The Math School, Sir Joseph Williamson's Mathematical School, provides selective education, while Chatham Grammar Schools (both boys and girls) offer excellent academic opportunities. For non-selective options, several academies provide strong comprehensive education.

Further Education

The Universities of Greenwich and Canterbury both have campuses in Medway, providing local higher education options. This also means the area benefits from the energy and amenities that come with a student population.

Shopping and Daily Amenities

Living in Rochester means having access to everything you need for daily life, plus some unique shopping experiences you won't find elsewhere.

High Street Shopping

Rochester High Street combines chain stores with independent shops. You'll find charity shops with genuine treasures, antique dealers, bookshops, and cafes. The weekly market adds to the traditional market town atmosphere.

Supermarkets and Convenience

Major supermarkets including Tesco, ASDA, and Sainsbury's all have stores in the Medway area. For daily essentials, you'll find convenience stores throughout Rochester, including several that stay open late.

Retail Parks and Shopping Centres

Dockside Outlet Centre in Chatham provides discount shopping for clothing and household items. Hempstead Valley Shopping Centre, though technically in Gillingham, serves the wider Medway area with major retailers and restaurants.

Healthcare and Medical Services

Moving to a new area always raises questions about healthcare access. Rochester and Medway provide excellent medical services across all specialties.

GP Practices and Primary Care

Several GP practices serve Rochester residents, including The Croft Medical Centre and Rochester Medical Centre. Most practices accept new patients, though it's worth registering as soon as you move to avoid any waiting periods.

Hospital Services

Medway NHS Foundation Trust operates Medway Maritime Hospital, providing accident and emergency services, maternity care, and specialist treatments. The hospital has a strong reputation and handles everything from routine procedures to complex surgery.

Dental and Specialist Care

Both NHS and private dental practices operate throughout the area. Specialist services including opticians, physiotherapy, and alternative health practitioners are readily available.

Recreation and Leisure Activities

Rochester offers far more leisure opportunities than many people expect. The combination of history, countryside access, and modern facilities creates options for every interest and age group.

Historic Attractions

Rochester Cathedral and Rochester Castle provide obvious starting points for exploring local history. The Guildhall Museum tells the story of the area, while the annual Dickens Festival celebrates the city's literary connections. Having these attractions on your doorstep means you can explore them gradually rather than feeling pressure to see everything at once.

Parks and Open Spaces

The Vines Park offers formal gardens and open space right in the city centre. For more extensive outdoor activities, the North Downs and Medway Valley are easily accessible. The Saxon Shore Way provides excellent walking opportunities along the Thames Estuary.

Sports and Fitness

Medway Park provides swimming, gym facilities, and various sports activities. Local football clubs, cricket teams, and other sports groups welcome new members. The area around the Thames and Medway offers opportunities for water sports and sailing.

Dining and Entertainment

The food scene in Rochester has developed significantly in recent years. You'll find everything from traditional pub food to international cuisine, plus several establishments that would hold their own in any major city.

Restaurants and Cafes

Rochester High Street features several excellent restaurants serving everything from modern British cuisine to authentic Indian, Italian, and Chinese food. The cathedral quarter has developed a particular reputation for independent cafes and bistros.

Pubs and Social Life

Traditional Kent pubs remain an important part of local social life. Many offer excellent food alongside local ales and provide genuine community meeting places. The newer bar scene caters to young professionals and offers more contemporary atmospheres.

Practical Moving Considerations

Having helped hundreds of families relocate to Rochester, we've learned what practical issues matter most when settling into the area.

Council Services and Local Government

Medway Council provides local services including council tax collection, waste management, and planning services. Their offices in Gun Wharf, Chatham handle most resident services. Council tax rates remain reasonable compared to many Kent authorities.

Utilities and Internet

All major utility providers serve the Rochester area. Broadband coverage is generally excellent, with both BT and Virgin Media providing high-speed options. Most areas can access superfast broadband, which matters increasingly for home working.

Local Tradespeople and Services

Rochester has a good network of local tradespeople, from plumbers and electricians to gardeners and handymen. The mix of older properties and new developments means skilled workers understand both period building requirements and modern systems.

Getting Settled After Your Move

The practical steps after moving matter just as much as choosing the right area. Based on our experience helping families settle in Rochester, certain actions make the transition much smoother.

Register with a local GP practice immediately after moving, even if you're healthy. This avoids complications if you need medical care unexpectedly. Similarly, registering children for school places should happen as soon as possible, particularly if you're moving during term time.

Joining local Facebook groups and community organizations helps you tap into local knowledge quickly. Rochester residents are generally welcoming to newcomers and happy to share recommendations for everything from the best local takeaway to reliable childcare options.
