Moving to Rochester — the complete newcomer's guide to living in the city

📅 25 May 2026 🕐 6 min read 👥 Medway & Kent Removals Team

Moving to Rochester in Kent means joining one of England's most historically rich cities, perfectly positioned in the Medway area with excellent transport links to London and the rest of the county. Whether you're relocating for work, family reasons, or simply seeking a better quality of life, this guide covers everything you need to know about making Rochester your new home. From housing markets to schools, transport connections to local amenities, we'll help you understand what makes this ancient city such an attractive place to live.

Why Choose Rochester for Your Next Move

Rochester offers an exceptional blend of historical charm and modern convenience that's hard to find elsewhere in Kent. The city sits at the heart of the Medway Towns, giving residents easy access to Chatham, Gillingham, and Strood while maintaining its own distinct character. The magnificent Rochester Cathedral and imposing castle create a stunning backdrop for daily life, while the High Street offers everything from independent shops to well-known retailers.

The location proves particularly attractive for commuters. Rochester station provides direct rail services to London Victoria, St Pancras International, and Cannon Street, with journey times typically between 35-50 minutes. This connectivity has made Rochester increasingly popular with professionals seeking affordable housing outside London without sacrificing career opportunities.

House prices in Rochester remain more accessible than many areas within commuting distance of the capital. The local property market offers everything from period terraces near the cathedral to modern developments along the Medway waterfront. Families particularly appreciate the range of good schools and the abundance of green spaces, including the beautiful Eastgate House gardens and nearby countryside walks.

Understanding Rochester's Neighbourhoods

Rochester City Centre

The historic city centre provides the ultimate in convenience, with shops, restaurants, and cultural attractions on your doorstep. Properties here tend to be period buildings, often converted into flats or maintained as family homes. Living centrally means walking to the station, cathedral, and castle, though parking can be challenging and property prices reflect the premium location.

Borstal and Temple Farm

These popular residential areas offer family-friendly streets with good access to local primary schools. Borstal sits slightly elevated, providing some lovely views across the Medway valley. The areas feature a mix of 1930s housing and more recent developments, with Temple Farm offering some of the newer builds in Rochester.

Strood and Rochester Riverside

The riverside areas have seen significant development in recent years, with modern apartment blocks and townhouses taking advantage of waterfront positions. These locations offer excellent transport links, being close to both Strood and Rochester stations, plus easy access to the A2 for drivers.

St Margaret's and Troy Town

These quieter residential areas provide a more suburban feel while keeping you within easy reach of the city centre. St Margaret's offers good value family housing, while Troy Town has become increasingly popular with young professionals and first-time buyers.

Housing Market and Property Types

Rochester's property market caters to all budgets and requirements. Victorian and Edwardian terraces dominate many streets, offering period features and generous room sizes. These properties typically range from two-bedroom workers' cottages to substantial family homes with four or five bedrooms.

Modern developments have added contemporary options, particularly around the riverside areas and on former industrial sites. These properties often include parking and gardens, addressing some of the limitations of older housing stock. Purpose-built apartments serve the rental market well, especially popular with commuters and young professionals.

First-time buyers often find Rochester more accessible than areas closer to London. The combination of good transport links and relatively affordable housing makes it possible to get onto the property ladder without compromising on amenities or lifestyle. The local market has shown steady growth, supported by continued demand from London commuters and the ongoing regeneration of the wider Medway area.

Transport Links and Getting Around

Rail Connections

Rochester station serves as your gateway to London and beyond. High-speed services reach St Pancras International in around 35 minutes, while regular services connect to Victoria and other London terminals. The station also provides connections to Dover, Canterbury, and other Kent destinations, making it easy to explore your new county.

Road Networks

The A2 runs close to Rochester, providing direct routes to London and Dover. Junction 1 of the M25 lies within easy reach, opening up connections to the wider motorway network. For local travel, the A229 links Rochester to Maidstone and the A228 connects to the Medway Towns and beyond to West Malling and Tonbridge.

Local Transport

Local bus services connect different areas of Rochester and link to neighbouring towns. Many residents find cycling practical for local journeys, with National Cycle Route 1 passing through the area. The compact city centre makes walking viable for daily errands, particularly if you live in the central areas.

Schools and Education Options

Rochester provides excellent educational opportunities across all age groups. The area hosts several outstanding primary schools, including St Peter's Catholic Primary and Warren Wood Primary Academy, both rated highly by Ofsted. These schools serve local catchment areas, making proximity an important consideration when choosing where to live.

For secondary education, Rochester has strong options including Rochester Grammar School and The Rochester Grammar School for Girls, both selective schools with excellent academic records. Non-selective options include Walderslade Girls' School and Holcombe Grammar School, providing quality education without the entrance exam requirements.

The nearby Universities at Greenwich (Medway campus) and Canterbury Christ Church University (Medway campus) mean higher education opportunities exist locally. These institutions also contribute to the area's cultural life and provide part-time and continuing education options for residents.

Shopping and Daily Amenities

Rochester High Street combines chain stores with independent retailers, creating an engaging shopping environment. The weekly market adds fresh produce and local goods, while the nearby Dockside Outlet Centre provides additional retail options with free parking.

For larger shopping trips, Bluewater shopping centre lies about 20 minutes away by car, offering department stores and designer outlets. Closer to home, Chatham's Pentagon Centre provides mainstream shopping with good public transport connections from Rochester.

Daily necessities are well covered with supermarkets including Tesco, ASDA, and smaller convenience stores throughout residential areas. The city centre hosts several banks, a post office, and various professional services, reducing the need to travel elsewhere for routine errands.

Healthcare and Medical Facilities

Medway Maritime Hospital serves as the main hospital for the area, providing comprehensive medical services including accident and emergency facilities. The hospital sits in Gillingham, easily accessible from Rochester by car or public transport.

Several GP practices serve Rochester residents, including the Riverside Practice and Temple Medical Centre. NHS dental services operate alongside private practices, though registering with a new dentist may require some patience as popular practices often have waiting lists.

Pharmacy services are well distributed throughout Rochester, with branches on the High Street and in residential areas. Many offer extended hours and additional health services like vaccinations and health checks.

Entertainment and Cultural Life

Rochester's cultural calendar revolves around its historical significance and literary connections. The annual Rochester Dickens Festival celebrates the city's connection to Charles Dickens, drawing visitors from across the country. The castle and cathedral host regular events, concerts, and exhibitions throughout the year.

The Guildhall Museum provides insights into local history, while the nearby Historic Dockyard Chatham offers family days out with maritime heritage. For evening entertainment, several pubs and restaurants line the High Street and side streets, ranging from traditional English fare to international cuisine.

The Central Theatre hosts touring productions and local amateur groups, while nearby venues in Chatham and Maidstone expand entertainment options. Many residents also take advantage of easy London access for West End shows and major cultural events.

Green Spaces and Outdoor Activities

Rochester provides excellent access to green spaces despite its urban setting. The Esplanade offers riverside walks with views across the Medway, perfect for morning jogs or evening strolls. Restoration House and its gardens provide peaceful corners in the city centre.

Just outside Rochester, the North Downs Way offers serious hiking opportunities with stunning countryside views. Ranscombe Farm Reserve provides family-friendly walking trails and wildlife spotting, while Cobham Park and Shorne Woods offer more extensive outdoor recreation within easy driving distance.

The River Medway supports various water sports, with sailing clubs and rowing clubs welcoming new members. Golf enthusiasts have several courses nearby, including Rochester and Cobham Park Golf Club and the Mid Kent Golf Club.

Cost of Living Considerations

Living costs in Rochester generally prove lower than inner London while remaining competitive with other commuter towns. Housing represents the largest expense for most residents, but prices typically offer better value than areas closer to the capital.

Council tax falls under Medway Council's rates, which tend to be reasonable compared to some Kent districts. Utility costs align with national averages, though older properties may have higher heating bills due to period construction methods.

Transport costs depend heavily on commuting patterns. Season tickets to London represent a significant monthly expense, but the time saved and lifestyle benefits often justify the cost for many residents. Local transport and fuel costs remain standard for the region.

Practical Moving Tips for Rochester

Planning your move to Rochester requires consideration of the area's specific characteristics. Parking restrictions apply in many central areas, so check regulations before your moving day. Some streets have narrow access, particularly near the historic centre, which may affect large removal vehicles.

Register with local services promptly after moving. GP practices, dentists, and schools can have waiting lists, so early registration proves beneficial. Contact Medway Council to register for council tax and electoral roll, and ensure your address change reaches all relevant organisations.

Utility connections generally proceed smoothly, but book services in advance, particularly during busy periods. Internet installation may take longer in some older properties, so arrange this early if you work from home or rely on good connectivity.

Getting Started in Your New Community

Rochester's community spirit shows through various local groups and activities. The Rochester Bridge Trust organises community events, while local churches, sports clubs, and hobby groups provide opportunities to meet neighbours and make friends.

Volunteering opportunities exist through the cathedral, local charities, and community organisations. These activities help you understand local issues while contributing positively to your new home area.

Local Facebook groups and community boards keep residents informed about events, recommendations, and local issues. These online communities prove particularly helpful for newcomers seeking advice about services, activities, and local knowledge.

Moving to Rochester opens doors to a lifestyle combining historical charm with modern convenience, excellent transport links with affordable living costs, and urban amenities with countryside access. The city's unique character and strong community spirit make it an exceptional choice for your next home.
