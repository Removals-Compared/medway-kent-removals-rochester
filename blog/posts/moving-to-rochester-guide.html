Moving to Rochester — the complete newcomer's guide to living in the city

📅 10 June 2026 🕐 6 min read 👥 Medway & Kent Removals Team

If you're considering moving to Rochester Kent, you're looking at one of England's most historic and well-connected cities. As a removal company that's helped hundreds of families relocate to Rochester over the years, we've seen firsthand how this ancient cathedral city captures hearts with its blend of medieval charm and modern convenience. Located in the heart of Medway, Rochester offers excellent transport links to London, beautiful historic sites, and a genuine sense of community that makes it perfect for families, professionals, and retirees alike.

Why Choose Rochester for Your New Home

Rochester sits at the heart of the Medway Towns, offering the perfect balance between historic character and modern living. The city boasts one of England's oldest cathedrals, a magnificent Norman castle, and charming cobbled streets that have remained largely unchanged since Charles Dickens walked them. Yet step away from the historic quarter, and you'll find modern housing developments, excellent schools, and all the amenities you need for contemporary life.

The location is genuinely hard to beat. You're just 30 minutes from central London by train, making Rochester ideal for commuters who want to escape the capital's high property prices without sacrificing career opportunities. The M2 and M20 motorways provide easy access to the rest of Kent and beyond, while the Channel Tunnel is less than an hour away for those with European travel plans.

Property prices remain significantly more affordable than London and many surrounding areas. Whether you're looking for a period home near the cathedral, a modern family house in one of the newer developments, or a riverside apartment with Medway views, Rochester offers options across all price ranges.

Understanding Rochester's Neighborhoods

The Historic Quarter

The area around Rochester Cathedral and Castle represents the city's beating heart. Living here means being surrounded by over 900 years of history, with the magnificent cathedral and imposing castle ruins on your doorstep. The High Street features independent shops, traditional pubs, and restaurants housed in buildings that have stood for centuries.

Properties in this area tend to be period homes, including Georgian terraces and Victorian houses that have been carefully maintained. Many have been converted into flats, making them accessible to first-time buyers. The trade-off for this historic charm is limited parking and the occasional tourist crowds, particularly during Rochester's famous festivals.

Strood

Just across the River Medway, Strood offers excellent value for money and has undergone significant regeneration in recent years. The area benefits from its own railway station with direct services to London, making it particularly popular with commuters. Temple Marsh retail park provides major shopping options, while the residential areas offer a mix of Victorian terraces, 1930s semis, and modern developments.

Strood's location means you get easy access to Rochester's historic attractions while enjoying more affordable housing and better parking options. The area is experiencing ongoing investment, with new housing developments and improved transport links planned for the coming years.

Borstal and Wouldham

For those seeking a more rural feel while staying within reach of Rochester's amenities, Borstal and nearby Wouldham offer attractive options. These areas provide larger properties with gardens, often at prices that would buy you a small flat closer to the city center. The communities here are tight-knit, with excellent primary schools and easy access to countryside walks along the North Downs.

The trade-off is a greater reliance on private transport, though bus services connect these areas to Rochester and the wider Medway region. For families with children or those who work from home, these neighborhoods offer the space and tranquility that's increasingly rare in the Southeast.

Transport and Commuting from Rochester

One of Rochester's greatest strengths is its transport connections. The city sits on the main London to Dover railway line, with regular high-speed services reaching London St Pancras in just 35 minutes. Standard services to London Victoria take around 45 minutes, making the daily commute entirely manageable.

For those working in other parts of Kent, the railway connections are equally strong. You can reach Canterbury in 30 minutes, Maidstone in 20 minutes, and the coast at Margate or Ramsgate in under an hour. This connectivity makes Rochester an excellent base for exploring your new county or maintaining work connections across the region.

By road, the M2 motorway runs just south of the city, providing quick access to Canterbury and the Channel ports. The M25 is 30 minutes away, opening up the entire Southeast road network. Within Medway itself, regular bus services connect all the main towns, though many residents find a car useful for reaching the more rural parts of Kent.

For air travel, London's airports are all accessible by public transport, with Gatwick being the most convenient via direct train services. The Channel Tunnel at Folkestone opens up continental Europe, making Rochester an excellent base for those with European business interests or holiday plans.

Education and Schools in Rochester

Families moving to Rochester will find a good selection of educational options across all age groups. The area benefits from several well-regarded primary schools, including St Nicholas C of E Primary and Warren Wood Primary Academy, both of which consistently achieve strong Ofsted ratings.

For secondary education, The Rochester Grammar School maintains its position as one of Kent's top-performing selective schools, though entrance requires passing the eleven-plus examination. Alternative options include Holcombe Grammar School for girls and several well-regarded comprehensive schools serving the wider Medway area.

The University of Kent's Medway campus, shared with Canterbury Christ Church University, brings a student population to the area and offers opportunities for adult learning and professional development. This academic presence contributes to the area's cultural life and provides employment opportunities beyond traditional industries.

For families with younger children, the area offers numerous nurseries and childcare options, from small independent providers to larger chain nurseries. The mix of provision means parents can usually find something that fits both their childcare needs and budget requirements.

Shopping and Amenities

Rochester's shopping scene blends independent retailers with modern convenience. The High Street maintains its traditional character with independent bookshops, antique dealers, and specialty food stores. The connection to Charles Dickens brings unique shops selling books, gifts, and memorabilia that you won't find elsewhere.

For everyday shopping, the Dockside Outlet Centre provides major retail brands at discounted prices, while nearby Bluewater shopping center offers one of Europe's largest retail experiences just 20 minutes away by car. Closer to home, Temple Marsh retail park in Strood covers most daily needs with supermarkets, home improvement stores, and restaurants.

The weekly farmers market brings local Kent produce to the city center, allowing residents to support local businesses while enjoying fresh, seasonal food. This connection to Kent's agricultural heritage remains strong, with many farm shops and pick-your-own operations within easy reach of the city.

Healthcare needs are well served by Medway Maritime Hospital, one of the largest hospitals in the Southeast, providing comprehensive medical services including accident and emergency care. Numerous GP practices serve different areas of the city, and dental and optical services are readily available.

Culture and Entertainment

Rochester's cultural calendar revolves around its literary connections and medieval heritage. The annual Rochester Dickens Festival transforms the city center into Victorian England, complete with period costumes, street performers, and traditional Christmas markets. The Sweeps Festival celebrates traditional English Morris dancing and folk music, drawing visitors from across the country.

The Guildhall Museum tells the story of Rochester's long history, while the nearby Historic Dockyard Chatham offers a full day of maritime history and education. For regular entertainment, the Central Theatre provides professional productions and local amateur dramatic society performances throughout the year.

The cathedral hosts regular concerts and recitals, taking advantage of its exceptional acoustics and stunning medieval architecture. Many events are free or very reasonably priced, making cultural entertainment accessible to all residents.

Rochester's pub scene reflects both its historic character and modern needs. Traditional pubs like The Eagle Tavern offer real ales and hearty food in atmospheric surroundings, while newer establishments provide contemporary dining and international cuisines.

Green Spaces and Recreation

Despite its urban location, Rochester provides good access to green spaces and recreational activities. The castle grounds offer a peaceful escape in the city center, while Eastgate House gardens provide a quiet spot for reflection.

The real recreational opportunities lie just outside the city boundaries. The North Downs Way passes close to Rochester, offering world-class walking with stunning views across the Medway valley. Ranscombe Farm Reserve provides 500 acres of chalk downland perfect for family walks and wildlife watching.

The River Medway itself offers boating opportunities, with several marinas providing moorings and boat hire services. The Medway Valley Walk follows the river through some of Kent's most beautiful countryside, accessible by public transport from Rochester city center.

For more active pursuits, the area offers several golf courses, tennis clubs, and swimming facilities. The nearby villages of Aylesford and Maidstone provide additional recreational facilities within easy reach.

Practical Considerations for Your Move

When planning your relocation to Rochester, timing can significantly impact both your moving costs and your settling-in experience. The summer months see higher demand for both removal services and rental properties, particularly from families wanting to move before the new school year begins.

Council tax rates in Medway are generally competitive compared to other parts of Kent and the Southeast. The local council provides efficient services, though it's worth registering for council tax, electoral roll, and waste collection services as soon as possible after your move.

Utility connections are generally straightforward, with most properties already connected to mains gas, electricity, and water. Internet connectivity is good throughout most of Rochester, with fiber broadband available in most areas. However, some of the older properties in the historic quarter may have limitations, so it's worth checking connectivity speeds if you work from home.

Parking can be challenging in the historic center, with many period properties having no off-street parking. However, residents' parking permits are available, and many of the newer developments include dedicated parking spaces. If you're viewing properties, consider your parking needs carefully, particularly if you have multiple vehicles.

Making the Move to Rochester

The key to a successful move to Rochester lies in understanding what each area offers and choosing the neighborhood that best fits your lifestyle and budget. Whether you prioritize historic character, modern amenities, good schools, or transport links, Rochester's diverse neighborhoods can accommodate different preferences.

Professional removal services become particularly valuable when moving to Rochester's historic areas, where narrow streets and limited parking can create challenges for DIY moves. Local knowledge of access routes, parking restrictions, and building quirks can save both time and stress on moving day.

Once you arrive, joining local community groups and societies provides the quickest route to feeling settled. Rochester has active residents' associations, hobby groups, and volunteer organizations that welcome newcomers and provide opportunities to meet like-minded neighbors.
