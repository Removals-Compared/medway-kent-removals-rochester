Area Guide

Moving to Rochester — the complete newcomer's guide to living in the city

📅 3 June 2026 🕐 6 min read 👥 Medway & Kent Removals Team

Thinking about moving to Rochester Kent? You've chosen one of the most historically rich and well-connected cities in the region. As a local removal company based right here in Rochester, we've helped hundreds of families make this exciting transition. This practical guide covers everything you need to know about relocating to Rochester Medway, from the best neighbourhoods to local amenities, transport links, and what makes this ancient city such a great place to call home.

Why Choose Rochester for Your New Home

Rochester sits at the heart of the Medway Towns, offering an unbeatable combination of historical charm and modern convenience. The city boasts a stunning cathedral, an impressive Norman castle, and a high street that Charles Dickens himself would recognise. Yet it's also perfectly positioned for commuters, with excellent rail links to London and easy access to the M2 and M20 motorways.

The cost of living here remains more affordable than many parts of Kent, while still offering excellent schools, healthcare facilities, and recreational opportunities. Whether you're a young professional, growing family, or looking to downsize in retirement, Rochester provides options for every lifestyle and budget.

Understanding Rochester's Neighbourhoods

Rochester City Centre

The historic heart of Rochester offers character properties ranging from Victorian terraces to modern apartments. Living in the city centre puts you within walking distance of shops, restaurants, and cultural attractions. The High Street features independent retailers alongside familiar brands, while the weekly farmers' market brings fresh local produce right to your doorstep.

Properties here tend to be well-connected to public transport, making them popular with commuters. However, parking can be limited, so consider this if you rely heavily on a car for daily activities.

Borstal and Temple Farm

These residential areas offer more space and greenery while remaining close to Rochester's amenities. Borstal provides excellent views across the Medway estuary and features a good mix of housing types, from 1930s semi-detached homes to newer developments. Temple Farm, where our removal company is based, represents some of Rochester's more recent residential growth, with modern family homes and good local schools.

Strood

Technically part of the wider Medway area, Strood connects directly to Rochester and offers some of the most affordable housing in the region. The area has undergone significant regeneration in recent years, with new retail parks and improved transport links. Families often choose Strood for the larger gardens and driveways that come with many properties.

Chatham and Gillingham

While separate towns, Chatham and Gillingham form part of the Medway conurbation and offer alternative living options within easy reach of Rochester. Chatham features the Historic Dockyard and a large shopping centre, while Gillingham provides excellent sports facilities and parks. Both towns connect seamlessly to Rochester via public transport and road networks.

Transport and Commuting from Rochester

Rail Connections

Rochester railway station provides direct services to London Victoria, with journey times of approximately 50 minutes during peak hours. Trains also run to London St Pancras via the High Speed 1 line from nearby Ebbsfleet International, reachable in about 20 minutes by car. For those working in Canterbury or the east Kent coast, regular services connect through to Dover and Ramsgate.

The station sits within easy walking distance of the city centre, and most residential areas connect via regular bus services. Season ticket costs remain competitive compared to other Kent commuter towns, making Rochester an attractive option for London workers seeking better value housing.

Road Networks

The M2 motorway runs close to Rochester, providing quick access to London, Canterbury, and the Channel ports. Junction 2 serves the Medway Towns directly, while Junction 1 offers an alternative route depending on your specific location. The A2 runs parallel to the M2, serving as both a local route and a connection to towns like Gravesend and Dartford.

For local journeys, the A228 connects Rochester to Tonbridge and the Weald of Kent, while the A229 provides access to Maidstone and the M20 corridor. Traffic can be heavy during peak hours, particularly around the Medway Tunnel and bridges, so factor this into your daily planning.

Local Bus Services

Arriva operates the majority of local bus services, connecting Rochester to surrounding towns and villages throughout Medway and Kent. The 101 and 141 routes provide frequent services to Maidstone, while various routes connect to Gravesend, Dartford, and other Medway towns. Most services run regularly throughout the day, with reduced evening and Sunday schedules.

Schools and Education in Rochester

Primary Schools

Rochester offers numerous primary school options, both state and independent. Warren Wood Primary School consistently receives strong Ofsted ratings and serves families across central Rochester. St Peter's Catholic Primary provides faith-based education, while Riverside Primary serves the Strood area with excellent facilities and community links.

Many families specifically relocate to Rochester to access these schools, so consider application deadlines and catchment areas early in your moving process. The local authority provides clear guidance on school admissions, and most schools welcome visits from prospective families.

Secondary Education

The Rochester area features several well-regarded secondary schools. The Rochester Grammar School for Girls and Sir Joseph Williamson's Mathematical School offer selective education for high-achieving students. For non-selective options, Chatham Grammar School for Girls and Fort Pitt Grammar School provide excellent education with strong university progression rates.

The University for the Creative Arts has a campus in Rochester, specialising in film, television, and digital media courses. This brings a youthful energy to the city and provides excellent cultural programming throughout the year.

Healthcare and Medical Services

Medway NHS Foundation Trust operates Medway Maritime Hospital, providing comprehensive healthcare services to Rochester residents. The hospital offers accident and emergency services, maternity care, and specialist departments covering most medical needs. Private healthcare options include BMI The Blackheath Hospital, easily accessible from Rochester.

Primary care services are well-distributed throughout Rochester, with GP practices in most residential areas. Dental services, opticians, and pharmacies are readily available, particularly along the High Street and in local shopping areas. Mental health support services operate through Kent and Medway NHS Trust, with local access points throughout the area.

Shopping and Daily Amenities

Local Shopping

Rochester High Street maintains its independent character while providing essential services. Local butchers, bakers, and delicatessens operate alongside national chains, creating a genuine community feel. The weekly market brings fresh produce and local crafts to the city centre, usually operating on Saturdays.

For larger shopping needs, Chatham offers a substantial town centre with major retailers and a covered shopping centre. Retail parks in Strood and Gillingham provide furniture stores, DIY outlets, and large supermarkets with ample parking.

Supermarkets and Food Shopping

Most major supermarket chains operate in the Rochester area. Tesco, Sainsbury's, and ASDA all have large stores within easy reach, while smaller Tesco Express and Co-op stores serve local neighbourhoods. Waitrose operates from nearby areas for those preferring premium groceries.

Local food markets and farm shops provide fresh, seasonal produce. The Rochester Farmers Market showcases Kent's agricultural heritage, while several local farms offer pick-your-own facilities during growing seasons.

Recreation and Leisure Activities

Historical Attractions

Rochester Castle stands as one of the finest examples of Norman architecture in England, offering stunning views across the Medway valley. Rochester Cathedral, founded in 604 AD, provides both historical interest and regular cultural events. The annual Dickens Festival transforms the High Street into a Victorian wonderland, attracting visitors from across the UK.

Guided walks explore Rochester's Roman heritage, medieval streets, and Dickensian connections. The city's compact size makes it perfect for exploring on foot, with most attractions within easy walking distance of each other.

Parks and Green Spaces

Eastgate House Gardens provide a peaceful retreat in the city centre, while Castle Gardens surround the historic castle with landscaped grounds perfect for family activities. For larger green spaces, Great Lines Heritage Park offers extensive walking trails, sports facilities, and regular events throughout the year.

The Medway estuary provides opportunities for water sports, fishing, and riverside walks. Several local sailing clubs welcome new members, while the river supports rowing, kayaking, and other water-based activities.

Sports and Fitness

Medway Park in Gillingham offers comprehensive sports facilities including swimming pools, courts, and fitness suites. Local football clubs operate throughout the Medway Towns, with Gillingham FC representing the area in professional football. Rugby, cricket, and tennis clubs provide options for most sporting interests.

Private gyms and fitness studios operate throughout Rochester, while the surrounding countryside offers excellent cycling and walking opportunities. The Medway Valley Walk provides a scenic route through local villages and countryside.

Cost of Living in Rochester

Housing Costs

Property prices in Rochester remain more affordable than many Kent locations while offering excellent transport links to London. Three-bedroom family homes typically range from £300,000 to £500,000 depending on location and condition. Victorian terraces in the city centre often provide character properties at competitive prices, while modern developments offer contemporary living with parking and gardens.

Rental properties cater to various budgets, from city centre apartments to family homes in residential areas. The presence of commuters to London maintains steady rental demand, while local employment provides additional market stability.

Daily Expenses

Day-to-day costs in Rochester reflect typical South East England pricing, though local markets and independent shops often provide better value than national chains. Fuel costs align with regional averages, while council tax rates remain competitive compared to neighbouring authorities.

Eating out offers good variety and value, from traditional pubs serving local ales to international restaurants reflecting the area's cultural diversity. Entertainment costs remain reasonable, with local theatres and cinemas offering regular promotions and discounts.

Planning Your Move to Rochester

Timing Your Relocation

Consider school term times if you have children, as this affects both school applications and settling-in periods. The property market typically shows more activity in spring and early autumn, which can affect both buying and selling timelines.

Summer months offer the best weather for moving, though removal companies often experience higher demand during school holidays. Booking your removal service well in advance ensures better availability and pricing, particularly for weekend moves.

Essential Services Setup

Contact utility providers early to arrange connections for gas, electricity, and water services. Internet and phone services may require installation appointments, particularly for newly built properties. Local providers often offer competitive packages for new residents.

Register with local services including GP practices, dentists, and schools as soon as possible after your move. Many services have waiting lists, so early registration ensures quicker access when needed.

Getting to Know Your New Area

Join local Facebook groups and community organisations to connect with neighbours and discover local recommendations. The Rochester and Chatham areas have active community networks that welcome newcomers and provide valuable local knowledge.

Explore your immediate neighbourhood on foot to discover local shops, services, and transport links. Most residents find Rochester remarkably walkable, with most daily needs accessible without requiring a car.

Making Rochester Your Home

Moving to Rochester Kent offers the perfect balance of historical character, modern convenience, and excellent connectivity. Whether you're relocating for work, family reasons, or simply seeking a change of pace, this guide provides the foundation for making an informed decision about your move.

The key to a successful relocation lies in careful planning and choosing the right support services. From understanding local neighbourhoods to timing your move effectively, every detail contributes to a smooth transition to your new life in Rochester.
