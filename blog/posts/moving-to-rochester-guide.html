Area Guide

Moving to Rochester — the complete newcomer's guide to living in the city

📅 15 June 2026 🕐 6 min read 👥 Medway & Kent Removals Team

Moving to Rochester Kent offers you the chance to live in one of England's most historically rich cities while enjoying excellent transport links to London and the surrounding areas. As a local removal company with years of experience helping families relocate to Rochester Medway, we understand exactly what newcomers need to know about making this beautiful cathedral city their home. From understanding the best neighbourhoods to finding local services and getting to grips with transport options, this living in Rochester guide covers everything you need for a successful move.

Why Choose Rochester as Your New Home

Rochester sits perfectly positioned in the heart of Medway, offering residents the best of both worlds. You get the charm and character of a historic cathedral city combined with modern amenities and excellent connectivity. The city forms part of the larger Medway Towns alongside Chatham, Gillingham, and Strood, giving you access to a wide range of facilities and services across the area.

The presence of Rochester Cathedral and Castle creates a stunning backdrop for daily life, while the high street offers independent shops, cafes, and restaurants that give the city a genuine community feel. Many of our clients choose Rochester specifically because it maintains a smaller city atmosphere while providing easy access to London for work.

House prices in Rochester typically offer better value than London and many surrounding Kent areas, making it particularly attractive for first-time buyers and families looking to upsize. The local property market includes everything from period terraced houses near the cathedral to modern developments on the outskirts of the city.

Understanding Rochester's Neighbourhoods

Rochester City Centre

The city centre area around the High Street and near Rochester Cathedral offers character properties, many dating back several centuries. Living here puts you within walking distance of shops, restaurants, and cultural attractions. Properties tend to be terraced houses and converted flats, perfect for professionals or couples who want to be in the heart of things.

Parking can be challenging in the very centre, so consider this if you rely heavily on a car. However, the railway station is easily walkable, making this area ideal for London commuters.

Troy Town and New Road Area

These residential areas offer a mix of Victorian and Edwardian houses, many with gardens and off-street parking. The area provides good access to local schools and is popular with families. You'll find local shops and services along New Road, creating a pleasant neighbourhood feel.

This part of Rochester offers more space than city centre properties while keeping you close to all amenities. Many of our clients moving with children specifically request this area for its family-friendly atmosphere.

Borstal and Temple Farm

On the outskirts of Rochester, these areas feature more modern housing developments alongside established residential streets. Borstal offers good value family homes with gardens, while Temple Farm provides newer properties with modern layouts and parking.

These neighbourhoods work well for families who need more space and don't mind a short drive or bus ride to reach Rochester city centre. The areas have good access to local primary schools and green spaces.

Transport and Connectivity

Railway Connections

Rochester railway station provides direct services to London Victoria, Cannon Street, and Charing Cross, with journey times around 45-50 minutes to central London. This makes Rochester an excellent choice for London commuters who want to escape city prices while maintaining reasonable travel times.

The station also connects you to other parts of Kent, including Canterbury, Margate, and Dover, making weekend trips and visits to family easy to manage. Southeastern operates the services, with trains running frequently throughout the day.

Road Links

The A2 runs directly past Rochester, providing fast road access to London and Dover. Junction 2 of the M25 is about 30 minutes away, connecting you to the national motorway network. For local travel, the A289 links Rochester to other Medway towns and the M2 motorway.

Driving to central London typically takes 45-60 minutes outside peak hours, though traffic can be heavy during rush periods. Many residents find the train more reliable for London commuting while using cars for local travel and weekend trips.

Local Transport

Arriva provides local bus services connecting Rochester to Chatham, Gillingham, Strood, and surrounding areas. The Medway towns are well connected by public transport, so you can easily access facilities across the whole area without needing a car.

Rochester city centre is compact and very walkable. Most residents find they can walk to shops, restaurants, and local amenities easily. Cycling is also popular, with relatively flat terrain and increasingly bike-friendly routes.

Schools and Education

Primary Schools

Rochester offers several well-regarded primary schools. Warren Wood Primary School consistently receives good ratings and serves the Borstal area. St Margaret's Church of England Primary School provides denominational education in the city centre area.

For families moving to Rochester, research school catchment areas early, as popular schools can become oversubscribed. Many of our clients specifically choose their Rochester neighbourhood based on school proximity and quality.

Secondary Education

The Rochester area includes access to several secondary schools across Medway. The Math School in Rochester specialises in mathematics education and attracts students from across the region. Thomas Aveling School in Rochester provides comprehensive education with good results.

Kent operates a selective education system, so grammar schools are available for children who pass the 11-plus examination. Grammar schools in nearby areas include Sir Joseph Williamson's Mathematical School in Rochester and others across Medway.

Further Education

The University of Greenwich has a campus in nearby Chatham Maritime, offering degree courses and creating a student presence in the Medway area. MidKent College provides vocational training and adult education courses across multiple Medway locations.

Shopping and Local Amenities

High Street Shopping

Rochester High Street maintains a good mix of independent shops and national retailers. You'll find charity shops, antique stores, bookshops, and cafes that give the street character and personality. The weekly market adds to the traditional market town atmosphere.

For everyday shopping, there are supermarkets within easy reach, including larger stores in nearby Chatham and Strood. This combination means you can do both regular grocery shopping and find unique items locally.

Chatham Maritime and Dockside

Just a short drive from Rochester, Chatham Maritime offers larger retail outlets, restaurants, and entertainment options. The Dockside Designer Outlet provides brand shopping opportunities, while the area also includes cinema and family entertainment options.

Many Rochester residents use Chatham Maritime for bigger shopping trips while keeping their day-to-day shopping local to Rochester itself. The area also offers extensive parking, making it convenient for car-based shopping trips.

Healthcare and Medical Services

GP Practices and Medical Care

Rochester has several GP practices serving different areas of the city. When moving to Rochester Kent, registering with a local GP should be an early priority. The Riverside Practice and Rochester Medical Practice are among the established surgeries in the area.

Dental services are available locally, though popular practices can have waiting lists for NHS treatment. Private dental care is also available if you prefer shorter waiting times or specific treatments.

Hospital Services

Medway Maritime Hospital in Gillingham provides full hospital services for the Rochester area, including accident and emergency care. The hospital serves all of Medway and is easily accessible from Rochester by car or public transport.

For specialist services not available locally, London hospitals are reachable within an hour, and Maidstone Hospital provides additional services within Kent.

Recreation and Leisure

Historic Attractions

Living in Rochester means having Rochester Cathedral and Rochester Castle literally on your doorstep. These aren't just tourist attractions but become part of your daily environment, creating a unique sense of place and history.

The annual Rochester festivals, including the Dickens Festival and Sweeps Festival, bring the city to life throughout the year. As a resident, you can enjoy these events without the hassle of traveling and finding accommodation.

Parks and Green Spaces

Eastgate House Gardens provide a peaceful green space right in the city centre. For larger outdoor recreation, The Vines park offers open space for walking and relaxation close to the cathedral.

The River Medway provides riverside walks and water activities. Many residents enjoy walking along the river, and there are opportunities for boating and water sports if that interests you.

Sports and Fitness

Rochester has local gyms and fitness facilities, while the nearby areas of Medway offer additional sports centres and recreational facilities. The area supports local football clubs and sports groups that welcome new members.

Golf courses in the surrounding Kent countryside provide excellent facilities within a short drive of Rochester. The relatively flat terrain also makes Rochester good for cycling and running.

Practical Moving Considerations

Council Services and Council Tax

Rochester falls under Medway Council, which provides all local government services including refuse collection, council tax, and local planning. Council tax rates in Medway tend to be reasonable compared to many other areas of Kent and the South East.

When moving to Rochester Medway, you'll need to register for council tax and arrange bin collections. The council provides good online services for most administrative tasks, making the moving process smoother.

Utilities and Internet

All major utility providers serve Rochester, giving you choice in gas, electricity, and water suppliers. Internet coverage is generally good, with fibre broadband available in most areas. This is particularly important if you plan to work from home or need reliable internet for London-based remote work.

When arranging your move, coordinate utility transfers early to ensure services are connected when you arrive. Most providers can arrange seamless transfers if you give adequate notice.

Parking and Residents' Permits

Rochester city centre has controlled parking zones where residents can apply for permits. If you're moving to a central location, check whether your new address qualifies for residents' parking permits and apply early.

Many residential areas outside the immediate centre offer on-street parking or properties with driveways. Factor parking arrangements into your house-hunting decisions, especially if you have multiple cars or work vehicles.

Cost of Living in Rochester

Overall living costs in Rochester compare favourably to London while being realistic for Kent. Housing costs vary significantly depending on the area and property type, but generally offer better value than many commuter towns closer to London.

Local shopping, dining, and services are reasonably priced, helping keep daily living costs manageable. The combination of good value and excellent transport links makes Rochester particularly attractive for London workers looking to improve their quality of life without massive increases in commuting time.

Commuting costs to London need factoring into your budget, but many residents find the savings on housing more than compensate for travel expenses. Season tickets offer the best value for regular London commuters.

Getting Settled in Rochester

The key to successfully settling in Rochester is getting involved in local community life. Rochester has active community groups, local societies, and regular events that help newcomers meet people and feel at home quickly.

Local Facebook groups and community boards provide excellent ways to ask questions, find recommendations, and connect with neighbours. Many of our clients find these online communities invaluable during their first few months in Rochester.

Take time to explore the local area properly. Walk the High Street, visit the cathedral, try local restaurants, and discover the shops and services you'll use regularly. Rochester rewards residents who engage with what the city offers rather than just using it as a dormitory town.

Your move to Rochester represents an opportunity to enjoy historic surroundings, reasonable living costs, and excellent connectivity to London and the rest of Kent. The city offers genuine community spirit alongside modern conveniences, making it an excellent choice for individuals, couples, and families looking for quality of life improvements.
