Moving to Rochester — the complete newcomer's guide to living in the city

📅 27 May 2026 🕐 6 min read 👥 Medway & Kent Removals Team

Moving to Rochester Kent opens the door to a unique blend of ancient history and modern convenience. As England's second oldest cathedral city, Rochester offers newcomers a rich cultural heritage alongside excellent transport links to London and the rest of Kent. Whether you're relocating for work, family, or simply seeking a change of pace, this historic Medway town provides an appealing balance of urban amenities and small-city charm that continues to attract new residents year after year.

Why Choose Rochester for Your New Home

Rochester sits at the heart of the Medway Towns, perfectly positioned between London and the Kent coast. The city's appeal lies in its accessibility - you can reach central London in just 35 minutes by train, while the beautiful Kent countryside and coastal towns remain within easy reach for weekend adventures.

The housing market in Rochester offers excellent value compared to London prices, with Victorian terraces, modern developments, and period properties providing options for different budgets and preferences. The local schools receive strong ratings, making Rochester particularly attractive to families considering a move from the capital or other parts of the UK.

Rochester Cathedral and Castle dominate the skyline, creating a sense of permanence and history that many new towns lack. This historical backdrop doesn't come at the expense of modern conveniences - the High Street features major retailers, independent shops, restaurants, and cafes that cater to contemporary lifestyles.

Understanding Rochester's Neighbourhoods

Rochester City Centre

The city centre revolves around the historic High Street, running from the Cathedral to the River Medway. Living here puts you within walking distance of shops, restaurants, and transport links. The area features a mix of converted period buildings and modern apartments, with many properties offering views of the Cathedral or Castle.

Parking can be challenging in the very centre, but residents often appreciate being able to walk everywhere. The Friday and Saturday markets bring additional life to the High Street, while the annual Dickens Festival transforms the area into a Victorian wonderland each December.

Borstal and Temple Manor

These residential areas sit on higher ground south of the centre, offering panoramic views across the Medway Valley. Borstal features mainly Victorian and Edwardian houses, many with generous gardens and period features intact. The area attracts families and professionals who want space while maintaining easy access to the city centre.

Temple Manor, named after the historic Templar building, includes newer developments alongside established residential streets. The area benefits from good bus links and remains within comfortable walking distance of Rochester station for commuters.

Watts Avenue and Surrounding Streets

This popular residential area features tree-lined streets with 1930s semi-detached houses that appeal to first-time buyers and growing families. The properties typically offer three bedrooms, front and rear gardens, and off-street parking - practical benefits that make daily life easier.

Local schools receive good ratings, while Watts Avenue itself provides a pleasant walking route towards the city centre. The area strikes a balance between suburban comfort and urban convenience that many newcomers find appealing.

Transport and Commuting from Rochester

Rochester's transport connections make it genuinely practical for commuting to London while enjoying lower living costs and a relaxed pace of life. The main railway station provides regular services to London Victoria, London Bridge, and St Pancras International, with journey times ranging from 35 to 50 minutes depending on your destination and service type.

The M2 and A2 provide direct road links to London, Canterbury, and Dover, though traffic can be heavy during peak hours. Many residents find the train more reliable for London commuting, while keeping cars for local travel and weekend trips around Kent.

Local bus services connect Rochester with neighbouring Medway towns including Chatham, Gillingham, and Strood. The Medway towns essentially function as one large urban area, so good local transport opens up employment, shopping, and leisure opportunities beyond Rochester itself.

For international travel, the Eurostar terminal at Ebbsfleet is easily accessible by car, while both Gatwick and Heathrow airports can be reached within 90 minutes during normal traffic conditions.

Schools and Education in Rochester

Rochester offers strong educational options from primary through secondary level. The King's School Rochester, an independent institution with ancient foundations, shares grounds with the Cathedral and maintains an excellent reputation. Several good state primary schools serve different areas of the city, with many receiving positive Ofsted ratings.

For secondary education, local comprehensives include The Math School and Rochester Grammar School for girls, which requires entrance exams. Kent still operates a selective system, so families should research grammar school options early if this interests them.

The nearby Universities at Greenwich (Medway Campus) and Canterbury Christ Church provide higher education options, while London universities remain accessible for students willing to commute.

Many families appreciate that good schools don't require the premium house prices often found in other commuter areas, making Rochester particularly attractive for those balancing education quality with affordability.

Shopping and Amenities

Rochester High Street combines national chains with independent retailers, creating variety without the sterile feel of purpose-built shopping centres. Waterstones occupies a beautiful period building, while charity shops, cafes, and specialist stores fill the gaps between familiar names like Boots and WH Smith.

For larger shopping trips, Chatham's Pentagon Centre sits just ten minutes away and houses major retailers including Next, Primark, and Argos. Blue Water shopping centre near Dartford provides designer outlets and extensive dining options for special trips, accessible by car in about 30 minutes.

The weekly markets bring fresh produce, local crafts, and general goods to the High Street, maintaining traditions that stretch back centuries. Many residents enjoy supporting local traders while picking up fresh vegetables, flowers, or household items at reasonable prices.

Banking, postal services, and healthcare facilities all operate within easy reach of residential areas. The NHS provides local GP surgeries, while Medway Hospital in nearby Gillingham handles more serious medical needs.

Recreation and Culture

Rochester Castle offers free entry to its grounds and provides a stunning backdrop for walks and picnics. The Cathedral welcomes visitors and hosts regular concerts and special events throughout the year. Both landmarks make Rochester feel special in ways that newer towns simply cannot match.

The Guildhall Museum tells local stories and frequently changes exhibitions to maintain interest for residents. The nearby Historic Dockyard Chatham, just a short drive away, provides a fascinating day out exploring maritime history and preserved warships.

For outdoor activities, the Medway Estuary offers walking and cycling routes with river views and wildlife watching opportunities. Several parks within Rochester provide green space for families, while the wider Kent countryside opens up endless possibilities for weekend exploration.

The annual Dickens Festival celebrates Rochester's connection to Charles Dickens, who lived locally and set several novels in thinly disguised versions of the city. This event brings thousands of visitors and creates a real sense of community pride among residents.

Practical Moving Considerations

When planning your move to Rochester, consider timing your relocation to avoid peak periods if possible. Summer months tend to be busiest for removal companies, while school holidays create additional challenges for families with children.

Parking restrictions in the city centre require careful planning on moving day. Many streets have permit parking for residents, while loading restrictions apply during certain hours. Professional removal companies familiar with Rochester can advise on the best approach for your specific location.

Council tax rates in Medway remain competitive compared to many areas, particularly considering the proximity to London. However, research the exact band for your chosen property to budget accurately for ongoing costs.

Utility connections usually proceed smoothly, but allow time for internet installation if you work from home. The area receives good coverage from major providers, though rural parts of Kent can experience slower speeds.

Getting Settled in Your New Community

Rochester's size makes it easy to establish local connections quickly. The Cathedral runs community groups and events, while local sports clubs welcome new members across various activities and skill levels. Many residents find volunteering an excellent way to meet people while contributing to their new community.

GP registration should happen promptly after moving, as popular practices sometimes have waiting lists. Dental practices also vary in availability, so research options early rather than waiting for problems to arise.

Local Facebook groups and community websites help newcomers stay informed about events, recommendations, and local issues. The Medway Messenger provides local news coverage, while Rochester-specific groups often share tips about everything from reliable tradespeople to family-friendly activities.

Banking can usually be managed online, but visiting local branches helps establish relationships for future needs like mortgages or business banking. The High Street includes most major banks, making routine transactions convenient.
