Area Guide

Moving to Rochester — the complete newcomer's guide to living in the city

📅 8 June 2026 🕐 6 min read 👥 Medway & Kent Removals Team

Rochester stands as one of Kent's most historic and appealing places to call home, combining centuries of fascinating heritage with modern amenities and excellent transport connections. If you're considering moving to Rochester Kent, this detailed guide will help you understand what makes this ancient city such an attractive choice for families, professionals, and retirees alike. From its magnificent cathedral to its thriving high street, Rochester offers a unique blend of old and new that creates a truly special living experience.

Why Choose Rochester as Your New Home

Rochester's appeal lies in its perfect balance of history, convenience, and community spirit. The city boasts over 1,400 years of recorded history, with Roman ruins, medieval architecture, and Victorian grandeur creating a stunning backdrop to daily life. Yet this isn't just a museum piece – Rochester is a living, working city with excellent schools, growing business opportunities, and strong transport links to London and the rest of Kent.

The proximity to the River Medway adds natural beauty to the urban landscape, while the surrounding countryside provides easy access to walking trails, cycling routes, and green spaces. For those relocating to Rochester Medway, the combination of cultural attractions, practical amenities, and strong community networks makes settling in straightforward and rewarding.

Understanding Rochester's Geography and Neighborhoods

Central Rochester

The heart of Rochester centers around the High Street and the cathedral precinct. This area offers a mix of period properties, from Georgian terraces to Victorian cottages, many with stunning views of the castle or cathedral. Living in central Rochester means walking distance to shops, restaurants, and cultural attractions, though parking can be challenging and property prices tend to be higher.

Borstal and Temple Hill

These residential areas on Rochester's eastern edge provide excellent value for money, with a good selection of semi-detached houses and some newer developments. Borstal offers panoramic views across the Medway valley, while Temple Hill provides easy access to the M2 motorway for commuters.

Higham and Lower Higham

For those seeking a more rural feel while remaining within easy reach of Rochester's amenities, Higham offers village-style living with excellent rail connections. The area features a mix of period cottages, modern family homes, and some stunning properties with countryside views.

Strood

Technically a separate town but closely connected to Rochester, Strood provides more affordable housing options and excellent transport links. The area has undergone significant regeneration in recent years, with new shopping facilities and improved infrastructure making it increasingly popular with young families.

Transport and Commuting from Rochester

One of Rochester's greatest strengths is its exceptional transport connectivity. The city sits at the intersection of major road and rail networks, making it ideal for commuters and those who need to travel regularly for work or leisure.

Rail Connections

Rochester railway station provides direct services to London Victoria, London St Pancras International, and Cannon Street, with journey times of approximately 45 minutes to central London. The High Speed 1 service via Ebbsfleet means you can reach St Pancras in just over 30 minutes, making Rochester particularly attractive for London commuters.

Local rail services connect Rochester to other Medway towns including Chatham, Gillingham, and Rainham, while longer-distance services reach Canterbury, Margate, and Dover. This excellent rail network means you can easily explore the rest of Kent without needing a car.

Road Networks

The M2 motorway runs close to Rochester, providing quick access to the M25 and connections to London, Canterbury, and the Channel ports. The A2 offers an alternative route to London and connects Rochester to Gravesend, Dartford, and beyond. Local road networks link Rochester efficiently to other Medway towns and to Maidstone, the county town of Kent.

Local Transport

Bus services within Rochester and to surrounding areas are reliable and frequent. The town center is compact and very walkable, with most amenities within easy reach on foot. For cyclists, Rochester offers improving cycle infrastructure and access to the excellent cycle paths along the River Medway.

Educational Opportunities in Rochester

Families moving to Rochester will find a strong selection of educational options at all levels. The area is home to both outstanding state schools and well-regarded independent institutions, making it attractive for families with children of all ages.

Primary Education

Rochester has several highly-rated primary schools, including Warren Wood Primary School, which consistently receives excellent Ofsted ratings. St Bartholomew's C of E Primary School offers faith-based education in a historic setting, while Riverside Primary School provides modern facilities and innovative teaching approaches.

Secondary Schools

The Rochester area benefits from access to several outstanding secondary schools. The Rochester Grammar School is one of Kent's most prestigious selective schools, while Holcombe Grammar School offers excellent education for girls. For non-selective education, Warren Wood Academy and The Thomas Aveling School both provide comprehensive education with strong academic results.

Further and Higher Education

MidKent College has a campus in Medway, offering vocational courses and apprenticeships. The University of Greenwich's Medway campus, located nearby in Chatham, provides undergraduate and postgraduate degrees in various subjects. Canterbury Christ Church University also has a Medway campus, expanding higher education opportunities locally.

Employment and Business Opportunities

Rochester's economy combines historic industries with modern business sectors, creating diverse employment opportunities for residents. The city's location and transport links also make it an excellent base for those working elsewhere in Kent or London.

Key Employment Sectors

The Medway area, including Rochester, has a strong maritime and engineering heritage that continues today. BAE Systems, one of the area's largest employers, provides high-skilled engineering and defense jobs. The growing technology sector includes software companies and digital businesses attracted by the area's connectivity and lower costs compared to London.

Public sector employment is significant, with Medway Council, NHS trusts, and educational institutions providing stable career opportunities. The tourism industry, centered around Rochester's historic attractions, supports numerous hospitality and retail jobs.

Business Environment

Rochester offers an increasingly attractive environment for businesses, with lower commercial rents than London while maintaining excellent transport links. The Innovation Centre Medway supports technology startups and growing businesses, while the historic setting attracts creative industries and heritage-related enterprises.

Shopping, Dining, and Entertainment

Living in Rochester means access to a diverse range of shopping, dining, and entertainment options that blend historic charm with modern convenience.

Shopping

Rochester High Street retains its historic character while offering modern shopping facilities. Independent boutiques, antique shops, and specialist stores sit alongside well-known chain stores. The nearby Pentagon Centre in Chatham provides major retail outlets and department stores for larger shopping trips, while Bluewater shopping center is easily accessible for those seeking designer brands and extensive retail choice.

Dining Scene

Rochester's restaurant scene reflects its diverse community and historic setting. The Old Bakery Restaurant offers fine dining in a beautifully restored medieval building, while Zucchini Italian Restaurant provides excellent family-friendly dining. The city also boasts numerous pubs with character, including several that claim connections to Charles Dickens, who lived in nearby Higham.

For casual dining, Rochester High Street offers everything from traditional fish and chips to contemporary cafes and international cuisine. The regular farmers' markets and food festivals add to the culinary appeal of the area.

Cultural Attractions and Entertainment

Rochester Castle and Cathedral dominate the city's skyline and provide year-round attractions for residents and visitors. The annual Dickens Festival transforms the city into a Victorian wonderland, while the Sweeps Festival celebrates traditional Morris dancing and folk culture.

The Guildhall Museum offers insights into local history, while the nearby Historic Dockyard Chatham provides a fascinating look at maritime heritage. Regular concerts, theater productions, and art exhibitions ensure there's always cultural entertainment available.

Healthcare and Medical Services

Rochester residents have access to comprehensive healthcare services through both NHS and private providers. Medway Maritime Hospital, located in nearby Gillingham, provides full hospital services including accident and emergency care. Several GP practices serve the Rochester area, with many offering online booking and extended hours to accommodate working patients.

Dental services are well-represented, with both NHS and private dentists available. Specialist services, including physiotherapy, opticians, and complementary health practitioners, are readily available throughout the area.

Recreational Activities and Green Spaces

Despite its urban setting, Rochester offers excellent access to green spaces and recreational activities. The River Medway provides opportunities for walking, cycling, and water sports, with the Medway Valley Walk offering scenic routes through the surrounding countryside.

Rochester's parks include the peaceful Castle Gardens, which provide a green oasis in the city center with stunning views of the castle and cathedral. Nearby Ranscombe Farm Reserve offers 600 acres of countryside perfect for walking and wildlife watching.

Sports facilities include several golf courses, tennis courts, and fitness centers. The nearby North Kent coast provides beaches and coastal walks, while the Kent Downs Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty is easily accessible for more adventurous outdoor activities.

Practical Considerations for Your Move

Council Services

Rochester falls under Medway Council, a unitary authority that provides all local government services. Council tax rates are competitive compared to other areas in the South East, and the council provides good online services for managing accounts and accessing information.

Utilities and Services

All major utility providers serve Rochester, with competitive options for gas, electricity, and broadband services. The area benefits from good broadband infrastructure, with fiber optic services available in most areas. Water services are provided by Southern Water.

Cost of Living

Rochester offers excellent value for money compared to London, while still providing easy access to the capital. Housing costs are reasonable for the South East, though they vary significantly between different areas and property types. Daily expenses including shopping, dining, and entertainment are generally lower than London prices while maintaining good quality and choice.
