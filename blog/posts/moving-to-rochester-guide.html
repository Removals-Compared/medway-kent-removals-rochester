Moving to Rochester — the complete newcomer's guide to living in the city

📅 22 May 2026 🕐 6 min read 👥 Medway & Kent Removals Team

Rochester has been drawing new residents for over 2,000 years, and it's easy to see why. This historic city in Kent offers an unbeatable combination of ancient heritage, modern amenities, and excellent transport links to London. Whether you're moving from another part of Kent, relocating from London for more space and better value, or coming from further afield, this guide will help you understand what makes Rochester such an appealing place to call home.

Why Choose Rochester for Your Next Move?

Rochester sits at the heart of the Medway Towns, perfectly positioned between London and the Kent coast. The city offers something genuinely special: you can walk past a Norman cathedral and castle on your way to catch a train that gets you to London Victoria in under an hour. For families, professionals, and retirees alike, Rochester delivers quality of life that's hard to match elsewhere in the Southeast.

The housing market here offers better value than many comparable areas closer to London, while still providing excellent connectivity. You'll find everything from period Georgian townhouses near the historic quarter to modern family homes in the surrounding areas of Strood and Chatham. The local schools consistently perform well, green spaces are plentiful, and the sense of community remains strong despite the city's growth.

Understanding Rochester's Geography and Neighborhoods

Rochester City Centre

The historic heart of Rochester clusters around the High Street, dominated by the magnificent cathedral and castle. Living here puts you within walking distance of independent shops, restaurants, and cultural attractions. The Georgian and Victorian terraced houses in this area are highly sought after, though parking can be challenging. This area suits those who prioritize character properties and don't mind paying a premium for the historic setting.

Strood

Just across the River Medway, Strood offers more modern housing options and excellent transport links. The area has seen significant regeneration in recent years, with new developments providing contemporary family homes. Strood is particularly popular with London commuters due to its railway station and slightly lower property prices compared to central Rochester.

Borstal and Wouldham

These areas on Rochester's outskirts provide a more rural feel while remaining within easy reach of the city centre. Borstal offers stunning views across the Medway Valley, while Wouldham village maintains a distinctly Kentish character. Both areas are excellent for families seeking larger properties with gardens and a slower pace of life.

The Delce and Walderslade

Popular residential areas that developed significantly in the latter half of the 20th century, these neighborhoods offer good family housing, local amenities, and easy access to both Rochester and Chatham. The areas feature a mix of housing types, from starter homes to larger detached properties.

Transport Links and Commuting Options

Rochester's transport connections make it an attractive base for both local workers and London commuters. The city sits on the main railway line from London Victoria to Dover, with regular services throughout the day. During peak hours, you'll find trains roughly every 15-20 minutes, with journey times to London Victoria typically around 50 minutes to an hour.

For road travel, the M2 and A2 provide direct routes to London and the rest of Kent. The M25 is easily accessible for connections to other parts of the Southeast. However, like much of Kent, traffic can be heavy during peak periods, particularly on the A2 approaching London.

Local transport within the Medway area includes regular bus services connecting Rochester with Chatham, Gillingham, and surrounding towns. Many residents find cycling a practical option for local journeys, with increasing investment in cycle paths and bike-friendly infrastructure.

Schools and Education in Rochester

Families moving to Rochester will find a good selection of educational options. The area benefits from both well-regarded state schools and several independent institutions. Rochester Grammar School and Rochester Girls Grammar School are selective state schools with excellent reputations, though places are competitive.

For primary education, schools like St Margaret's Rochester and Cuxton Infant School consistently receive positive Ofsted ratings. The King's School Rochester, founded in 604 AD and one of the oldest schools in the world, offers independent education from nursery through to sixth form.

The nearby Universities at Greenwich (Medway Campus) and Canterbury Christ Church (Medway Campus) provide higher education opportunities within the area, contributing to Rochester's academic atmosphere and offering part-time and continuing education options for adult learners.

Shopping and Amenities

Rochester High Street maintains much of its historic charm while offering practical shopping options. You'll find a mixture of chain stores and independent retailers, including several excellent bookshops that capitalize on the city's literary connections to Charles Dickens.

For larger shopping needs, Chatham's Pentagon Centre and Bluewater (about 20 minutes by car) provide extensive retail options. The weekly Rochester Farmers Market brings local produce and artisan goods to the cathedral grounds, while Rochester Market in the High Street operates several days a week.

The city has seen growth in its restaurant and cafe scene, with options ranging from traditional pub meals to international cuisine. The historic setting attracts quality establishments, and the proximity to Kent's farming areas means many restaurants can source excellent local ingredients.

Healthcare Services

Medway Maritime Hospital in nearby Gillingham serves as the main NHS hospital for the area, providing comprehensive medical services including accident and emergency facilities. The hospital has undergone significant investment in recent years and serves the entire Medway Towns area effectively.

Rochester has several well-established GP practices, and NHS dental services are generally available, though as elsewhere in the Southeast, you may need to join waiting lists for some services. Private healthcare options are available locally, and London's world-class medical facilities remain easily accessible for specialist treatments.

Recreation and Leisure Activities

Living in Rochester means having access to an impressive range of leisure activities. The River Medway offers opportunities for boating, fishing, and riverside walks. The Esplanade provides pleasant walking routes with views across to Strood and the surrounding countryside.

Rochester Castle grounds and Cathedral Gardens offer green space right in the city centre, while larger parks like Eastgate House Gardens provide areas for families to relax. For more extensive outdoor activities, the North Downs are easily accessible, offering excellent walking and cycling opportunities.

The city's cultural calendar includes the famous Rochester Dickens Festival, the Sweeps Festival, and regular concerts at the cathedral. The Central Theatre in nearby Chatham provides professional productions, while several local groups offer amateur dramatic and musical performances throughout the year.

Cost of Living Considerations

Rochester generally offers better value for money than areas closer to London, while still providing excellent transport links to the capital. Property prices vary significantly depending on the specific area and property type, but you'll typically find more space for your money compared to inner London or prime commuter towns in Surrey.

Council tax rates in Medway are competitive compared to many Kent authorities. Utility costs are standard for the Southeast, though the area's good transport links mean you're not entirely dependent on a car for daily needs, potentially reducing transport costs.

Local services like restaurants and leisure activities generally offer good value, particularly compared to London prices. The farmers market and local suppliers provide opportunities to source quality food at reasonable prices.

What to Consider Before Making Your Move

While Rochester offers many advantages, it's worth considering a few practical points before making your decision. Parking in the historic city centre can be challenging, particularly during tourist season and special events. If you're moving to a period property in the city centre, factor in potential parking costs or the need to rely more heavily on public transport.

The area's popularity with tourists means the city centre can become quite crowded during peak times, particularly during festivals and summer weekends. However, most residents find this adds to rather than detracts from the area's character.

If you're moving from a large city, you might find the selection of shops and services more limited than you're used to, though London remains easily accessible for anything not available locally.

Making Your Move to Rochester Smooth and Stress-Free

Planning your relocation to Rochester requires careful consideration of timing, logistics, and local knowledge. The narrow streets in parts of the historic city centre can present challenges for larger removal vehicles, so it's essential to work with removal professionals who understand the local area.

Consider the timing of your move carefully. The summer months see increased tourist traffic, which can affect accessibility and parking around the city centre. School holiday periods also tend to be busier, so if you're moving to a family area, timing your move during term time might be easier.

Getting familiar with local parking restrictions and permit systems before your move day will save time and potential parking fines. Many historic streets have specific time restrictions or require permits for residents.

The key to a successful relocation lies in proper planning and working with professionals who understand both the opportunities and challenges of moving to this special part of Kent.
