Moving to Rochester — the complete newcomer's guide to living in the city

📅 12 June 2026 🕐 6 min read 👥 Medway & Kent Removals Team

Moving to Rochester in Kent represents one of the smartest decisions you can make for your family's future. This historic Medway town combines centuries of fascinating heritage with modern amenities and excellent transport links to London, making it perfect for professionals, families, and retirees alike. Whether you're relocating from elsewhere in Kent or moving from further afield, Rochester offers an unbeatable blend of character, convenience, and community that newcomers quickly fall in love with.

Why Choose Rochester for Your New Home

Rochester sits at the heart of the Medway Towns, positioned perfectly between London and the Kent coast. The city offers something genuinely special that many other areas simply can't match. You'll find yourself living in a place where Norman castles stand alongside contemporary shopping centres, where cobbled streets lead to modern restaurants, and where you can commute to central London in under an hour.

The housing market here provides excellent value compared to London prices, with Victorian terraces, modern developments, and period properties all available within reasonable budgets. Many newcomers are surprised by how much space they can afford here compared to the capital, often upgrading to larger homes with gardens that seemed impossible in their previous location.

Rochester's location within the wider Medway area means you're perfectly positioned to explore Kent's attractions. Canterbury lies just 30 minutes away by car, while the seaside towns of Whitstable and Herne Bay are easily reachable for weekend trips. The M2 and A2 provide direct routes to London, Dover, and the Channel ports.

Understanding Rochester's Different Areas

Rochester City Centre

The historic heart of Rochester centres around the High Street, where you'll find the magnificent Rochester Cathedral and the imposing Rochester Castle. Living here puts you within walking distance of independent shops, restaurants, and cultural attractions. Properties in this area tend to be period buildings, many converted into flats or carefully restored family homes.

The annual Dickens Festival transforms the city centre, with residents and visitors dressing in Victorian costume to celebrate the area's connection to Charles Dickens. If you enjoy being part of community events and living surrounded by history, the city centre area will appeal strongly.

Borstal and Temple Hill

These residential areas offer more modern housing options, including post-war developments and newer builds. Borstal provides excellent views across the Medway Valley, while Temple Hill offers good transport connections and local amenities. Both areas are popular with families due to their proximity to good schools and green spaces.

Strood

Technically separate from Rochester but closely connected, Strood provides more affordable housing options while maintaining easy access to Rochester's amenities. The area has seen significant regeneration in recent years, with new housing developments and improved transport links making it increasingly attractive to newcomers.

Surrounding Villages

Villages like Cuxton, Halling, and Wouldham offer rural living within easy reach of Rochester. These locations suit those seeking countryside charm while maintaining access to urban amenities. Properties here often come with larger gardens and parking, perfect for families wanting space to grow.

Education Options for Families

Rochester provides excellent educational opportunities from nursery through to university level. The area hosts several outstanding primary schools, including St Nicholas Church of England Primary and Rochester Independent College for older students.

Secondary education options include The Rochester Grammar School, one of Kent's remaining grammar schools with an exceptional academic record. Comprehensive schools in the area also perform well, with many achieving good Ofsted ratings and strong exam results.

For higher education, the nearby Universities at Medway campus hosts courses from the University of Greenwich, Canterbury Christ Church University, and the University of Kent. This means your children can potentially study at university level while remaining close to home.

Many families choose Rochester specifically for its educational advantages, particularly the grammar school system that allows academically able children to thrive in a selective environment.

Transport and Commuting

Rochester railway station provides direct services to London Victoria, Cannon Street, and Charing Cross, with journey times typically between 45-60 minutes. This makes the area extremely popular with London commuters who want to escape high city property prices without sacrificing career opportunities.

The station also connects to other Kent destinations including Canterbury, Margate, and Dover, opening up both employment opportunities and leisure activities across the county. Season tickets represent good value compared to zones closer to London.

Road connections are equally impressive. The M2 motorway runs close to Rochester, providing direct access to London and the Channel ports. The A2 offers an alternative route with good connections to Canterbury and Dover. Local roads connect easily to other Medway towns including Chatham and Gillingham.

Bus services operate regularly throughout Medway and into surrounding areas. Arriva buses provide the main service, connecting Rochester to all major local destinations and many smaller villages.

Shopping and Amenities

Rochester High Street maintains its historic character while offering practical modern shopping. Independent boutiques, antique shops, and specialist retailers create a unique shopping experience you won't find in typical chain-dominated high streets.

For major shopping needs, Chatham's Pentagon Centre lies just minutes away, housing all major retailers including John Lewis, Next, and Marks & Spencer. Bluewater Shopping Centre sits within easy driving distance for larger shopping trips.

The weekly Rochester Farmers Market brings fresh, local produce to the city centre, while several supermarkets provide everyday shopping convenience. Waitrose, Tesco, and Morrison's all operate local stores.

Banking facilities include all major high street banks, while the post office provides standard postal services and government services access. Medical facilities include several GP practices, dental surgeries, and Medway Maritime Hospital for emergency care.

Cultural Life and Entertainment

Rochester's cultural scene centres around its historic connections and literary heritage. The annual Dickens Festival attracts thousands of visitors, while the Sweeps Festival celebrates traditional May Day customs with Morris dancing and street performances.

The Guildhall Museum showcases local history, while Rochester Castle and Cathedral provide regular exhibitions and events. Many residents become actively involved in heritage organizations, contributing to the preservation and celebration of local history.

Restaurants range from traditional pubs serving Kent ales to contemporary dining establishments. The area's food scene has improved significantly in recent years, with several restaurants gaining recognition for quality and innovation.

The nearby Central Theatre in Chatham provides professional productions, while Rochester itself hosts smaller venue performances and community theatre groups. Cinema options include multiplexes in nearby retail parks.

Green Spaces and Recreation

Castle Gardens provide beautiful green space right in the city centre, perfect for picnics and children's play. The gardens offer stunning views of Rochester Cathedral and provide a peaceful retreat from urban life.

The River Medway offers walking and cycling opportunities along its banks, with the Saxon Shore Way long-distance footpath passing through the area. Many residents discover a love for riverside walks after moving to Rochester.

Sports facilities include several golf courses, tennis clubs, and football pitches. The nearby countryside provides excellent opportunities for hiking, with the North Downs Way accessible within short driving distances.

Boat trips operate from Rochester Pier during summer months, offering unique perspectives of the historic waterfront and connecting to other Medway towns by river.

Healthcare and Essential Services

Medway Maritime Hospital provides comprehensive healthcare services including accident and emergency facilities. The hospital has undergone significant investment in recent years, improving facilities and expanding services.

Several GP practices serve Rochester residents, with most accepting new patients. Dental practices, opticians, and pharmacies provide additional healthcare support throughout the area.

Utility connections are straightforward, with standard providers offering gas, electricity, and water services. Internet connectivity is generally good, with fibre broadband available in most areas.

Cost of Living Considerations

Housing costs in Rochester remain significantly lower than London while offering much larger properties and outdoor space. Victorian terraced houses, modern apartments, and family homes all provide options across different budgets.

Council tax rates are reasonable compared to many areas, while utility costs reflect standard UK rates. Transport costs, particularly for London commuting, represent excellent value given the journey times and service frequency.

Local services and amenities are competitively priced, with independent retailers often providing better value than chain alternatives. The farmers market and local suppliers offer fresh produce at reasonable prices.

Making Your Move to Rochester

Planning your move to Rochester requires careful consideration of timing, particularly if you're starting a new job or need to coordinate with school terms. The best approach involves visiting the area several times, exploring different neighborhoods, and getting a feel for local amenities.

Consider seasonal factors when planning your move. Summer months provide better weather for moving day, while autumn and winter moves often come with reduced property prices and less competition from other buyers.

Professional removal services make the transition much smoother, particularly when moving from London or other distant locations. Local removal companies understand the area's roads, parking restrictions, and access issues, ensuring your moving day runs smoothly.

Register with essential services as soon as possible after moving. This includes GP practices, schools, and local government services. Many services can be arranged before your move, reducing stress during your first weeks in Rochester.

Connect with local community groups and organizations to build social networks quickly. Rochester residents are generally welcoming to newcomers, and many lasting friendships develop through shared interests in local history, sports, or community activities.

Whether you're moving to Rochester for work, family, or lifestyle reasons, you're making a choice that thousands of satisfied residents have made before you. The combination of history, convenience, and community creates a living environment that improves quality of life while providing practical advantages for modern living.
