Moving House in Chatham: A Complete Guide

Published 20 May 2025  |  9 min read  |  Medway & Kent Removals

Chatham is the largest and most populous of the Medway towns, a place of genuine historical significance — the Historic Dockyard that shaped British naval history for three centuries sits at its heart — and a town undergoing significant regeneration that is attracting new residents, businesses and investment. For families and professionals moving to or within Chatham, this guide covers everything you need to know.

Chatham's neighbourhoods

Chatham Town Centre

The ME4 postcode covers central Chatham including the Pentagon shopping centre, the railway station and the streets leading down to the waterfront. Properties here are a mix of Victorian and Edwardian terraces, post-war housing and modern apartments. The town centre has undergone significant investment in recent years with improved retail, food and entertainment options. It is the most affordable part of Chatham for buyers and well served by public transport.

Luton and Wayfield

South of the town centre, Luton and Wayfield are residential areas popular with families for their range of larger semi-detached and detached houses, good primary schools and quieter residential streets. Well placed for access to the M2 motorway at junction 3.

Walderslade and Lordswood

To the southwest of Chatham, the ME5 postcode covers Walderslade and Lordswood — hillside residential areas with a mix of post-war housing, modern estates and some larger detached properties. Popular with families, particularly those with children in Walderslade Primary School and the surrounding primaries. Good access to open countryside and the North Downs from the higher parts of Walderslade.

Hempstead

Hempstead sits between Gillingham and Walderslade in ME7, a quiet residential area popular with families seeking good schools and a more suburban feel. The Hempstead Valley shopping centre provides convenient local retail and leisure amenities. Hempstead is one of the more sought-after parts of the Medway towns for family buyers.

Property in Chatham

Chatham offers excellent value compared to many Kent towns and dramatically better value than equivalent London properties. The average property price in ME4 is approximately £230,000 to £270,000. A three-bedroom terraced house typically costs between £200,000 and £260,000. Larger detached properties in ME5 and Hempstead cost between £280,000 and £400,000 depending on size and condition.

For buyers relocating from London, Chatham's value proposition is compelling. A budget that would buy a studio flat in Zone 3 London will buy a two or three-bedroom house in Chatham with a garden.

Commuting from Chatham

Chatham station on the Southeastern network provides direct services to London Victoria, London Charing Cross and London Bridge with journey times of approximately 45 to 60 minutes on faster services. Local Medway connections are excellent. The M2 motorway at junction 3 provides fast road access to the M25, Dartford Crossing and onward to Essex and East London.

Schools in Chatham

Chatham has a range of state primary and secondary schools across the ME4 and ME5 postcodes. Chatham Grammar School for Girls is a selective school in the Medway grammar system. Thomas Aveling School and Robert Napier School are the main comprehensive options in the Chatham area. Hempstead Valley and Walderslade have good local primary schools that are consistently popular with families moving to the area.

The Historic Dockyard

Chatham Historic Dockyard is one of the most complete examples of a Georgian naval dockyard in the world and one of Kent's most significant visitor attractions. The dockyard closed as a working naval facility in 1984 and has since been transformed into a world-class heritage site with museums, events, period buildings and the preserved warships HMS Ocelot, HMS Gannet and the RNLI museum. Living in Chatham means having one of England's most extraordinary heritage sites effectively on your doorstep.

Moving day in Chatham — what to expect

Chatham's dense terraced streets — particularly in ME4 — can present parking challenges for removal vehicles. Narrow roads with cars parked on both sides are common in the older residential areas close to the town centre. For properties in controlled parking zones or on particularly narrow streets, a parking suspension from Medway Council may be required.

Chatham's terraced streets require smaller vehicles

Many of the older terraced streets in ME4 are too narrow for a large removal lorry to park safely.
