Area Guide

Best areas to live in Medway — Rochester vs Chatham vs Gillingham compared

📅 26 April 2026 🕐 6 min read 👥 Medway & Kent Removals Team

Choosing where to live in Medway can feel overwhelming with so many fantastic areas to consider. As a local removal company based right here in Rochester, we've helped countless families make the move between Medway's three main towns: Rochester, Chatham, and Gillingham. Each area has its own unique character, benefits, and lifestyle offerings that make them appealing to different types of residents. Whether you're a young professional, growing family, or looking to downsize in retirement, understanding what each area offers will help you make the best decision for your next move in Kent.

Understanding Medway's Geography and Transport Links

Medway sits strategically in Kent, offering excellent transport connections that make it one of the best areas to call home in the county. The three main towns - Rochester, Chatham, and Gillingham - are all well-connected by road and rail, with regular services to London making them popular choices for commuters.

The M2 and A2 provide direct routes to London and Canterbury, while the High Speed 1 rail service from nearby Ebbsfleet reaches London St Pancras in just 17 minutes. Medway's position along the River Medway has shaped its character over centuries, creating distinct neighborhoods with varied housing stock, from Victorian terraces to modern developments.

Transport Infrastructure Benefits

Each town benefits from Medway's excellent transport network, but with subtle differences. Rochester station offers direct services to London Victoria and Cannon Street, while Chatham and Gillingham stations provide similar connectivity. The towns are also well-served by local bus routes, making it easy to travel between areas without a car.

Rochester: Historic Charm Meets Modern Living

Rochester stands out as one of the most historically significant areas in Medway, with its stunning cathedral, Norman castle, and strong connections to Charles Dickens. This makes it particularly appealing to those who appreciate heritage and character in their surroundings.

Property Types and Housing Market

Rochester offers diverse housing options, from period properties near the historic center to modern family homes in quieter residential areas. The Georgian and Victorian properties around the High Street and near Rochester Cathedral command premium prices, while more affordable options can be found in areas like Borstal and Strood.

Property prices in Rochester tend to be slightly higher than neighboring Chatham and Gillingham, reflecting the area's historic appeal and riverside locations. However, this investment often pays off with strong capital growth and rental demand from both commuters and local professionals.

Lifestyle and Amenities

Rochester's High Street buzzes with independent shops, cafes, and restaurants, creating a vibrant community atmosphere. The annual Dickens Festival and Sweeps Festival draw visitors from across Kent and beyond, adding to the area's cultural richness.

Families particularly appreciate Rochester's excellent primary schools, including several outstanding-rated options. The proximity to countryside walks along the River Medway and the North Downs makes it ideal for those who enjoy outdoor activities while maintaining urban conveniences.

Who Rochester Suits Best

Rochester appeals most to professionals who commute to London, families seeking good schools and community spirit, and anyone who values historic character. The area attracts residents who don't mind paying slightly more for the privilege of living somewhere truly special in Kent.

Chatham: Urban Energy and Regeneration

Chatham represents Medway's commercial heart, with ongoing regeneration projects transforming it into one of Kent's most exciting places to live. The area successfully balances urban energy with residential tranquility.

The Dockyard and Waterfront Development

The Historic Dockyard Chatham serves as both a major tourist attraction and a symbol of the area's transformation. Waterfront developments have created modern apartments and townhouses with river views, attracting young professionals and downsizers alike.

Pentagon Shopping Centre anchors the town center, providing comprehensive retail options that serve the wider Medway area. This convenience factor makes Chatham particularly attractive to busy families and professionals who value having everything on their doorstep.

Property Market Dynamics

Chatham offers excellent value for money compared to Rochester, with property prices typically 10-15% lower while offering similar transport links and amenities. The ongoing regeneration means many properties offer good potential for capital growth as improvements continue across the area.

New build developments near the waterfront command premium prices, while traditional Victorian terraces and 1930s semi-detached houses in established neighborhoods offer more affordable entry points into the Medway property market.

Education and Family Life

Chatham boasts several highly-rated schools, including both primary and secondary options that consistently perform well in Ofsted inspections. The University of Kent's Medway campus adds a youthful energy to the area and supports local businesses.

Green spaces like the Great Lines Heritage Park provide family-friendly recreation areas, while the proximity to both countryside and coast means weekend activities are never far away in Kent.

Ideal Residents for Chatham

Chatham suits first-time buyers seeking value for money, young families wanting good schools and amenities, and anyone who enjoys being at the center of Medway's action. The area particularly appeals to those who want urban conveniences without London prices.

Gillingham: Community Spirit and Affordability

Gillingham often surprises newcomers with its strong community feel and excellent value for money. As one of Medway's most affordable areas, it attracts a diverse mix of residents while maintaining distinct neighborhood characters.

Residential Areas and Housing Options

Gillingham's housing stock ranges from Victorian terraces near the High Street to post-war estates and modern developments on the outskirts. Areas like Wigmore and Parkwood offer family-friendly environments with good local amenities.

Property prices in Gillingham typically offer the best value across Medway, making it particularly attractive to first-time buyers and young families. The area has seen steady capital growth as more people discover its benefits as a place to live in Kent.

Local Character and Community

Gillingham High Street maintains a traditional market town feel, with local independent businesses alongside national chains. The area has a strong sense of community, with local events and festivals bringing residents together throughout the year.

Gillingham Football Club adds local pride and community identity, while the nearby Great Lines and Riverside Country Park provide excellent outdoor spaces for families and fitness enthusiasts.

Transport and Connectivity

Despite being further from the historic attractions of Rochester and Chatham, Gillingham offers equally good transport links. The station provides direct services to London, while road connections make it easy to reach other parts of Kent and the M25.

Best Suited Residents

Gillingham appeals particularly to first-time buyers, young families prioritizing value for money, and anyone seeking genuine community spirit. The area suits those who prefer a quieter lifestyle while maintaining easy access to employment centers and London.

Comparing the Three Areas: Key Factors to Consider

Cost of Living and Property Values

When comparing Rochester vs Chatham vs Gillingham, property prices generally follow a clear hierarchy. Rochester commands the highest prices due to its historic appeal and prestige locations. Chatham sits in the middle, offering good value with regeneration benefits. Gillingham provides the most affordable entry point while still delivering excellent transport links and community amenities.

However, all three areas offer better value than many parts of Kent closer to London, making Medway an attractive option for those seeking the best of both worlds - countryside living with city connectivity.

Schools and Education

All three towns offer good educational opportunities, with outstanding primary schools and well-regarded secondary options. Rochester's schools often have longer waiting lists due to the area's popularity, while Chatham and Gillingham may offer more immediate availability.

The presence of the University of Kent's Medway campus benefits all three areas, creating educational opportunities and supporting local businesses throughout the region.

Shopping and Entertainment

Chatham leads for shopping convenience with Pentagon Shopping Centre and numerous High Street options. Rochester offers the best selection of independent shops, restaurants, and cultural venues. Gillingham provides good local amenities with a more community-focused retail environment.

Green Spaces and Recreation

All three areas benefit from Medway's excellent green spaces. Rochester offers riverside walks and easy access to countryside. Chatham provides the Great Lines Heritage Park and waterfront areas. Gillingham boasts Riverside Country Park and excellent sports facilities.

Making Your Decision: Which Area Suits You Best?

For Young Professionals and Commuters

If you're commuting to London and value historic character, Rochester offers the best lifestyle balance. The slightly higher property costs often pay off through better capital growth and rental yields if you need to relocate for work.

Chatham provides excellent value with good transport links and urban amenities, perfect for busy professionals who want convenience and competitive property prices.

For Growing Families

Families should consider their priorities carefully. Rochester offers prestige and excellent schools but at higher cost. Chatham provides good schools, shopping, and value for money. Gillingham delivers the best affordability while maintaining good educational options and strong community support.

For Retirees and Downsizers

Rochester's cultural offerings and historic charm appeal to many retirees, while waterfront developments in Chatham offer modern, low-maintenance living options. Gillingham's community spirit and affordability make it attractive for those prioritizing value and local connections.

Practical Moving Considerations

When planning your move within Medway, consider the practical aspects of relocating between these areas. Each town has its own character, but they're close enough that you can easily explore all three before making your final decision.

Local knowledge becomes invaluable when choosing specific streets or neighborhoods within each area. Some roads offer better transport links, while others provide quieter family environments or better investment potential.

Timing Your Move

The Medway property market shows seasonal variations, with spring and summer typically offering more choice but higher competition. Winter moves often provide better value and more negotiating power with sellers.

School catchment areas play a crucial role in family decisions, so timing moves to align with academic years can be important for families with school-age children.

Local Services and Healthcare

All three areas benefit from good local services and healthcare provision. Medway Hospital serves the entire region, while local GP practices and dental services are well-distributed across Rochester, Chatham, and Gillingham.

Local councils provide consistent services across Medway, though some areas benefit from additional regeneration investment that enhances local facilities and infrastructure.

Future Development and Investment

Medway continues to attract investment and development, with major projects planned across all three main towns. Understanding these future developments can help inform your decision about the best areas to live in Medway for long-term satisfaction and property value growth.

Rochester's heritage protection ensures its character remains unchanged while supporting sustainable development. Chatham's regeneration continues with waterfront and town center improvements. Gillingham benefits from infrastructure investment and community facility upgrades.
