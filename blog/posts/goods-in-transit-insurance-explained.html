What is goods in transit insurance and why does it matter for your move?

📅 4 May 2026 🕐 6 min read 👥 Medway & Kent Removals Team

When planning a house move in Rochester, Medway or anywhere across Kent, you'll likely encounter the term "goods in transit insurance" during your research into removal companies. This crucial type of coverage protects your belongings during the moving process, yet many homeowners don't fully understand what it entails or why it's so important. As a professional removal company serving the Medway area and beyond, we believe every customer should be fully informed about their insurance options before moving day arrives.

Understanding Goods in Transit Insurance

Goods in transit insurance is a specialised form of coverage designed to protect your personal belongings while they're being transported from one location to another. Unlike your standard home contents insurance, which typically covers items only when they're safely inside your property, goods in transit insurance provides protection during the vulnerable period when your possessions are being loaded, transported, and unloaded by your removal team.

This type of insurance becomes active the moment your belongings are lifted from their original location and remains in effect until they're safely placed in your new home. For residents moving within Kent, whether that's from Rochester to Maidstone, Chatham to Canterbury, or anywhere in between, this coverage bridges the gap when your standard home insurance may not apply.

What Does Goods in Transit Insurance Cover?

A comprehensive goods in transit policy typically covers your belongings against several types of damage and loss. Physical damage caused by accidents during transport is the most common claim, including items that might be crushed, broken, or damaged if the removal vehicle is involved in a collision. Water damage from unexpected weather conditions or vehicle issues is also covered, which is particularly relevant for moves across Kent where coastal weather can be unpredictable.

Fire damage, though rare, is another covered risk, as is theft of your belongings from the removal vehicle. Loss of items during the moving process is also typically included, providing peace of mind that if something goes missing during your move from Gravesend to Tunbridge Wells, for example, you'll be financially protected.

Why Standard Home Insurance Isn't Enough

Many homeowners in the Medway area assume their existing home contents insurance will cover their belongings during a move. Unfortunately, this is rarely the case. Most standard home insurance policies have specific exclusions for items that are in transit or being moved by third parties.

The moment your possessions leave your current home, they typically fall outside the scope of your home contents policy. Similarly, your new home's insurance may not take effect until your belongings are actually inside the property and the move is complete. This creates a coverage gap that can last anywhere from a few hours for local moves within Rochester to several days for longer distance relocations.

The Financial Reality of Replacement Costs

Consider the total value of everything you're moving. For most families in Kent, this easily amounts to tens of thousands of pounds when you factor in furniture, electronics, clothing, kitchenware, and personal items. Without proper goods in transit insurance, you could be personally liable for replacing any damaged or lost items, which could create significant financial hardship on top of the stress of moving house.

Even a minor accident during transport could result in damage to multiple items. A sudden stop or minor collision might damage your television, crack picture frames, and break ornaments simultaneously. The cumulative cost of replacing these items without insurance could easily reach thousands of pounds.

Types of Removal Insurance Available in Kent

When choosing a removal company in the Medway area, you'll typically encounter several different levels of insurance coverage. Understanding these options will help you make an informed decision about what level of protection is right for your move.

Basic Carrier Liability

Most removal companies are required by law to provide basic carrier liability coverage. However, this minimum level of protection is often insufficient for most moves. Basic liability typically covers damage at a rate per kilogram of weight rather than the actual replacement value of your items. This means an expensive but lightweight item like a laptop might only be covered for a few pounds despite costing over a thousand to replace.

For moves within Kent, basic carrier liability might be suitable only for those moving items of minimal value or those who have alternative insurance arrangements already in place.

Comprehensive Goods in Transit Insurance

A comprehensive policy provides full replacement value coverage for your belongings. This means if your antique dining table is damaged beyond repair during your move from Sittingbourne to Folkestone, you'll receive enough compensation to purchase a comparable replacement.

This type of coverage typically includes professional packing services in the policy terms. If your removal company professionally packs your items and they're subsequently damaged due to inadequate packing, the insurance will still provide coverage.

All Risks Coverage

The most comprehensive option available, all risks coverage protects against virtually any type of damage or loss that could occur during transit. This includes coverage for mysterious disappearance, damage from handling, and even damage that occurs due to the natural characteristics of your items, such as warping of wooden furniture due to temperature changes.

For high-value moves or those involving irreplaceable items, all risks coverage provides the ultimate peace of mind for residents moving anywhere across Kent.

Factors Affecting Your Insurance Premium

Several factors influence the cost of goods in transit insurance for your move in Kent. The total declared value of your belongings is the primary factor, as higher-value shipments naturally require higher premiums to provide adequate coverage.

The distance of your move also plays a role. A local move within Medway will typically cost less to insure than a long-distance relocation from Kent to Scotland. The type of items being moved affects pricing too, with high-value electronics, antiques, or artwork potentially requiring additional premiums or special handling provisions.

Your Property Type and Location

The type of properties you're moving between can impact your premium. Moving from a ground floor flat in Chatham to another ground floor property presents different risks than relocating from a three-story Victorian house in Rochester to a similar property in Maidstone. Access issues, stairs, and the need for special equipment all factor into the risk assessment.

Even the specific areas within Kent can affect pricing, with some postcodes potentially presenting higher risks due to local conditions or crime statistics.

How to Ensure You're Properly Covered

Securing appropriate goods in transit insurance starts with choosing a reputable removal company that offers comprehensive coverage options. When researching removal companies in the Medway area, always ask about their insurance offerings and ensure they can provide proof of their coverage.

Before your move, create a detailed inventory of everything being transported. For valuable items, take photographs and keep receipts where possible. This documentation will be crucial if you need to make a claim and will help ensure you declare an accurate total value for insurance purposes.

Declaring the Correct Value

One of the most critical aspects of goods in transit insurance is accurately declaring the total value of your belongings. Undervaluing your possessions to reduce premium costs is a false economy that could leave you significantly under-compensated if something goes wrong.

Consider the replacement cost of items rather than their current second-hand value. Your five-year-old sofa might not be worth much if you were selling it, but replacing it with something similar would cost considerably more.

What to Do If Damage Occurs

Despite the best efforts of professional removal teams, sometimes damage does occur during a move. Knowing how to respond properly can make the difference between a smooth insurance claim and a frustrating experience.

If you discover damage when your belongings arrive at your new Kent home, document everything immediately. Take clear photographs of any damaged items before moving them, and make detailed notes about what you've found. Notify your removal company as soon as possible, ideally while their team is still present.

The Claims Process

Most reputable removal companies will have a straightforward claims process for goods in transit insurance. You'll typically need to provide your original inventory, photographs of damaged items, and any receipts or proof of value you have available.

The insurance provider may arrange for damaged items to be assessed by a professional, particularly for high-value claims. For residents in Kent, this process is usually handled quickly by local assessors who understand the regional market for replacement items.

Choosing the Right Insured Removal Company in Medway

When selecting a removal company for your move in Kent, their approach to insurance should be a key consideration. A professional company will be transparent about their insurance options, provide clear explanations of what's covered, and help you choose the right level of protection for your specific needs.

Look for removal companies that offer multiple insurance tiers and are happy to explain the differences between them. They should also be able to provide proof of their own insurance coverage and professional accreditations.

Questions to Ask Your Removal Company

Before booking your move, ask your potential removal company about their insurance options. What levels of coverage do they offer? What's included in their basic service, and what costs extra? Can they provide examples of their claims process and typical settlement times?

Also inquire about any exclusions or limitations in their policies. Some companies may exclude certain types of items or have restrictions on coverage for items packed by the customer rather than their professional team.

The Peace of Mind Factor

Beyond the financial protection, goods in transit insurance provides invaluable peace of mind during what is often a stressful time. Moving house in Kent, whether you're relocating within Rochester or moving across the county, involves countless details and potential concerns. Knowing that your belongings are properly protected allows you to focus on other aspects of your move.

This peace of mind is particularly valuable when moving high-sentimental-value items that couldn't be replaced at any price. While insurance can't restore lost family heirlooms, knowing you've taken every reasonable precaution to protect them helps ensure you won't have regrets about your moving choices.

Making Your Decision

Goods in transit insurance is an essential consideration for anyone planning a move in Kent. While it represents an additional cost on top of your removal fees, the protection it provides far outweighs the premium for most households. The key is choosing the right level of coverage for your specific situation and ensuring you're working with a removal company that takes insurance seriously.

Remember that the cheapest removal quote isn't always the best value if it doesn't include adequate insurance protection. When comparing removal companies in the Medway area, factor in their insurance offerings as part of your overall value assessment.
