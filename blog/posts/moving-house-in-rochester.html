Rochester Guide

Moving House in Rochester: The Complete Guide

Published 6 May 2025  |  10 min read  |  Medway & Kent Removals

Rochester is one of Kent's most historically significant and characterful cities, and the home base of Medway & Kent Removals. Sitting at the heart of the Medway towns, Rochester combines a rich Norman heritage — the cathedral, the castle, the cobbled High Street — with a diverse and growing residential community that makes it one of the most sought-after addresses in the county.

Whether you are moving into Rochester for the first time, relocating within the city, or moving out to another part of Kent or beyond, this guide covers everything you need to know about moving house in Rochester — the neighbourhoods, the property market, the schools, the practical challenges on moving day and how to choose the right removal company for your Rochester move.

Rochester neighbourhoods — where to live

Rochester City Centre and the Historic Quarter

The historic heart of Rochester — the Cathedral, the Castle, the High Street — sits in the ME1 postcode. Properties here range from Georgian townhouses and Victorian terraces to period conversions above the independent shops and restaurants that line the High Street. This is Rochester at its most characterful and most sought-after. Properties in the historic core command a premium and are in consistent demand from professionals, downsizers and those who value location and character above space.

Moving into the historic quarter presents specific practical challenges. The streets are narrow and some are pedestrianised or restricted during the day. Access for removal vehicles requires careful planning — smaller vehicles are often necessary and arrival times need to be coordinated with any local parking restrictions. Medway & Kent Removals carries out numerous moves in the historic Rochester area each month and knows exactly how to plan access for every street and property type.

Borstal and Wouldham

To the south of Rochester, the villages of Borstal and Wouldham sit along the Medway Valley with outstanding countryside access and a quieter pace of life. Popular with families and those seeking a semi-rural setting within easy reach of Rochester's amenities. Property here is predominantly detached and semi-detached housing with good gardens — a significant draw for families relocating from more urban parts of Medway or from London.

Strood — just over the bridge

Technically a separate town in the ME2 postcode, Strood is effectively the western continuation of Rochester across the River Medway. Fast access to the A2 and M2 makes Strood popular with commuters. Property prices are slightly lower than equivalent Rochester properties, making it a popular first step for buyers who want Medway living at a more accessible price point.

Cuxton and Halling

Two villages south of Strood in the Medway Valley, Cuxton and Halling offer a genuinely rural feel while remaining within easy commuting distance of Rochester and the rest of Medway. Properties here are predominantly larger family homes with good gardens. Rail connections from Cuxton to Rochester and London are reasonable.

Property prices in Rochester

Rochester offers strong value compared to equivalent London and South East locations. The average property price in the ME1 postcode is approximately £280,000 to £320,000, with significant variation by property type and specific location. A three-bedroom terraced house in a typical Rochester street costs between £230,000 and £290,000. A four-bedroom detached house on the outskirts of the city costs between £320,000 and £450,000. Period properties in the historic core and larger family homes in the village locations command significant premiums.

For London buyers relocating to Rochester, the value proposition is compelling. A budget that buys a one or two-bedroom flat in Zone 3 or Zone 4 London buys a three or four-bedroom family house in Rochester with a garden and driveway. The improvement in space, quality and lifestyle is dramatic.

Commuting from Rochester

Rochester has good rail connections to London via Southeastern services. Direct trains from Rochester station to London Victoria and London Charing Cross take approximately 45 to 55 minutes on faster services. The Medway City Estate and Rochester's business park provide significant local employment. The proximity to the M2 motorway makes Rochester practical for those commuting by car to other parts of Kent, Essex via the M25 and beyond.

The Elizabeth line at Stratford is accessible from Rochester via the Southeastern high speed service to Ebbsfleet International and then Crossrail services into central London and beyond — a route increasingly used by Rochester residents working at Canary Wharf and in the City.

Schools in Rochester

Rochester has a strong and varied school offering. The Rochester Grammar School — a selective secondary school in the SE16 postcode — is one of the most sought-after state schools in Medway and consistently achieves strong results. Thomas Aveling School is a well-regarded comprehensive in the Rochester area. The independent sector is represented by several prep schools and Rochester Independent College which provides an alternative sixth form pathway.

Primary school provision in Rochester includes a good range of community schools and Church of England primaries, several of which are rated Good or Outstanding by Ofsted. Families relocating to Rochester with school-age children should begin school application research early — popular schools have waiting lists and in-year transfer applications can take several months to process.

Rochester's cultural and lifestyle offer

Rochester's cultural offer is exceptional for a city of its size. The annual Dickens Festival — Rochester was home to Charles Dickens for much of his life and features heavily in his novels — draws visitors from across the country. The Rochester Cathedral is one of England's oldest, with a heritage stretching back to 604 AD. The castle, the Corn Exchange, the independent galleries and the growing restaurant scene on the High Street make Rochester a genuinely attractive place to live as well as visit.

The River Medway provides a beautiful waterfront setting for walking, cycling and leisure. The Medway Valley Walk runs south from Rochester through Borstal and Wouldham into the Kent countryside. The North Downs Way is accessible within a short drive and the Kent coast at Whitstable is less than 30 minutes away.

Moving house in Rochester — what to expect on moving day

Parking and access

Parking in Rochester varies significantly by area. The historic city centre has extensive controlled parking zones and some streets are pedestrianised or have time-restricted access. For moves in the historic quarter, a parking suspension from Medway Council may be required to ensure the removal vehicle can park legally at the kerb outside the property.

Medway Council parking suspension applications can be made online and require a minimum of five working days notice. Fees are approximately £50 to £80 per bay per day. Medway & Kent Removals advises on parking requirements for every Rochester move and manages suspension applications where needed as part of our service.

Property access challenges in Rochester

Rochester's older housing stock — particularly in the ME1 historic core and along the older Victorian terraces — can present access challenges. Narrow hallways, steep stairs, limited turning space in front gardens and restricted street access are all common in older Rochester properties. Our team carries out a pre-move assessment of both properties before every move to ensure we select the right vehicle size, the right crew size and plan the most efficient approach for the specific properties involved.

Moving into a new-build in Rochester

Rochester has seen significant new-build development in recent years, particularly in the waterfront areas near the Medway and on the eastern edges of the city. New-build properties are generally easier to access with good parking, wider hallways and no access restrictions. However, new-build developments sometimes have narrow service roads and restrictions on large vehicles during working hours — always confirm access with the developer before moving day.

Choosing a removal company for your Rochester move

Choosing the right removal company for a Rochester house move matters more than many people realise. The specific access challenges of Rochester properties — particularly in the historic core — require a team with genuine local knowledge. A national removal company unfamiliar with Rochester's streets and parking arrangements can cause delays and frustration on moving day that a local company would avoid entirely.

When choosing a removal company for your Rochester move, check the following:

  • Are they locally based? A Rochester-based removal company knows the streets, the parking restrictions and the property access challenges intimately.
  • Are they fully insured? Goods-in-transit insurance and public liability insurance are essential — ask to see the certificate before booking.
  • Do they offer a fixed price? Hourly rates can spiral on moving day. A fixed price means you know exactly what you are paying.
  • Do they carry out a pre-move survey? Any professional removal company should assess both properties before providing a quote — not guess over the phone.
  • What do their reviews say? Look for consistent Google reviews from Rochester and Medway customers specifically.
