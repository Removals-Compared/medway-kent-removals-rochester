Moving to Chatham: A Neighbourhood Guide for New Residents

Published 10 Jun 2025  |  7 min read  |  Medway & Kent Removals

Chatham is the largest and most historically significant of the Medway towns — the birthplace of Charles Dickens, home to the extraordinary Historic Dockyard and a town undergoing significant regeneration that is attracting new residents and investment. For those considering a move to Chatham, this neighbourhood guide covers everything you need to know about where to live and what to expect.

Chatham's neighbourhoods

Chatham Town Centre — ME4

Central Chatham including the Pentagon shopping centre and the railway station. Properties are a mix of Victorian and Edwardian terraces, post-war housing and modern apartments. The most affordable part of Chatham for buyers. Well served by public transport and close to the Historic Dockyard.

Luton and Wayfield

South of the town centre, popular with families for larger semi-detached houses, good primary schools and quieter residential streets. Easy access to the M2 at junction 3.

Walderslade and Lordswood — ME5

Hillside residential areas southwest of Chatham with a mix of post-war housing and modern estates. Popular with families. Good primary schools and outstanding countryside access from the higher parts of Walderslade.

Hempstead — ME7

Between Gillingham and Walderslade, a quiet residential area with Hempstead Valley shopping centre nearby. One of the more sought-after parts of the Medway towns for family buyers.

Property in Chatham

Chatham offers excellent value — a three-bedroom terraced house costs between £200,000 and £260,000. Larger detached properties in Walderslade and Hempstead cost between £280,000 and £400,000. Outstanding value for the London commute time of 45 to 60 minutes.

Chatham Historic Dockyard

One of the most complete examples of a Georgian naval dockyard in the world. Closed as a working facility in 1984 and transformed into a world-class heritage site. Living in Chatham means having one of England's most extraordinary heritage attractions on your doorstep.

Chatham's terraced streets need smaller vehicles

Many of the older terraced streets in ME4 are too narrow for large removal lorries. Medway and Kent Removals always selects the right vehicle size for each specific Chatham street.
