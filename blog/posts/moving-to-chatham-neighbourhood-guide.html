Moving to Chatham — neighbourhood guide and what to expect

📅 11 May 2026 🕐 6 min read 👥 Medway & Kent Removals Team

Moving to a new area can feel overwhelming, but choosing Chatham as your new home is an excellent decision that offers the perfect blend of historical charm, modern amenities, and excellent transport links. As a thriving town in the heart of Medway, Kent, Chatham has transformed dramatically in recent years while maintaining its rich maritime heritage. Whether you're relocating for work, family, or simply seeking a change of pace, this comprehensive guide will help you understand what makes living in Chatham Medway such an attractive proposition and what you can expect from your new neighbourhood.

Understanding Chatham's Location and Geography

Chatham sits perfectly positioned in the Medway Towns, forming part of the larger Medway unitary authority alongside Rochester, Gillingham, Strood, and Rainham. This strategic location in North Kent places you within easy reach of London, Canterbury, and the beautiful Kent countryside, making it an ideal base for both commuters and those who prefer to stay local.

The town is built along the River Medway, which has shaped its character and development for centuries. Moving to Chatham means you'll be living in an area where the river valley creates a unique landscape of hills and waterfront areas, offering varied scenery and different neighbourhood characters depending on which part of town you choose.

From Chatham, you'll find Rochester just a short walk or drive to the west, with its stunning cathedral and castle, while Gillingham extends to the east. This proximity means you'll have access to the amenities and attractions of all the Medway Towns, significantly expanding your options for dining, entertainment, and recreational activities.

Transport Links and Commuting Options

Railway Connections

One of the biggest advantages of moving to Chatham is the excellent rail connectivity. Chatham Railway Station provides regular services to London Victoria, London Cannon Street, and London Bridge, with journey times typically between 45 minutes to an hour. This makes it particularly attractive for London commuters who want to escape the capital's high property prices without sacrificing career opportunities.

The station also connects you to other Kent destinations, including Canterbury, Dover, and Ramsgate, perfect for exploring your new county. For those working in Rochester or other Medway Towns, the local train services make commuting straightforward and affordable.

Road Networks

Living in Chatham Medway means excellent road access via the M2 and A2, connecting you directly to London and the Channel ports. The M25 is easily accessible, opening up routes to other parts of the Southeast. For local travel, the A229 runs through Chatham, connecting you to Maidstone and other Kent towns.

The area has seen significant investment in road infrastructure, and while traffic can be busy during peak times, the multiple route options mean you're rarely stuck without alternatives.

Local Transport

Bus services throughout Medway are comprehensive, with regular routes connecting Chatham to Rochester, Gillingham, and surrounding areas. The town center is compact and walkable, and cycling is becoming increasingly popular with new cycle lanes being developed as part of ongoing urban improvements.

Chatham Neighbourhoods and Areas

Chatham Town Center

The heart of Chatham has undergone significant regeneration in recent years. The Pentagon Shopping Centre anchors the retail district, while the high street offers a mix of national chains and independent businesses. Living in the town center area means walking distance to shops, restaurants, and transport links, though it can be busier and more built-up than outlying areas.

Chatham Maritime

This waterfront development represents modern Chatham at its best. Built around the historic dockyard, Chatham Maritime combines new housing developments with preserved maritime heritage. Properties here tend to be newer and often come with river views, though prices reflect the premium location. The area includes the University of Kent's Medway campus and excellent recreational facilities.

Walderslade

Popular with families, Walderslade offers a more suburban feel while remaining part of Chatham. The area features good schools, parks, and a strong sense of community. Properties range from 1960s developments to newer builds, often with gardens and parking, making it attractive for those moving from other parts of Kent or beyond.

Luton and Wayfield

These residential areas offer good value housing options with easy access to Chatham's amenities. The neighborhoods are well-established with local shops, schools, and green spaces. They're particularly popular with first-time buyers and young families moving to Chatham for affordability without compromising on convenience.

Housing Market and Property Types

The Chatham housing market offers something for every budget and lifestyle. Victorian and Edwardian terraced houses are common in the older parts of town, many of which have been beautifully restored and offer period features alongside modern conveniences. These properties often provide excellent value compared to similar homes in Rochester or other historic Kent towns.

For those seeking modern accommodation, new developments in Chatham Maritime and other regeneration areas offer contemporary apartments and houses, often with parking and shared amenities. The area has also seen significant investment in affordable housing, making it accessible for key workers and first-time buyers.

Family homes with gardens are readily available in the suburban areas, while the town center offers apartments and converted properties ideal for professionals and couples. Rental options are diverse, from student accommodation near the university to family homes in residential neighborhoods.

Schools and Education

Families moving to Chatham will find a good selection of primary and secondary schools. The area benefits from Kent's selective education system, with grammar schools accessible to high-achieving students. Chatham Grammar School for Girls is highly regarded, while nearby Rochester has additional grammar school options.

Primary schools throughout Chatham generally perform well, with many receiving positive Ofsted ratings. The diversity of schools means parents can usually find options that match their preferences and their children's needs.

Higher education is well-represented with the University of Kent's Medway campus located in Chatham Maritime. This brings a vibrant student population to the area and ensures excellent library and cultural facilities that benefit the whole community.

Shopping and Amenities

Living in Chatham Medway means access to comprehensive shopping facilities. The Pentagon Shopping Centre houses major retailers including Primark, Next, and H&M, while the high street offers banks, pharmacies, and everyday services. For larger shopping trips, Bluewater is easily accessible by car, and Rochester's historic high street provides a more boutique shopping experience.

The town has numerous supermarkets including large Tesco and ASDA stores, plus smaller convenience shops throughout residential areas. The Saturday market in the town center adds character and offers fresh produce and local goods.

Healthcare facilities are excellent, with Medway Maritime Hospital providing comprehensive medical services. The town has numerous GP practices, dental surgeries, and specialist healthcare providers, ensuring you'll have access to quality medical care after moving to Chatham.

Entertainment and Cultural Attractions

Chatham's entertainment scene has flourished with recent regeneration efforts. The Central Theatre hosts touring productions and local performances, while numerous pubs and restaurants cater to all tastes and budgets. The town's proximity to Rochester means easy access to Rochester Castle, Rochester Cathedral, and the annual Dickens Festival.

The Historic Dockyard Chatham is a world-class attraction right on your doorstep, offering insights into Britain's naval heritage with historic ships, exhibitions, and events throughout the year. This UNESCO World Heritage site makes living in Chatham particularly special for history enthusiasts.

For families, the area offers numerous parks and recreational facilities. The Great Lines Heritage Park provides green space and walking trails, while Chatham Maritime offers waterfront walks and modern leisure facilities.

Dining and Nightlife

The dining scene in Chatham has expanded significantly, reflecting the town's growing diversity and prosperity. From traditional pubs serving classic British fare to authentic Indian, Chinese, and Turkish restaurants, there's something to suit every palate. The town center has several popular chain restaurants, while hidden gems in residential areas offer more intimate dining experiences.

The nightlife caters primarily to a relaxed, community-focused crowd, with traditional pubs, wine bars, and a few nightclubs. Many residents also take advantage of the easy transport links to enjoy London's nightlife when they want something more extensive.

Parks and Recreation

Green spaces are abundant for those moving to Chatham. The Great Lines Heritage Park is perfect for walking, jogging, and family activities, while smaller neighborhood parks provide playgrounds and open space throughout residential areas. The riverside locations offer pleasant walks along the Medway, particularly around Chatham Maritime.

Sports facilities include several gyms, swimming pools, and recreational centers. The proximity to the Kent countryside means excellent walking, cycling, and outdoor activity opportunities are never far away. Golf courses, including the Rochester and Cobham Park Golf Club, provide excellent recreational options for enthusiasts.

Community and Local Culture

Living in Chatham Medway means joining a community that takes pride in its heritage while embracing positive change. The town has a strong sense of identity shaped by its maritime history and its role in the broader Medway area. Community groups, local societies, and volunteer organizations provide opportunities to get involved and meet neighbors.

The diversity of the population creates a welcoming atmosphere for newcomers. Whether you're moving from elsewhere in Kent, from London, or from further afield, you'll find Chatham residents generally friendly and helpful. Local Facebook groups and community forums make it easy to connect with neighbors and stay informed about local events and services.

Cost of Living

One of the major advantages of moving to Chatham is the reasonable cost of living compared to many parts of Southeast England. Property prices and rental costs remain significantly lower than London and many other Kent towns, while still offering excellent transport links to higher-wage employment areas.

Daily expenses like groceries, utilities, and services are generally reasonable, and the competition between local businesses helps keep prices competitive. Council tax rates in Medway are typically lower than many comparable areas, providing additional savings for residents.

Future Development and Investment

Chatham continues to benefit from significant investment and development. The regeneration of the town center is ongoing, with improvements to public spaces, new housing developments, and enhanced transport infrastructure planned. The Medway Council's commitment to improving the area means property values are likely to continue rising, making it an excellent time to consider moving to Chatham.

The development of Chatham Maritime continues, with new residential and commercial projects enhancing the area's appeal. Investment in education, healthcare, and recreational facilities shows the long-term commitment to making Chatham an increasingly attractive place to live.

Making Your Move to Chatham

Moving to any new area requires careful planning, and relocating to Chatham is no exception. Start by researching specific neighborhoods that match your lifestyle preferences and budget. Visit at different times of day and week to get a feel for the area's rhythm and character.

Consider your daily routines and priorities. If you'll be commuting to London, proximity to Chatham station might be crucial. If you have school-age children, research catchment areas for preferred schools. For those working locally in Rochester or other Medway Towns, different areas might offer better convenience.

Connect with local estate agents who understand the Chatham market and can guide you to properties that meet your needs. Join local social media groups and online forums to get insider perspectives from current residents about the best areas and local services.

Practical Moving Considerations

Once you've decided on moving to Chatham, practical preparation becomes essential. Research local services you'll need, including healthcare providers, schools, and utility companies. Many services in Medway cover all the local towns, so you'll have good choice and availability.

Plan your moving day carefully, considering Chatham's geography and traffic patterns. The town's hill terrain and busy roads during peak times can affect moving logistics, so professional local knowledge can be invaluable. Ensure you understand any parking restrictions or narrow streets that might affect large removal vehicles.

Register with local services as soon as possible after your move. This includes GP practices, which can get busy, and schools if you have children. The local council offices in Medway can help with council tax registration and other administrative requirements.

Living in Chatham Medway offers an excellent quality of life with the perfect balance of urban amenities and community feel. The town's rich history, ongoing regeneration, and strategic location make it an increasingly popular choice for families, professionals, and anyone seeking a vibrant yet affordable place to call home in Kent.
