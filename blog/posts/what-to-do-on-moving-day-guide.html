What to Do on Moving Day: A Complete Guide for Kent and Medway Movers

Published 22 Jul 2025  |  7 min read  |  Medway & Kent Removals

Moving day is the culmination of weeks of planning and preparation. Even with everything organised in advance, there are specific things to do on the day itself that make the difference between a smooth move and a stressful one. This guide covers everything from the moment the removal crew arrives to the moment you hand back the keys to your old property.

Before the crew arrives

  • Get up early — give yourself at least an hour before the crew is due to arrive
  • Take meter readings at the old property and photograph them with your phone
  • Make sure all boxes are sealed and labelled
  • Clear access routes — hallways, stairs and doorways must be completely clear
  • Have the kettle ready and drinks available for the crew
  • Confirm key collection arrangements with your estate agent or solicitor
  • Ensure pets and children are with a designated carer for the day

While loading is in progress

  • Walk through every room with the crew foreman before loading begins to identify any items not going on the van
  • Point out any fragile items requiring special care that have not already been flagged
  • Stay available to answer questions but do not get in the way of the loading
  • Check the loft, garage, shed and any outbuildings — these are the most commonly overlooked areas
  • Keep your essentials box and any personal valuables separate and accessible

When loading is complete

  • Do a final walk-through of every room, cupboard, loft and garden shed before the van leaves
  • Check under beds, in wardrobes and behind doors — items left behind cannot be retrieved easily
  • Take final photographs of the empty property for your records
  • Ensure all windows are locked, all taps are off and the boiler is set appropriately
  • Return all keys, fobs, parking permits and gate remotes to the estate agent

At the new property

  • Take meter readings immediately before the crew begins unloading
  • Check all utilities are working — water, gas, electricity, boiler
  • Direct the crew to place furniture in the correct rooms according to your floor plan
  • Check all items are accounted for before the crew departs
  • Set up children's bedrooms first — they need a familiar space as soon as possible
  • Find the stopcock in case of any water emergencies

At the end of the day

  • Confirm with your energy supplier the opening meter readings at the new address
  • Check the heating system is working
  • Locate your essentials box — kettle, mugs, toilet paper, phone chargers
  • Do not try to unpack everything on day one — it is not necessary and it is exhausting
Photograph everything on moving day

Take photographs of meter readings, the condition of the empty old property, and any existing marks or damage in the new property before unloading. These photographs protect you from any disputes with landlords, sellers or energy suppliers.
