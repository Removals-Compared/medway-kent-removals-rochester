What to do on moving day — an hour-by-hour guide for Medway families

📅 17 May 2026 🕐 6 min read 👥 Medway & Kent Removals Team

Moving day has arrived, and you're probably feeling a mix of excitement and anxiety. After weeks of planning, packing, and preparation, today is when everything comes together. Whether you're relocating within Rochester, moving from Chatham to Gillingham, or heading anywhere across Medway and Kent, having a clear hour-by-hour plan can transform a potentially stressful day into a smooth, organised experience. As professional removal specialists serving the Medway area for years, we've helped thousands of families navigate this important day, and we're sharing our insider knowledge to help make your move as seamless as possible.

Early Morning Preparation (6:00 AM - 8:00 AM)

Start Your Day Right

Wake up early, even if you're naturally more of a night owl. Moving day requires energy and clear thinking, so getting a proper start is essential. Have a good breakfast, but keep it simple. You don't want to be washing up elaborate cooking equipment when your removal team arrives.

Check the weather forecast for your area of Kent. If rain is expected, gather plastic sheeting, towels, and extra coverings to protect your belongings and floors. Medway weather can be unpredictable, and being prepared saves time and stress later.

Final Safety Check

Walk through your Rochester home one more time, checking that all electrical appliances are unplugged and that you've turned off the gas supply if you're disconnecting any appliances. Ensure pathways are clear both inside and outside your property. If you're on a busy Medway street, consider placing cones or signs to reserve parking space for the removal van.

Prepare Your Moving Day Kit

Gather essential items you'll need throughout the day: important documents, medications, phone chargers, snacks, drinks, cleaning supplies, toilet paper, and a first aid kit. Keep these in a clearly marked box or bag that travels with you, not in the removal van.

Removal Team Arrival and Initial Setup (8:00 AM - 9:00 AM)

Meet Your Moving Team

Professional removal companies typically arrive between 8:00 and 9:00 AM. When your team arrives, take a few minutes to introduce yourself and walk them through your home. Point out any items requiring special care, narrow doorways, or potential challenges they should know about. Many Rochester properties have period features or tight staircases that need extra attention.

Review the Moving Plan

Discuss the order of loading with your removal team leader. Generally, large furniture items go in first, followed by boxes, with fragile items carefully positioned and secured. If you're moving locally within Medway, you might have multiple trips planned. If you're heading further across Kent, everything needs to fit in one load.

Protect Your Properties

Professional movers will lay protective coverings on floors and doorframes, but double-check that both your current property and your new home are adequately protected. This is particularly important in older Medway properties where original features need extra care.

Loading Phase (9:00 AM - 12:00 PM)

Stay Available but Out of the Way

Your removal team needs space to work efficiently. Position yourself where you can answer questions quickly but aren't blocking doorways or work areas. Use this time to handle last-minute tasks like meter readings or final cleaning of areas that have been cleared.

Document Everything

Take photos of valuable items before they're loaded, particularly electronics, artwork, or antiques. If you're moving from a period property in Rochester's historic areas, you might have items of particular value that deserve extra documentation.

Handle Utilities and Services

While the team loads, contact your utility providers to confirm disconnection times. Many families in Medway forget this step, but it's important for ensuring smooth service transfer to your new address. If you're staying local in Chatham or Gillingham, some providers can handle the switch more quickly.

Final Room Checks

As each room is cleared, do a thorough check for forgotten items. Look in cupboards, behind doors, in lofts, and garden sheds. Check garages carefully, as these spaces often contain overlooked items. Many Medway homes have cellars or unusual storage areas that are easily forgotten in the excitement of moving day.

Midday Tasks and Coordination (12:00 PM - 2:00 PM)

Lunch Break Strategy

Professional removal teams usually take a lunch break around midday. Use this time to grab a quick meal yourself, but avoid anything too heavy. If you're moving locally within Medway, this might be a good time to drive to your new property and ensure everything is ready for the team's arrival.

Key Handover Preparation

If you haven't already, confirm the exact timing for key collection at your new property. Estate agents and solicitors in Kent typically have specific procedures, and delays here can hold up your entire move. Keep important phone numbers easily accessible.

Travel Planning

Plan your route to your new home, considering current traffic conditions. If you're moving during school term time, be aware of school run traffic in areas like Strood or Rainham. Check for any roadworks or events that might affect travel times across Medway and Kent.

Final Loading and Property Exit (2:00 PM - 3:00 PM)

Last Items and Cleaning

The final items to load are usually cleaning supplies and any last-minute essentials. Do a final clean of your old property, focusing on areas that were behind furniture. Many letting agreements and sale contracts require properties to be left in good condition.

Meter Readings and Documentation

Take final meter readings for gas, electricity, and water. Photograph these for your records. This is particularly important in older Medway properties where meter locations might be unusual or difficult to access.

Final Security Check

Ensure all windows are closed and locked, alarm systems are set correctly, and you have all keys ready for handover. Don't forget garage remotes, shed keys, or any other access devices that should stay with the property.

Transit and New Property Arrival (3:00 PM - 4:00 PM)

Travel to Your New Home

If you're travelling separately from the removal van, plan to arrive at your new property before or around the same time as your moving team. This ensures you're there to direct them and handle any immediate issues.

New Property Preparation

Upon arrival at your new Medway or Kent home, do a quick walkthrough to identify any issues that need immediate attention. Check that utilities are connected and working, and ensure pathways are clear for the removal team.

Neighbourhood Considerations

If your new home is on a busy street in Rochester, Chatham, or another Medway town, consider informing immediate neighbours about the move. This courtesy helps prevent parking issues and maintains good relationships from day one.

Unloading and Initial Setup (4:00 PM - 6:00 PM)

Direct the Unloading Process

Be present to direct items to their correct rooms. This saves enormous time later and ensures fragile items are placed safely immediately. If your new Kent property has multiple floors, prioritise getting heavy items positioned correctly on the first attempt.

Priority Items First

Identify which items you need access to immediately. Beds, basic cooking equipment, toiletries, and a change of clothes should be easy to locate. Mark these boxes clearly and ensure they're unloaded in an accessible location.

Damage Inspection

Check items as they're unloaded, particularly furniture and fragile items. Professional removal companies have insurance, but identifying any damage immediately makes the claims process much smoother.

Team Departure and Initial Settling (6:00 PM - 8:00 PM)

Final Paperwork

Complete all paperwork with your removal team, including delivery confirmations and any damage reports. Keep copies of all documents for your records. If you're happy with the service, ask about leaving reviews online to help other Medway families.

Essential Services Check

Once the removal team has left, check that all essential services are working properly. Test taps, heating systems, electrical outlets, and any installed appliances. If you discover issues, contact the relevant parties immediately while it's still business hours.

Basic Setup Priorities

Focus on essentials first. Set up at least one bed, ensure you can prepare basic meals, and locate toiletries and medications. Everything else can wait until tomorrow. Don't try to unpack everything on moving day; you'll be tired and more likely to make mistakes.

Evening Wind-Down (8:00 PM onwards)

Celebrate Your Achievement

Moving home is one of life's most stressful experiences, and you've successfully completed the biggest day. Order takeaway from a local Rochester or Medway restaurant rather than attempting to cook. You deserve a break, and supporting local businesses helps you start connecting with your new community.

Safety and Security

Before settling in for the night, check that all external doors and windows are secure. Locate your fuse box, water stopcock, and gas meter in case of emergencies. Many Kent properties have these in unusual locations, so finding them now saves panic later.

Plan Tomorrow

Make a realistic list for tomorrow, but keep it short. Unpacking is a marathon, not a sprint. Focus on one room at a time, starting with the kitchen and bedrooms. Register with local services like doctors and dentists in your new Medway area when you're feeling more settled.

Pro Tips from Medway Moving Experts

Weather Contingencies

Kent weather can change quickly. Keep towels and plastic sheeting handy even if the morning starts dry. Professional removal teams come prepared, but extra protection for your belongings and floors is always wise.

Communication is Key

Stay in regular contact with your removal team leader throughout the day. If you notice potential problems or have concerns, address them immediately. Experienced movers appreciate customers who communicate clearly and promptly.

Local Knowledge Advantage

If you're new to Medway, ask your removal team about local tips. Professional movers who regularly work in Rochester, Chatham, and surrounding areas often know useful information about parking restrictions, local services, and area-specific challenges.

When Things Don't Go to Plan

Stay Calm and Flexible

Even with perfect planning, moving day can present unexpected challenges. Completion delays, weather issues, or traffic problems can affect timing. Professional removal companies are experienced in handling these situations, so trust their expertise and remain flexible.

Have Backup Plans

Keep contact details for local Medway hotels or friends who could provide temporary accommodation if needed. While rare, completion delays can occasionally mean you can't access your new property when expected.

Emergency Contacts

Keep a list of emergency contacts easily accessible, including your removal company, solicitor, estate agent, and utility providers. Having these numbers readily available prevents panic if issues arise.

Moving day doesn't have to be the stressful experience many people fear. With proper planning, clear communication, and realistic expectations, your move within Medway, Rochester, or anywhere across Kent can be surprisingly smooth. Remember that professional removal companies handle moves every day and have systems in place to manage challenges efficiently.

The key to a successful moving day is preparation, flexibility, and trusting the expertise of your chosen removal team. Take care of yourself throughout the day, stay hydrated, and remember that any minor hiccups are normal parts of the moving process.
