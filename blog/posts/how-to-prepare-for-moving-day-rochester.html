Moving Tips

How to prepare for moving day — tips from our Rochester removal team

📅 27 April 2026 🕐 6 min read 👥 Medway & Kent Removals Team

Moving day can feel overwhelming, but with proper preparation and the right guidance, it can be a smooth and stress-free experience. As Rochester's trusted removal specialists, we've helped countless families and businesses across Medway and Kent relocate successfully. Our experienced team has witnessed firsthand how good preparation makes all the difference between a chaotic moving day and a seamless transition to your new home. Whether you're moving within Rochester, relocating from Chatham to Maidstone, or making a longer journey across Kent, these essential tips will help you prepare effectively for your moving day.

Start Planning Your Move Early

The key to a successful moving day begins weeks before the removal van arrives at your door. Early planning is crucial, especially if you're moving during peak times or to popular areas like Canterbury, Tunbridge Wells, or the coastal towns of Whitstable and Margate.

Create a Moving Timeline

Begin planning at least eight weeks before your moving date. This timeline should include booking your removal company, notifying utility providers, and arranging time off work. In our experience serving Rochester and surrounding Medway areas, customers who start planning early experience significantly less stress and enjoy better availability of services.

Your timeline should include key milestones such as finalizing removal quotes, confirming your moving date, arranging pet care if needed, and scheduling disconnection and reconnection of utilities. Don't forget to factor in school arrangements if you have children and are moving to different areas of Kent.

Book Your Removal Company

Professional removal companies in Kent often get booked up quickly, particularly during summer months and at the end of the academic year when many families prefer to move. Contact removal specialists early to secure your preferred date and ensure you have experienced professionals handling your belongings.

When booking, provide detailed information about your current property and destination. This helps the removal team prepare adequately, whether you're moving from a Victorian terrace in Rochester's historic area or relocating from a modern development in Gillingham to a rural property near Sevenoaks.

Declutter and Organize Before Packing

One of the most valuable pieces of moving day advice we share with Kent residents is to declutter thoroughly before packing begins. This process not only reduces moving costs but also ensures you start fresh in your new home without unnecessary items.

Sort Items by Category

Work through each room systematically, sorting items into categories: keep, donate, sell, and dispose. This methodical approach works well whether you're downsizing from a large family home in Strood or moving to a bigger property in Maidstone's growing residential areas.

Focus on items you haven't used in the past year. Books, clothing, kitchen gadgets, and decorative items often accumulate over time. Consider donating good-quality items to local Kent charities or selling valuable pieces through local Facebook groups or car boot sales popular throughout Medway.

Handle Special Items Early

Certain items require special attention and shouldn't be left until moving day. These include important documents, valuable jewelry, family heirlooms, and sentimental items. Create a separate box for essential documents like passports, birth certificates, and property deeds that you'll transport personally.

If you have valuable artwork, antiques, or delicate items, discuss these with your removal team beforehand. Many items can be safely moved with proper preparation and materials, but they need special consideration during the planning phase.

Master the Art of Packing

Effective packing is crucial for protecting your belongings during transit, whether you're making a short move within Rochester or relocating across Kent to areas like Ashford or Folkestone.

Gather Quality Packing Materials

Invest in sturdy boxes, quality packing tape, bubble wrap, and packing paper. Avoid using newspaper directly against items as the ink can transfer. Professional removal companies often provide packing materials, which ensures you have the right supplies for a safe move.

Different items require specific packing approaches. Heavy items like books should go in smaller boxes to make them manageable, while lighter items like bedding and cushions can fill larger boxes. This strategy helps removal teams work efficiently and safely.

Pack Room by Room

Packing systematically by room makes unpacking much easier in your new home. Start with rooms you use least, such as guest bedrooms or storage areas, and finish with essential daily-use areas like the kitchen and main bedroom.

Label each box clearly with its destination room and a brief description of contents. This labeling system helps removal teams place boxes in the correct rooms, saving you considerable time and effort when unpacking in your new Kent home.

Create an Essentials Box

Pack a clearly marked essentials box containing items you'll need immediately upon arrival at your new home. Include basic cleaning supplies, toilet paper, hand soap, phone chargers, basic tools, medications, and a change of clothes for each family member.

Add some non-perishable snacks and drinks, as unpacking works up an appetite and local shops might not be immediately accessible, especially if you're moving to more rural areas of Kent.

Prepare Your Properties

Both your current home and your new property need preparation to ensure moving day runs smoothly and safely for everyone involved.

Prepare Your Current Home

Clear pathways throughout your current home, ensuring removal teams can move freely without obstacles. This is particularly important in older Rochester properties where hallways and doorways might be narrower than modern homes.

Protect floors and walls along the removal route, especially in high-traffic areas. Professional removal teams take care to avoid damage, but additional protection helps preserve your property's condition for the final inspection or new occupants.

If you live in a terraced street common throughout Medway or in an area with parking restrictions, check whether you need to reserve parking spaces for the removal vehicle. Some Kent councils require permits for large vehicles, so investigate this early to avoid delays.

Prepare Your New Home

Visit your new property before moving day to plan furniture placement and identify any potential challenges. Measure doorways, staircases, and rooms to ensure your furniture will fit through and position appropriately.

If possible, complete any necessary cleaning, minor repairs, or decorating before your belongings arrive. This approach is much easier when rooms are empty and allows you to settle in more quickly.

Ensure utilities are connected and working at your new Kent property. Arrange for electricity, gas, water, and internet connections to be active from your moving day so you can function normally from the first evening.

Plan for Moving Day Logistics

The practical aspects of moving day require careful coordination to ensure everything runs according to plan.

Confirm Details with Your Removal Team

Contact your removal company a few days before moving to confirm arrival times, crew size, and any specific requirements. Weather can occasionally affect moving schedules, particularly during Kent's wetter months, so maintaining communication helps everyone stay informed.

Provide clear directions to both properties, including any access restrictions or parking challenges. If you're moving to a new development in areas like Kings Hill or Ebbsfleet, construction traffic or unfamiliar road layouts might affect timing.

Arrange Childcare and Pet Care

Moving day involves considerable activity, open doors, and potential safety hazards. Arranging childcare and pet care removes stress and ensures everyone's safety during the process.

If childcare isn't possible, designate a safe area where children can stay with their favorite toys and snacks while removal work continues. Similarly, pets should be secured in a quiet room or with a trusted friend until the main moving activity concludes.

Prepare Cash and Refreshments

While not obligatory, providing refreshments for your removal team is appreciated, especially during longer moves or hot weather. Tea, coffee, cold drinks, and light snacks help maintain energy levels and show appreciation for their hard work.

Keep some cash available for tips if you're pleased with the service, though this remains entirely at your discretion based on the quality of service provided.

Handle Last-Minute Tasks

Several important tasks must be completed in the final days and hours before your move across Kent.

Complete Final Preparations

Pack a suitcase with clothes and personal items for your first few days in the new home, similar to packing for a short holiday. This ensures you have everything needed while you gradually unpack and organize your new space.

Clean out your refrigerator and freezer, using up perishable food in the days leading to your move. Defrost the freezer if necessary, allowing time for cleaning and drying before the removal team arrives.

Do Final Checks

Walk through your current property methodically, checking every room, cupboard, and storage area. It's easy to overlook items in less obvious places like under stairs cupboards, garden sheds, or garage storage areas.

Check that all windows are closed and locked, and ensure you have all keys ready for the new occupants or letting agents. Take final meter readings for utilities and photographs of the property's condition if required.

Make Moving Day Run Smoothly

When moving day arrives, your preparation will pay off, but staying organized and communicative ensures everything proceeds smoothly.

Be Present and Available

Plan to be present throughout the moving process to answer questions, provide access to different areas, and make decisions about item placement. Your removal team may need clarification about fragile items or specific handling requirements.

Keep your phone charged and easily accessible for communication with utility companies, estate agents, or family members who might need updates on your progress.

Check Inventory and Condition

Most professional removal companies provide an inventory list of items being moved. Check this carefully and note any existing damage before items are loaded. This documentation protects both you and the removal company.

At your new Kent property, check items as they're unloaded and positioned. Report any concerns immediately while the removal team is still present to address issues promptly.

Settle Into Your New Kent Home

Once your belongings arrive at your new home, focus on essential unpacking first while gradually creating your comfortable new environment.

Prioritize Essential Rooms

Unpack bedrooms first to ensure comfortable sleeping arrangements for your first night. Then focus on the kitchen and bathroom to establish basic daily routines. Other rooms can be organized gradually over the following days and weeks.

Don't feel pressured to unpack everything immediately. Moving is exhausting, and taking time to plan room layouts and organization systems often produces better long-term results.

Register with Local Services

Register with local Kent services including GP practices, dentists, schools, and libraries. Update your address with banks, insurance companies, employers, and subscription services to ensure important correspondence reaches you.

Explore your new neighborhood when energy permits. Locate essential shops, restaurants, and services that will become part of your new routine in your Kent community.
