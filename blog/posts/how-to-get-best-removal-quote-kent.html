How to Get the Best Removal Quote in Kent: A Practical Guide

Published 12 Aug 2025  |  6 min read  |  Medway & Kent Removals

Getting the right removal quote in Kent is not just about finding the lowest price. A quote that is too low almost always means hidden charges on moving day, inadequate insurance or a crew that is unfamiliar with the specific requirements of your move. This guide explains how to get an accurate, competitive and trustworthy removal quote in Kent and Medway.

Always start with a pre-move survey

The single most important thing you can do to get an accurate removal quote is to arrange a pre-move survey before any price is provided. A survey — in person at your property or by video call — allows the removal company to assess the actual volume of your belongings, identify access challenges at both properties and provide a genuinely fixed price. Any company that provides a quote without a survey is guessing. That guess will either be deliberately low to win your booking or will be inflated to cover unknown risks.

What information to provide when requesting a quote

  • Your current full address including postcode
  • The new property full address including postcode
  • The approximate property sizes at both ends — number of bedrooms is a starting point
  • Your anticipated moving date or a date range if not confirmed
  • Any specialist items — pianos, safes, American-style appliances, antiques, gym equipment
  • Whether packing services are required
  • Any known access challenges — narrow streets, no lift, stairs only, restricted parking
  • Whether storage is needed between the two properties

Get at least three quotes

For any significant move in Kent, get at least three fixed-price quotes from different removal companies. Compare the total price, what is included, the insurance cover and each company's local reputation alongside the headline figure. The cheapest quote is not automatically the best choice — understand exactly what is included and what is not before deciding.

What a good quote should include

  • Full collection and delivery at both addresses
  • All loading and unloading by the crew
  • Goods-in-transit insurance coverage level clearly stated
  • Any parking costs or suspension fees at both addresses
  • Disassembly and reassembly of agreed items
  • A clear statement of what is NOT included

Red flags in a removal quote

  • A price provided without a survey of your property
  • No mention of insurance or a vague reference to being insured
  • A price that is significantly lower than all other quotes
  • No written confirmation of the quote
  • Pressure to confirm and pay a deposit immediately
Ask specifically what happens if you have more items than expected

Ask every removal company you quote with what happens if the actual volume on moving day is more than anticipated. A professional company with a fixed price will honour the quoted price for the items disclosed in the survey. A company without a proper survey may add significant charges on the day.
