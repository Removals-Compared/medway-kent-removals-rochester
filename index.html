Moving tips

How to prepare for your removal day

A well-prepared move is a smooth move. Here is our practical guide based on hundreds of removals across Medway and Kent.

📅

Book as early as possible

The most sought-after moving dates in Medway are end-of-month Fridays, particularly during the summer school holiday period between July and September. If your completion date or tenancy end date falls around these times, book your removal as soon as you have an agreed date. Leaving it too late could mean your preferred date is not available. We recommend booking at least 4 to 6 weeks in advance for peak periods.

📦

Start packing non-essentials early

Begin packing items you do not use regularly at least two weeks before your move. Books, ornaments, out-of-season clothing, spare bedding, DVDs and decorative items can all be boxed up well in advance. Label every box clearly with the contents and the room it belongs in at your new property. This makes unloading far faster and ensures nothing ends up in the wrong room. If you have booked our packing service, we will advise you on exactly what to prepare beforehand.

🕔

Arrange parking for the removal van

One of the most common causes of delay on moving day is inadequate parking for the removal vehicle. Contact your local council well in advance to arrange a parking suspension if needed, particularly in busy Medway streets where double yellow lines or permit zones are common. In Rochester city centre, Chatham High Street and parts of Gillingham, this is especially important. We can advise you on the van size we will bring so you can arrange the correct amount of space.

🏠

Prepare your new property in advance

If possible, visit your new property before moving day and plan where your main items of furniture will go. Take measurements of doorways, stairwells and rooms so you know in advance if any large items need to be dismantled to fit through. This avoids expensive delays on moving day. Share your floor plan or room preferences with our crew at the start of the day so they can place items in the right rooms first time without having to move them again.
