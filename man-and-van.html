What our man and van service covers

Man and van is our flexible small-move service: one or two trained movers with a Transit or Luton van, priced for jobs that do not need a full removal crew. Across Rochester, Medway and Kent, the jobs we handle most often are:

  • Small home moves: studio and one-bedroom flats, house shares and partly furnished moves where a full crew would be overkill.
  • Single item transport: sofas, beds, wardrobes, appliances, pianos quoted separately, and anything that will not fit in a car.
  • Marketplace and eBay collections: we collect from the seller, load carefully and deliver to your door, with payment on collection arranged where sellers require it.
  • Store and showroom deliveries: collections from furniture stores, auction houses and reclamation yards across Kent when the retailer cannot deliver soon enough.
  • Student moves: to and from universities across the South East at the start and end of every term, boxes and bedding included.
  • Small office and equipment jobs: a few desks to a new unit, stock between premises, or exhibition kit to a venue.
  • House clearance runs: furniture to family members, donations to charity shops or loads to the tip, done respectfully and efficiently.

Man and van, done the removal-company way

Man and van is a crowded market, and most of it runs on casual labour: a van, a phone number and whoever is free that day. It works until it does not: until a sofa is dragged up a stairwell unwrapped, a fridge travels on its side, or a no-show leaves you standing outside a flat with your deposit clock running. The horror stories are common enough that we built our man and van service specifically to be the opposite of them.

When you book with us, the person driving the van is a directly employed, background-checked mover from the same crew that handles our full house removals. The van carries blankets, straps, a trolley and tools as standard. Your goods are covered by the same goods-in-transit and public liability insurance as every other job we do. And the price is fixed in advance: not an hourly meter that rewards slow work, and not a doorstep renegotiation once your furniture is already on the van.

The difference shows in small things. A wardrobe gets blanket-wrapped even for a two-mile trip across Strood. A washing machine travels upright with its drum secured. A flat-pack bed gets dismantled with the fittings bagged and taped to the headboard, then rebuilt at the other end if you want. It is a small job, but it is done like a removal, because that is what we are.

What does a man and van cost in Medway and Kent?

Man and van jobs are priced by the size of the load, the distance and the access at both ends, then fixed. Local single-item jobs within Medway are the cheapest, typically well under £100. A one-bedroom flat move within the Medway towns usually lands between £150 and £300 depending on volume and stairs. Longer trips across Kent or into London are quoted by distance, and we will always tell you honestly when a job is big enough that our full house removal service would be better value than two van runs.

One honest warning about cheap quotes: if a man and van price seems too good, check three things: whether they are insured for your goods rather than just their vehicle, whether the price is fixed or hourly, and whether anyone will actually help carry. Our quotes include all three, in writing.

Marketplace, eBay and auction collections

A growing share of our man and van work is collecting furniture that our customers bought secondhand: Facebook Marketplace finds in Maidstone, eBay wins in Canterbury, auction lots in Rochester. The pattern is always the same: the item is a bargain, the seller wants it gone this week, and it will not fit in your car.

We handle the whole run. Send us the listing, the collection address and the seller's phone number, and we arrange a pickup time directly with them, inspect the item against the listing when we arrive, wrap it, and deliver it into the room you want it in. If a seller requires cash on collection we can arrange that with you in advance. For auction houses, we work to their collection deadlines, which are often tight, and we can consolidate several lots into one run.

Student and first-flat moves

Every September and June we run students to and from universities across Kent, London and the wider South East. A student move is usually one room's worth: boxes, a bike, maybe a desk chair and a television. We price these as simple fixed jobs, and parents appreciate that the person carrying their child's belongings up a halls staircase is an insured professional rather than a stranger from a listings app.

The same applies to first-flat moves: leaving home with a bedroom's worth of belongings plus a few donated pieces of family furniture is the classic man and van job, and a careful one sets the tone for every move that follows. We do it properly, and our five-star reviews include plenty of exactly these small jobs.

When man and van is the wrong choice

We would rather send you to the right service than sell you the wrong one. If you are moving a fully furnished two-bedroom home or larger, a man and van will need multiple trips, and by the second trip a proper house removal crew would have been faster and often cheaper. If your job involves a whole office, look at our office removals service. And if your dates do not line up and your belongings need somewhere to wait, our storage service plugs the gap. Tell us the whole picture when you call and we will point you at the option that actually serves you best.

Clearances, tip runs and doing disposal properly

A steady share of our small jobs are about things leaving your life rather than arriving: clearing a rented room between tenants, taking the old sofa away when the new one arrives, running a garage's worth of accumulated life to the household waste site. We do these respectfully and legally. Reusable furniture goes to charity partners around Chatham and Rochester where they will take it, and genuine waste goes to licensed disposal, not a country lane. If someone offers to take your waste for cash with no paperwork, remember that fly-tipped loads get traced back to the household they came from, and the fine lands on you. Our clearance jobs are priced like any other man and van work: fixed, in advance, with the disposal fees stated rather than discovered.

How to make a small move cheaper

Man and van pricing rewards preparation, so here is how to keep your quote at the bottom of its range. Be accurate about the load: a precise list, or thirty seconds of phone video, gets you a sharper price than a guess, because we quote worst-case when we have to guess. Box everything boxable before we arrive: loading fifteen sealed boxes takes minutes, loading fifteen open bags of belongings takes four times as long and risks damage. Dismantle what you can if you are handy, or tell us to bring tools if not. Sort parking at both ends, even if it just means moving your own car to reserve the space, because the closer the van, the faster the job. And be flexible on timing if you can: a midweek morning slot is easier to schedule than Saturday, and for single items we can sometimes pair your job with another run in the same direction, which keeps the price down for both customers.
