Our local knowledge of Dartford

The M25 Dartford Crossing is both an asset and a challenge for Dartford removal logistics. For moves that involve travelling across the Thames, the Crossing must be incorporated into route planning — the QE2 Bridge carries southbound traffic into Kent while the twin tunnels carry northbound traffic out. Both can experience significant congestion, particularly during the morning and afternoon peak hours and on busy summer weekends. We plan cross-Crossing moves to travel outside peak hours wherever possible.

Dartford town centre has the characteristic challenges of a busy town centre removal location — restricted parking zones, yellow lines, active delivery restrictions and significant footfall on the main shopping streets. For properties in Dartford town centre and the immediately surrounding streets, parking suspension planning with Dartford Borough Council is important for removal vehicles.

The Temple Hill and Wilmington residential areas on the southern and eastern sides of Dartford are predominantly post-war housing estate development with generally good road widths and vehicle access. The Stone area to the north has a mix of property types including some riverside and waterfront properties near the Thames. Joydens Wood and the areas to the east of Dartford towards Swanley are primarily inter-war and post-war semi-detached and detached housing with good access conditions.

Bluewater is nearby but its presence creates significant traffic on the A225 and surrounding roads, particularly at weekends and during school holiday periods. We factor Bluewater traffic into our route planning for Dartford and Swanley moves that occur at weekends or during peak retail periods.

Did you know? Your home contents insurance almost certainly does not cover your belongings during a removal. Every move we carry out is protected by comprehensive goods-in-transit insurance covering full replacement value. We carry proof of cover on every job and provide it on request before you commit to a booking.
