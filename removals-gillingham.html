Removal services in Gillingham: everything you need to know

Gillingham is a densely populated residential town forming one of the five towns of Medway, with a housing stock dominated by late Victorian and Edwardian terraces, substantial semi-detached properties and a growing number of newer private and social housing developments. The town stretches from the area immediately adjacent to Chatham in the west through to Rainham in the east, encompassing distinct neighbourhoods including Brompton, Twydall, Wigmore, Parkwood and Hempstead.

We carry out regular removals throughout Gillingham and have extensive first-hand knowledge of the access logistics across the town's varied street patterns. The older residential core around Gillingham High Street and the area immediately south of the railway station has some of the most challenging parking conditions in Medway, with narrow streets, terraced houses with no off-road parking and active permit parking zones that require advance planning to navigate correctly on moving day.

The outer areas of Gillingham (including Twydall, Wigmore and Hempstead), offer somewhat more straightforward vehicle access, with wider roads and more off-street parking. The newer estates on the eastern edges of the town are generally the most accessible for removal vehicles. Our team plans each Gillingham removal individually based on the specific address, ensuring we bring the correct vehicle size and allow sufficient time for access at both the loading and unloading addresses.

Our local knowledge of Gillingham

Gillingham's street layout reflects its development primarily in the late Victorian and Edwardian period, dense rows of terraced housing with narrow carriageways, front doors that open directly onto the pavement and very limited off-street parking. In the Gillingham High Street area and the streets immediately to the north and south of it, the removal logistics require particularly careful advance planning.

Our team knows which streets in Gillingham require a parking suspension to allow the removal vehicle to load safely, and we advise every customer in these areas to apply to Medway Council for the suspension at least 7 working days before their moving date. We specify the correct vehicle length based on what we are bringing, which avoids the frustration of arranging an inadequate suspension that leaves the van partially blocking the road.

Gillingham also has a significant number of properties in purpose-built flat blocks, particularly in the areas around Gillingham station and the High Street. These properties require the same stair-carry logistics as similar properties in Chatham, and our crew handles these moves regularly with the same trained, careful approach regardless of the number of floors involved.

The military history of Gillingham is reflected in some distinctive property types in the Brompton area, including the Royal Engineers barracks and surrounding married quarters, which have their own specific access arrangements for removal vehicles. If you are moving in or out of service accommodation in Gillingham, call us and we will advise on the correct procedure for arranging access.

Gillingham has some of the longest unbroken stretches of Victorian and Edwardian terraces in Medway, and the practical experience of moving these properties matters. We plan around the residents' parking permit schemes that apply across most of central Gillingham, including the streets off Canterbury Street, Marlborough Road and Skinner Street. For larger family homes in Hempstead, Wigmore and Rainham our team runs the right vehicle size for the street rather than the postcode, and we always confirm loft, garage and shed contents in advance so the crew and the lorry are correctly sized on the day.

Gillingham is a hub for cross-Medway moves, and we handle a steady volume of Gillingham-to-Chatham work, particularly between the older terraced areas; office removals in Chatham details are covered on our Chatham page.
