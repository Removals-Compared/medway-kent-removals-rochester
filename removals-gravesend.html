Removal services in Gravesend — everything you need to know

Gravesend is North Kent's largest town and one of the most active areas for residential and commercial removals in the whole of the county. The town sits on the Thames Estuary in the Gravesham borough and has been growing significantly in recent years, driven in part by the enormous Ebbsfleet Garden City development on its western edge — one of the largest new settlement projects in the South East of England — which is adding thousands of new homes to the area and generating a very high volume of removal demand.

We carry out regular removals throughout Gravesend and the full Gravesham borough, serving the established residential areas of the town including Northfleet, Singlewell, Riverview Park, Perry Street, Whitecliffe, Windmill Hill and the town centre, as well as the fast-expanding Ebbsfleet Valley development, the villages of Longfield, Meopham, Cobham, Bean, Higham and Shorne, and the neighbouring areas of Swanscombe and Dartford.

Gravesend has a distinctive and varied housing market. The town centre and inner residential areas contain a mix of period Victorian and Edwardian terraces, significant post-war social housing, and smaller private developments. The outer areas and suburbs have more recent private housing estates. The Ebbsfleet development represents a completely different product — brand new housing in a planned environment with excellent infrastructure but with the specific logistical characteristics of new build completions.

Our local knowledge of Gravesend

Gravesend town centre presents some of the most complex removal logistics in North Kent. The one-way system, the pedestrianised sections of the town centre, the busy junction at New Road and the significant volume of through traffic on the A226 all require careful routing for larger removal vehicles. We plan Gravesend town centre moves carefully, using our knowledge of the permitted vehicle access routes and the times when traffic is lightest.

Northfleet, to the west of Gravesend, has a mix of older terraced housing in its central areas and more recent residential development further out. The chalk cliffs and escarpment that characterise this part of North Kent create some distinctive steep-gradient roads that require specific vehicle and driver capability. Our team knows these roads and plans accordingly.

The Ebbsfleet Garden City development is a major and growing part of our Gravesend removal work. This planned new community is being developed over a number of years across the former Ebbsfleet and Swanscombe Peninsula sites. New build completions here have their own specific logistics — developer-managed access arrangements, snagging periods before completion, and the characteristic situation where multiple completions happen simultaneously in the same development, requiring careful coordination to avoid congestion around shared access points.

The Gravesham villages — Longfield, Meopham, Cobham, Bean, Higham, Shorne and Sole Street — present a completely different set of removal logistics. These are primarily rural villages with narrow lanes, limited turning space for larger vehicles and properties that are often accessed via farm tracks or private drives. Our team assesses each rural property individually and brings the correctly sized vehicle to ensure we can access the property without difficulty.

Did you know? Your home contents insurance almost certainly does not cover your belongings during a removal. Every move we carry out is protected by comprehensive goods-in-transit insurance covering full replacement value. We carry proof of cover on every job and provide it on request before you commit to a booking.
